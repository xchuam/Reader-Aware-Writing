# Caspase 5c potentiates Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models

## Abstract

CASP5 belongs to the inflammatory caspase family, but its biological role has been less well defined than that of CASP4 and mouse caspase 11. Although these related proteases mediate noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria, CASP5 did not act as the main effector in tested human intestinal epithelial cells or macrophages. Here, evidence from human intestinal organoids and cell-based reconstitution experiments supports a distinct function for a CARD-less CASP5 isoform, CASP5C, in the regulation of Wnt/beta-catenin signalling. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with CASP5 enriched in epithelial compartments and inducible by inflammatory or infectious stimuli. Proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation identified dishevelled proteins as CASP5-associated factors. Among CASP5 isoforms, CASP5C selectively increased Wnt reporter activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, increased Wnt target gene expression and enhanced proliferative features in human colonic organoids. Mechanistically, CASP5C catalytic activity cleaved APC, with D556 contributing to major cleavage products, thereby weakening destruction-complex assembly and reducing beta-catenin ubiquitination. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and restrained this activity. These findings define CASP5C as an isoform-specific amplifier of epithelial Wnt signalling, while leaving disease relevance and therapeutic potential as unresolved questions.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases are best known for their roles in innate immune sensing and pyroptotic cell death. CASP4, CASP5 and mouse caspase 11 form a closely related group of proteases that has been linked to noncanonical inflammasome activation after detection of cytosolic lipopolysaccharide or intracellular bacteria. Within this group, CASP4 more closely resembles mouse caspase 11 by sequence and phylogeny and acted as the principal mediator of the tested noncanonical inflammasome response in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages. CASP5 did not perform this canonical role in those experiments, raising the possibility that it has acquired a separate function.

The intestine provided a plausible setting for such a function. CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and was detected in epithelial cells as well as selected monocyte and macrophage populations. In human colonic organoids, CASP5 expression exceeded that of CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophages, whereas CASP4 had a broader distribution. Human intestinal epithelial models therefore offered a system in which to ask whether CASP5 contributes to epithelial biology independently of the noncanonical inflammasome pathway.

Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to intestinal epithelial development, repair, stem-cell renewal and lineage allocation. Dishevelled proteins connect receptor-proximal Wnt events to downstream regulation of beta-catenin, whereas APC functions as a scaffold and tumour suppressor within the beta-catenin destruction complex. Modulation of this pathway can alter the balance between proliferation and differentiation in intestinal epithelia. The present study addresses whether CASP5, and specifically its alternative isoforms, intersects with this pathway in human intestinal epithelial models.

## Methods

The study integrated expression profiling, human intestinal organoid assays and cell-based mechanistic experiments. CASP5 tissue and cell-type expression patterns were evaluated across intestinal and immune contexts, including single-cell and pseudotime analyses. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids were used to assess endogenous isoform expression and induction after Salmonella exposure, IFN-beta, IFN-gamma or lipopolysaccharide.

CASP5 isoforms were characterized as CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate protein sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD entirely. siRNA experiments distinguished CASP5C from a cleavage product of the longer isoforms. BioID2 proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation tested CASP5 associations with DVL proteins, AXIN1 and beta-catenin.

Functional effects on Wnt signalling were assessed with TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T and HCT116 cells and in organoids, with and without WNT3A. Downstream markers, beta-catenin localization, organoid growth, proliferation and differentiation were measured. Mechanistic experiments tested catalytic activity with CASP5C(C173A), CARD-deleted isoforms, APC cleavage assays and APC knockout cells reconstituted with wild-type APC or APC(D556A).

## Results

Human intestinal organoids expressed all three CASP5 isoforms. CASP5C was not explained by proteolytic processing of CASP5A or CASP5B, supporting its status as an independently expressed isoform. CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella exposure and after IFN-beta or IFN-gamma treatment, whereas lipopolysaccharide alone produced limited epithelial induction, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 expression.

BioID2 proximity labelling identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5 in human colonic organoids, and Wnt-related terms were enriched among candidate interactors. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed association between CASP5 isoforms and DVL proteins. DVL2-containing complexes also included AXIN1 and beta-catenin. Mapping experiments implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain in this association, although the available evidence does not by itself establish a direct binary interaction.

The functional effect on Wnt signalling was isoform specific. CASP5C increased TOPFlash reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both with and without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not reproduce this effect. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and shifted beta-catenin into cytosolic and nuclear fractions. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduced CASP5C-dependent signalling. CARD-less forms of mouse caspase 11 did not mimic CASP5C, suggesting, without proving, a possible primate CASP5 specialization.

In human intestinal organoids, CASP5C again increased Wnt reporter activity, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B did not. CASP5C expression increased viable cell number, produced organoids approximately 40% larger, increased Ki-67 and EdU positivity, and reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. In colonic organoids, CASP5C raised AXIN2 about threefold, TCF7 1.4-fold, MYC 1.3-fold, LGR5 1.2-fold and MKI67 2.4-fold, while reducing ALPI by about 57% and MUC2 by about 53%.

CASP5C also altered organoid behaviour under Wnt limitation and injury-associated stress. CASP5C-expressing organoids grew for longer after Wnt withdrawal and re-entered the cell cycle more readily when Wnt was restored. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC then increased faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These findings support a role in organoid recovery-associated proliferation, but do not prove repair in the human intestine in vivo.

The cellular localization of CASP5 isoforms was complex. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. However, CASP5c localized deeper in crypt regions and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than did CASP5a or CASP5b. CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated or CK20-positive states. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses supported enrichment of CASP5c in early-lineage or transit-amplifying states, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b tracked with mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, while CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiation markers.

Loss-of-function experiments reinforced the competition model. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects declined. Together, the gain- and loss-of-function data indicate that the long isoforms can restrain CASP5C, at least in part by competition for DVL2-containing complexes.

Mechanistically, CASP5C disrupted the beta-catenin destruction complex after Wnt stimulation. CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and lowered beta-catenin ubiquitination. These effects required catalytic activity: CASP5C(C173A) failed to activate Wnt reporter signalling or produce the beta-catenin effects seen with wild-type CASP5C. CASP5C showed greater enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removal of the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased activity and conferred greater Wnt reporter signalling.

APC emerged as a key substrate linking CASP5C proteolysis to Wnt pathway activation. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated in the APC N terminus. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC residue D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as an important cleavage site, although additional APC sites may also contribute. In APC knockout HEK293T cells, reconstitution with wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex assembly and increase Wnt reporter activity. APC(D556A) weakened this effect. APC fragments resembling CASP5C cleavage products reduced beta-catenin incorporation into the destruction complex and increased reporter activity.

Finally, disease datasets suggested possible relevance but did not establish causality. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while maintaining their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations were present in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were reported as mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In one microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer comparison, CASP5 mutations were more frequent than APC mutations, approximately 68% versus 17%. These observations do not demonstrate that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease, ulcerative colitis or colorectal cancer.

## Discussion

These findings assign CASP5 a function that differs from the established noncanonical inflammasome role of CASP4 and mouse caspase 11. Rather than acting primarily as the tested cytosolic lipopolysaccharide effector, CASP5C operated as an epithelial Wnt amplifier in human intestinal models. The specificity was striking: CASP5C enhanced reporter activity, beta-catenin redistribution, Wnt target expression and organoid proliferation, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B did not. The longer isoforms instead restrained CASP5C activity by competing for DVL2-containing complexes.

The proposed mechanism is consistent with the central architecture of the Wnt pathway. By associating with DVL proteins and cleaving APC, CASP5C reduced productive assembly of AXIN1-containing destruction complexes and decreased beta-catenin ubiquitination. APC D556 contributed to the major cleavage products and to the functional effect, but the data do not exclude additional CASP5C-sensitive APC sites. Catalytic activity was essential, and CARD removal from longer isoforms increased activity, suggesting that isoform architecture regulates access to this Wnt-amplifying protease function.

The organoid data connect this mechanism to epithelial state control. CASP5C expression favoured proliferative and Wnt-responsive features, while reducing differentiation-associated markers. Its induction after irradiation and its early peak during Wnt withdrawal-associated differentiation place CASP5C near transit-amplifying or early-lineage epithelial states rather than mature enterocyte programmes. The apparently mixed localization data are therefore not contradictory: total CASP5 transcripts can be abundant in differentiated cells while the CASP5c isoform is enriched in deeper crypt and proliferative contexts.

Several limitations temper the interpretation. Most evidence comes from organoids and reconstituted cell systems, so physiological repair functions in human tissue remain unproven. The CASP5-DVL interaction may be direct, but proximity and co-immunoprecipitation do not fully establish direct binding. Disease associations in ulcerative colitis and colorectal cancer datasets are hypothesis-generating only. They should not be interpreted as evidence that CASP5C is a validated disease driver or therapeutic target.

Overall, the study supports an isoform-resolved model in which CASP5C, a CARD-less CASP5 product enriched in intestinal epithelial contexts, amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin signalling through catalytic cleavage of APC and disruption of the destruction complex. CASP5A and CASP5B provide a counterweight by competing for DVL2 and limiting CASP5C activity. This mechanism expands the functional repertoire of inflammatory caspase-family proteins and provides a framework for future work on epithelial homeostasis, repair-associated proliferation and Wnt-linked disease states.

# Caspase 5c amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling through APC cleavage

## Abstract

Human CASP5 encodes an inflammatory caspase-family protease related to CASP4 and mouse caspase 11, but its function in intestinal epithelium has remained unclear. In tested human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages, CASP4, not CASP5, was the main noncanonical inflammasome effector for responses to cytosolic LPS and bacteria. CASP5 was instead enriched in intestinal and appendiceal tissues and in epithelial populations, suggesting a distinct epithelial role. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and the CARD-less CASP5C. Proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation connected CASP5 isoforms with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3, placing CASP5 near Wnt regulatory complexes. Among the isoforms, CASP5C selectively enhanced Wnt reporter activity, increased Wnt and proliferation markers, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, and supported larger, more proliferative organoids. Mechanistically, CASP5C required catalytic activity and cleaved APC, with D556 contributing to major cleavage products but not necessarily accounting for all APC processing. CASP5A and CASP5B did not activate Wnt in the same way and could restrain CASP5C, consistent with competition for DVL2-containing complexes. These findings support a model in which CASP5C amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling by weakening APC-dependent beta-catenin destruction complex function.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases are best known for innate immune effector functions, yet related proteases can acquire tissue-specific roles. CASP5 has often been discussed with CASP4 and mouse caspase 11 because of sequence and family relationships. However, CASP4 more closely resembled mouse caspase 11 in the tested noncanonical inflammasome response, whereas CASP5 did not perform that role in human intestinal epithelial cells or macrophages. This discrepancy raised a separate question: whether CASP5 has an epithelial function that is missed when the pathway is framed only around cytosolic LPS sensing.

The intestine provided a strong setting for this question. CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and appeared in epithelial cells as well as some monocyte and macrophage populations. In colonic organoids, CASP5 was higher than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD relative to macrophages, whereas CASP4 was more broadly expressed. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD entirely. siRNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B.

Because Wnt/beta-catenin signalling controls intestinal development, repair, stem-cell renewal, proliferation and differentiation, even a modest connection between CASP5 and Wnt complexes would have clear epithelial significance. DVL proteins transmit Wnt receptor events to downstream beta-catenin regulation, while APC acts as a scaffold and tumour suppressor within the beta-catenin destruction complex. The evidence summarized here supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, amplifies Wnt signalling through catalytic disruption of APC-dependent destruction complex function.

## CASP5C connects to DVL-dependent Wnt signalling

CASP5 responded to inflammatory and infectious cues in organoids, but not in a way that fit a simple epithelial LPS-response model. Salmonella infection, IFN-beta and IFN-gamma increased CASP5 expression, whereas LPS alone had little effect in epithelium, probably because epithelial TLR4 expression was low. These observations placed CASP5 at an interface between inflammatory stimulation and epithelial state but did not identify the pathway affected by CASP5.

BioID2 proximity labelling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms appeared in the interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation experiments supported this connection: CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3, and DVL2-containing complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin. Mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain in the interaction. These results do not prove a direct binary interaction, but they place CASP5 in the molecular neighbourhood of DVL-containing Wnt regulatory complexes.

Functional assays then separated CASP5C from the other isoforms. In TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity with or without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same effect. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 and shifted beta-catenin into nuclear and cytosolic fractions. CASP5A or CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduce the CASP5C effect, indicating that the CASP5 locus encodes isoforms with different consequences for the same pathway.

The CARD-less structure of CASP5C appeared relevant but was not sufficient to explain all activity. CASP5C had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing CARD regions from CASP5A/B increased activity and Wnt reporter signalling. However, CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce the Wnt effect. The most cautious interpretation is that CASP5C reflects a primate CASP5 specialization, rather than a generic consequence of deleting a CARD domain from any related caspase.

## CASP5C promotes proliferative organoid states

Organoid experiments showed that the Wnt reporter effect extended beyond transformed cell lines. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity in human intestinal organoids. CASP5C-expressing colonic organoids contained more viable cells, were about 40% larger, had more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. This phenotype matched the direction expected from increased Wnt-responsive proliferation and reduced differentiation under the tested culture conditions.

Marker changes reinforced this interpretation. In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 about threefold, TCF7 1.4-fold, MYC 1.3-fold, LGR5 1.2-fold and MKI67 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated markers moved in the opposite direction, with ALPI and MUC2 decreasing about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids also grew longer without Wnt and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored. After irradiation, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b, followed by faster increases in LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These findings support a repair-associated organoid program, but they do not prove repair activity in the human body.

Lineage analyses helped clarify why total CASP5 expression alone is not enough to infer function. In one single-cell analysis, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. Yet CASP5c was deeper in crypts and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a/b, whereas CASP5A/B were more luminal and associated with differentiated or CK20-positive cells. Pseudotime analysis, CIBERSORTx and Wnt-withdrawal differentiation experiments similarly linked CASP5c with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and CASP5a/b with mature or differentiating states.

Isoform knockdown experiments directly tested this balance. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP abundance, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. This pattern supports a competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C, at least in part by competing for DVL2-containing complexes.

## CASP5C activates Wnt through APC cleavage

CASP5C required catalytic activity to amplify Wnt signalling. The catalytic mutant CASP5C(C173A) lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects, arguing against a purely scaffolding mechanism. In AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP. It also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Together, these findings are consistent with weakened beta-catenin destruction complex function.

APC was the key substrate connecting CASP5C protease activity to this pathway. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were found mostly in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary site, although other APC cleavage sites may also contribute.

APC-reconstitution experiments linked cleavage to signalling output. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex components and increase Wnt reporter activity. APC(D556A) weakened this effect. APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. These results support a sequence in which CASP5C associates with DVL-containing complexes, cleaves APC, reduces APC-dependent destruction complex function and amplifies beta-catenin signalling.

## Discussion

The central conclusion is that CASP5C has an epithelial Wnt-amplifying activity distinct from the noncanonical inflammasome function associated with CASP4. This activity is isoform-specific, catalysis-dependent and mechanistically tied to APC cleavage. CASP5A and CASP5B should therefore not be grouped with CASP5C as equivalent Wnt activators; in several experiments they failed to activate the pathway and instead restrained CASP5C.

This isoform logic also helps reconcile apparently mixed expression patterns. Total CASP5 can be high in differentiated epithelial populations, yet CASP5c more closely aligns with crypt, MKI67, AXIN2 and transit-amplifying features. The relevant variable is therefore not simply whether CASP5 is expressed, but which isoform is present and whether CASP5C activity can act within DVL- and APC-containing Wnt complexes.

Disease-associated observations are compatible with this model but do not establish disease causation. CASP5 and CASP4 increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations occurred in about 3% of colon or rectal cancers in one analysis, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In one MSI-high colorectal cancer comparison, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC mutations, about 68% versus 17%. These associations warrant further study, but they do not show that CASP5C causes ulcerative colitis or colorectal cancer, nor do they validate CASP5C as a drug target.

Several uncertainties remain. CASP5-DVL proximity and co-immunoprecipitation support shared complexes, but direct binding is not fully proven. D556 is a major APC cleavage site, but it may not be the only relevant site. Organoid data support a role for CASP5C in Wnt-responsive proliferation and stress recovery in culture, but in vivo repair and disease biology require additional evidence. Within these limits, CASP5C provides a mechanistic bridge between inflammatory caspase-family biology and epithelial Wnt control: a CARD-less CASP5 isoform can cleave APC, weaken beta-catenin destruction complex function and promote a proliferative intestinal epithelial state.

### CASP5 isoforms mark distinct epithelial states

CASP5 isoform localization differed across epithelial states. In one single-cell RNA analysis, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. However, isoform-resolved analyses placed CASP5c deeper in crypt regions, with stronger overlap with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b. CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated, CK20-positive states.

Pseudotime analysis and CIBERSORTx deconvolution supported this division. CASP5c associated with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b associated with mature or differentiating states. During Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early alongside MKI67, while CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiation markers.

Isoform knockdown experiments tested this competition model. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while retaining CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP abundance, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects were reduced.

## Discussion

These findings assign a noncanonical epithelial signalling function to human CASP5. Rather than acting as the principal CASP4-like executor of cytosolic lipopolysaccharide responses in the tested systems, CASP5 is partitioned into isoforms with opposing effects on Wnt/beta-catenin regulation. CASP5C lacks a CARD, has relatively high catalytic activity, associates with DVL-containing complexes, cleaves APC and amplifies Wnt signalling. CASP5A and CASP5B can restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2.

The proposed mechanism connects inflammatory-caspase diversification to a core intestinal homeostasis pathway. In the working model, CASP5C is recruited near DVL and AXIN1-associated destruction-complex components. Its catalytic activity cleaves APC, with D556 serving as a primary but not exclusive site. APC cleavage weakens beta-catenin retention in the destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination and permits increased beta-catenin signalling.

The isoform balance may be as important as total CASP5 expression. CASP5c aligned with early-lineage and transit-amplifying features, damage-induced organoid responses and rapid induction after irradiation. CASP5a and CASP5b aligned more strongly with differentiated compartments and limited CASP5C activity.

Disease-associated observations should be interpreted cautiously. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while preserving their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations were reported in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison, about 68% versus 17%. These associations do not demonstrate that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they establish CASP5C as a validated therapeutic target.

Several limitations remain. Direct CASP5-DVL binding was suggested but not definitively proven. Most functional evidence came from cell lines and organoids, so the in vivo consequences of CASP5C induction during human epithelial injury remain to be determined. The apparent primate specialization of CASP5C also requires broader comparative testing.

Overall, the data support a model in which CASP5C is an epithelial Wnt amplifier rather than a redundant CASP4-like inflammasome protease. By cleaving APC and altering destruction-complex dynamics, CASP5C promotes beta-catenin signalling, organoid growth and proliferative recovery programs. The opposing behaviour of CASP5A and CASP5B suggests that CASP5 isoform composition may provide a local rheostat for intestinal epithelial renewal and differentiation.
