# A CARD-less CASP5 isoform amplifies epithelial Wnt signaling through APC cleavage

## Abstract

Human CASP5 is an inflammatory caspase-family protease related to CASP4, but its function has remained unclear. CASP4 is the closer human counterpart of mouse caspase 11 and the principal mediator of noncanonical inflammasome activation after cytosolic lipopolysaccharide detection. Here, CASP5 is shown to have a distinct intestinal epithelial role. Human organoids, cell lines, tissue analyses and public datasets identified three epithelial CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and the shorter CARD-less isoform CASP5C. CARD denotes the caspase activation and recruitment domain, present in CASP5A and partly retained in CASP5B but absent from CASP5C. CASP5C, not CASP5A or CASP5B, amplified beta-catenin-dependent Wnt signaling and promoted organoid growth. Mechanistically, CASP5C associated with dishevelled proteins, Wnt hubs that link receptor activation to beta-catenin regulation, and cleaved APC, a scaffold and tumor suppressor in the beta-catenin destruction complex, primarily at D556. This reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination and weakened destruction-complex assembly. CASP5C was enriched in transit-amplifying cells, proliferative progenitors between stem cells and differentiated enterocytes. CASP5A and CASP5B restrained CASP5C by competing for dishevelled binding. These findings define CASP5C as a human intestinal epithelial Wnt amplifier rather than a substitute for canonical Wnt regulation or CASP4-mediated inflammasome activity.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases are usually viewed as innate immune proteases that detect microbial danger and promote inflammatory cell death. Human CASP4 and CASP5 are both related to mouse caspase 11 and both can bind lipopolysaccharide through CARD regions. Yet sequence relatedness has not translated into equivalent function. CASP4 is closer to mouse caspase 11 and has retained the dominant noncanonical inflammasome role. CASP5 arose after primate-specific duplication, leaving open whether it simply duplicates CASP4 or has acquired a separate function.

The intestinal epithelium is a plausible setting for such specialization. This tissue renews continuously while maintaining a barrier at a host-microbial interface. Wnt/beta-catenin signaling controls intestinal development, regeneration and the balance between renewal and differentiation. Stem cells occupy a Wnt-rich crypt base, but transit-amplifying progeny continue dividing as they move away from that niche.

Beta-catenin is controlled by a destruction complex that includes AXIN1 and APC. APC is a scaffold in this complex and a tumor suppressor frequently altered in colorectal cancer. Without pathway activation, the destruction complex promotes beta-catenin phosphorylation and ubiquitination, limiting transcription. Dishevelled, or DVL, proteins link Wnt receptors to this complex. TOPFlash reports beta-catenin/TCF-dependent Wnt transcriptional activity.

The study asked whether CASP5 has a non-inflammasome function in human intestinal epithelial cells, which isoform mediates it and how it connects to Wnt signaling.

## Methods

The investigation combined computational, biochemical, cellular, organoid, tissue and dataset approaches. Sequence analyses compared human CASP4, human CASP5 and mouse caspase 11. Human Protein Atlas and single-cell datasets mapped CASP4 and CASP5 expression.

Human colonic and small intestinal organoids provided epithelial models, and monocyte-derived macrophages served as inflammatory comparators. Noncanonical inflammasome responses and bacterial clearance were tested after Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri exposure. HEK293T, HCT116, MODE-K, APC-knockout HEK293T cells and organoids were used for Wnt reporter, knockdown, rescue and biochemical assays.

BioID2 proximity labeling, mass spectrometry, co-immunoprecipitation, domain mapping and immunoblotting identified CASP5-associated proteins and Wnt complex composition. TOPFlash/FOPFlash reporters, beta-catenin localization, gene expression, beta-catenin ubiquitination and organoid proliferation assays measured pathway function. Catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A), CARD-deleted constructs, APC(D556A), APC fragments and recombinant CASP5C tested enzymatic mechanism. Irradiated colonic organoids modeled injury and recovery; human tissue and public disease datasets assessed localization and disease-associated patterns.

## Results

CASP4, not CASP5, mediated the tested noncanonical inflammasome responses. Although CASP4 and CASP5 share sequence similarity and CARD-dependent lipopolysaccharide binding, CASP4 was more similar to mouse caspase 11. In organoid-derived intestinal epithelial cells, noncanonical inflammasome activation and clearance of intracellular Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri depended on CASP4 rather than CASP5. In monocyte-derived macrophages, phagocytosis of virulent and avirulent live bacteria also activated noncanonical inflammasome responses and bacterial clearance through CASP4. These findings motivated the search for a separate CASP5 function.

Expression analyses pointed to the intestinal epithelium. CASP4 transcripts were broadly distributed, whereas CASP5 transcripts were concentrated mainly in the intestinal tract and appendix and, at single-cell level, largely in intestinal epithelial cells and monocytes/macrophages. CASP5 transcripts were more abundant in healthy colonic epithelial fractions and colonic organoids than in macrophages, while CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD showed the reverse pattern. Human colonic and small intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate sizes of 49.7, 43.2 and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CASP5A CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the entire CARD. siRNA experiments supported CASP5C as a distinct isoform rather than a cleavage product. In organoids, CASP5 increased after Salmonella Typhimurium or interferon stimulation, but not after lipopolysaccharide alone, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 expression.

CASP5 isoforms associated with Wnt signaling machinery. BioID2 proximity labeling in colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 among leading CASP5-associated proteins, and gene ontology enrichment highlighted Wnt-related processes. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed endogenous CASP5 interactions with DVL proteins. AXIN1 and beta-catenin were present in CASP5-DVL2 complexes. Domain mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although the interaction may be direct or indirect.

Functional assays distinguished CASP5C from the larger isoforms. In HEK293T and HCT116 cells lacking endogenous CASP5, CASP5C increased TOPFlash activity with or without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not. CASP5C increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and promoted nuclear beta-catenin accumulation. CASP5A or CASP5B co-expression reduced CASP5C-mediated Wnt activity and competed with CASP5C for DVL2 binding. Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 isoforms did not regulate Wnt in MODE-K or HEK293T cells, suggesting specialization after primate CASP5 duplication.

In primary colonic and small intestinal organoids, CASP5C again increased Wnt reporter activity, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B did not. CASP5C caused cytosolic and nuclear beta-catenin accumulation and increased Wnt-responsive, stem-associated and proliferation-associated markers. In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 about threefold, TCF7 about 1.4-fold, MYC about 1.3-fold, LGR5 about 1.2-fold and MKI67 about 2.4-fold. Differentiation markers ALPI and MUC2 fell by about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids had more viable cells, were about 40% larger, contained more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells and had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. CASP5C also enabled prolonged Wnt-independent growth and cell-cycle re-entry after Wnt restoration. After irradiation, CASP5c increased about 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b, and CASP5C-expressing organoids rapidly increased LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC.

CASP5C acted through catalytic remodeling of the destruction complex. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. CASP5C(C173A) lost the ability to promote Wnt reporter activity, beta-catenin localization and Wnt-responsive gene expression. CASP5C showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and deleting the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and Wnt reporter signaling, supporting the idea that the absent CARD permits CASP5C activity.

APC was the key substrate. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC were concentrated mainly in the N-terminal region. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, generated APC cleavage products in cells; CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 to alanine abolished the major CASP5C-mediated cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary cleavage site. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduced wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the destruction complex and enhance TOPFlash activity, whereas APC(D556A) reduced these effects. APC fragments mimicking cleavage products decreased beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity, while leaving open possible contributions from additional cleavage sites.

CASP5C localized to the epithelial compartment where Wnt amplification would be most useful. In healthy human colon single-cell data, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, increased in transit-amplifying cells and were most enriched in enterocytes; CASP4 was more uniform. In situ hybridization showed CASP5a and CASP5b more luminally, whereas CASP5c localized deeper in crypts and overlapped with MKI67-positive transit-amplifying zones and AXIN2. Immunohistochemistry, CIBERSORTx and pseudotime analyses supported CASP5C enrichment in proliferative early-lineage states and CASP5A/B association with mature or differentiating states.

Endogenous perturbation supported competition among isoforms. During organoid differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiation markers. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C enhanced Wnt reporter activity in colonic and small intestinal organoids; concurrent CASP5c knockdown returned reporter activity toward baseline. Preserving CASP5C while reducing CASP5A/B reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination by about 50%, increased organoid size by about 60%, increased EdU-positive and Ki-67-positive cells and increased ATP concentration. These growth effects were prevented by simultaneous CASP5c knockdown.

Disease-associated observations were suggestive but not definitive. CASP5 and CASP4 expression increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type distributions. Colon or rectal adenocarcinomas carried CASP5 mutations in about 3% of cases in the analyzed context. CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with other Wnt pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In a microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer comparison, CASP5 mutations were more common than APC mutations, about 68% versus 17%. These findings generate hypotheses but do not establish CASP5C as a cause of inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer, or as a validated therapeutic target.

## Discussion

The study separates CASP5 biology from CASP4-like inflammasome activity. CASP4 carried the tested noncanonical inflammasome responses in epithelial cells and macrophages, whereas CASP5C functioned as an epithelial Wnt amplifier. This role is not a replacement for canonical Wnt regulation. Rather, CASP5C appears to modulate beta-catenin signaling locally in transit-amplifying cells as they move away from the Wnt-rich stem-cell zone.

The mechanism links isoform structure to pathway output. CASP5C lacks the CARD region, displays higher enzymatic activity and depends on its catalytic cysteine for Wnt activation. By associating with DVL proteins and cleaving APC at D556, CASP5C weakens the beta-catenin destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination and promotes Wnt-responsive transcription. CASP5A and CASP5B share DVL binding but have lower activity and can compete with CASP5C, providing a plausible switch between proliferative and differentiating epithelial states.

Important limits remain. The mechanistic evidence relies heavily on organoids, cell lines, overexpression, knockdown and biochemical assays. Human tissue and public datasets support localization and disease associations but do not prove causal in vivo function. Mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce CASP5C Wnt activity, limiting direct mouse-to-human inference. Disease observations should therefore be treated as future directions rather than clinical conclusions.

## Conclusion

CASP5C defines a non-inflammasome function for human CASP5 in intestinal epithelial biology. It binds dishevelled proteins, cleaves APC at D556, disrupts the beta-catenin destruction complex and amplifies Wnt signaling in transit-amplifying cells. CASP5A and CASP5B do not share this activity and can restrain CASP5C by competing for dishevelled binding. Together, the findings place CASP5C as an epithelial modulator of Wnt-dependent renewal and repair, distinct from CASP4-mediated noncanonical inflammasome activation and cautiously relevant to intestinal disease biology.
