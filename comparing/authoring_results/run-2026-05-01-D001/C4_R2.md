# A CARD-less CASP5 isoform tunes epithelial Wnt signaling through APC cleavage

## Abstract

CASP5 is a human inflammatory caspase-family protease related to CASP4, yet its function has remained uncertain because CASP4 carries the main noncanonical inflammasome activity in the tested human systems. We asked whether CASP5 instead has a distinct role in human intestinal epithelial cells. Integrated computational, organoid, cell-line, biochemical, tissue, and public-dataset analyses identified a Wnt-modulatory function for CASP5C, a CASP5 isoform lacking the caspase activation and recruitment domain (CARD). CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased beta-catenin/TCF-dependent TOPFlash reporter activity, nuclear beta-catenin accumulation, Wnt-responsive transcription, organoid growth, and epithelial proliferation. Mechanistically, CASP5C associated with dishevelled proteins, cleaved APC at D556, reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination, and disrupted the beta-catenin destruction complex. CASP5A and CASP5B restrained CASP5C by competing for dishevelled binding.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases link microbial detection to innate immune responses and cell death. In humans, CASP4 and CASP5 are orthologues of mouse caspase 11, but they are not equivalent. CASP4 is closer to mouse caspase 11 and is the principal mediator of noncanonical inflammasome activation after cytosolic lipopolysaccharide detection in the systems examined. CASP5 shares high sequence similarity with CASP4 and its CARD-containing isoforms can bind lipopolysaccharide, yet its biological role has remained unclear. One reason is evolutionary: CASP5 arose after primate-specific gene duplication, whereas CASP4 retained the ancestral inflammasome function.

The intestinal epithelium is a logical setting in which to reassess CASP5. CASP5 expression is concentrated in the intestinal tract, and epithelial renewal depends on Wnt/beta-catenin signaling. Transit-amplifying cells, the proliferative progeny between LGR5-positive stem cells and differentiated enterocytes, must maintain proliferation as they move away from the Wnt-rich crypt base. A local Wnt-amplifying mechanism in this compartment could support homeostasis without replacing canonical Wnt regulation.

Canonical Wnt signaling is controlled by beta-catenin stability. In low-Wnt conditions, the beta-catenin destruction complex, including AXIN1 and APC, promotes beta-catenin phosphorylation, ubiquitination, and degradation. APC is a scaffold protein in this complex and a colorectal cancer tumor suppressor. Dishevelled proteins, or DVL proteins, are Wnt signaling hubs that connect receptor activation to downstream beta-catenin regulation. When beta-catenin escapes destruction, it accumulates in the nucleus and drives TCF-dependent transcription. TOPFlash measures this beta-catenin/TCF-dependent activity.

We tested whether CASP5 has a non-inflammasome epithelial function, which CASP5 isoform mediates it, and how that isoform connects to Wnt signaling.

## Methods

The study combined computational, cellular, organoid, biochemical, tissue, and public-dataset analyses. Sequence and phylogenetic comparisons evaluated human CASP4, human CASP5, and mouse caspase 11. Human Protein Atlas and single-cell datasets were used to compare CASP4 and CASP5 tissue and cell-type expression.

Human colonic and small intestinal organoids modeled intestinal epithelial CASP5 expression and function. Human monocyte-derived macrophages served as inflammatory-caspase comparators. Noncanonical inflammasome activity and bacterial clearance were tested after intracellular Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri infection in epithelial cells and after phagocytosis of virulent and avirulent live bacteria in macrophages.

HEK293T, HCT116, MODE-K, APC-knockout HEK293T cells, and organoids were used for reporter, overexpression, knockdown, cleavage, and rescue assays. TOPFlash and FOPFlash reporters assessed Wnt transcriptional activity. Beta-catenin localization, Wnt-responsive gene expression, beta-catenin ubiquitination, organoid growth, EdU incorporation, Ki-67 staining, and differentiated epithelial markers measured pathway output and epithelial state.

BioID2 proximity labeling, mass spectrometry, co-immunoprecipitation, domain mapping, and immunoblotting tested CASP5-associated proteins and destruction-complex composition. Catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A), CARD-deleted constructs, APC(D556A), APC fragments, and recombinant CASP5C tested enzymatic mechanism. Irradiated colonic organoids modeled epithelial injury. Human tissue and public datasets provided localization and disease-associated context.

## Results

CASP4, not CASP5, accounted for the tested noncanonical inflammasome responses. In human intestinal epithelial cells derived from colonic organoids, noncanonical inflammasome activation and clearance of intracellular Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri depended on CASP4. Human monocyte-derived macrophages showed the same pattern after uptake of virulent and avirulent live bacteria. These data separated CASP5 from the CASP4-dominated antibacterial inflammasome response and motivated a search for another function.

Expression analyses pointed to intestinal epithelial biology. CASP4 transcripts were broadly distributed, whereas CASP5 transcripts were concentrated mainly in the intestinal tract and appendix. CASP5 was more abundant in healthy colonic epithelial fractions and colonic organoids than in monocyte-derived macrophages, while CASP4, CASP1, and PYCARD showed the reverse pattern. Human colonic and small intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B, and CASP5C. CASP5A is about 49.7 kDa, CASP5B about 43.2 kDa, and CASP5C about 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD, and CASP5C lacks the entire CARD. siRNA experiments supported CASP5C as a distinct isoform. In organoids, CASP5 increased after Salmonella Typhimurium, IFN-beta, or IFN-gamma stimulation, whereas lipopolysaccharide alone did not, consistent with low TLR4 expression.

CASP5 proximity labeling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2, and DVL3 among the leading associated proteins, and gene ontology enrichment highlighted Wnt-related processes. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that endogenous CASP5 isoforms interacted with DVL proteins. AXIN1 and beta-catenin were also present in CASP5-DVL2 complexes. Domain mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although the interaction may be direct or indirect.

CASP5C was the Wnt-activating isoform. In HEK293T and HCT116 cells lacking endogenous CASP5, CASP5C increased TOPFlash activity with or without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1, and CASP4 did not. CASP5C increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and promoted nuclear beta-catenin accumulation. CASP5A or CASP5B reduced CASP5C-mediated Wnt activity and competed with CASP5C for DVL2 interaction. Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 isoforms did not regulate Wnt in MODE-K or HEK293T cells.

Primary organoids confirmed the epithelial effect. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity in colonic and small intestinal organoids. CASP5C caused cytosolic and nuclear beta-catenin accumulation, increased Wnt-responsive and proliferation-associated markers, and reduced differentiated epithelial markers. In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 about 3-fold, TCF7 about 1.4-fold, MYC about 1.3-fold, LGR5 about 1.2-fold, and MKI67 about 2.4-fold, while reducing ALPI and MUC2 by about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids had more viable cells, were about 40% larger, contained more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes.

An injury model further linked CASP5C to repair-associated Wnt programs. After irradiation of colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased about 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with about 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. CASP5C-expressing organoids then showed rapid increases in LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7, and MYC.

CASP5C acted through its catalytic activity and the destruction complex. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC, and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. CASP5C(C173A) lost the ability to activate TOPFlash, alter beta-catenin localization, or induce Wnt-responsive genes. CASP5C showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and CARD deletion from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and Wnt reporter signaling.

APC connected CASP5C catalysis to beta-catenin stabilization. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC clustered mainly in the N-terminal region. In cells, CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, or CASP5C(C173A), generated APC cleavage products. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 to alanine abolished the major CASP5C-mediated cleavage products, establishing D556 as a primary cleavage site. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduced wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the destruction complex and enhance Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) reduced this effect. APC fragments mimicking cleavage products decreased beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased Wnt reporter activity. Other APC cleavage sites may also contribute.

Human tissue and dataset analyses placed CASP5C in the transit-amplifying compartment. In healthy human colon single-cell data, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, increased in transit-amplifying cells, and were most enriched in enterocytes, while CASP4 was more uniform. Isoform-resolved localization showed CASP5a and CASP5b more luminally, whereas CASP5c localized deeper in crypts and overlapped with MKI67-positive and AXIN2-positive zones. Immunohistochemistry and pseudotime analyses similarly linked CASP5C to proliferative crypt or early-lineage states and CASP5A/CASP5B to mature states.

Endogenous isoform perturbation supported competition among CASP5 isoforms. During colonic organoid differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with mature epithelial markers. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C enhanced Wnt reporter activity in colonic and small intestinal organoids; concurrent CASP5c knockdown returned reporter activity toward baseline. Preserving CASP5C while reducing CASP5A and CASP5B reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination by about 50%, increased organoid size by about 60%, increased EdU-positive and Ki-67-positive cells, and increased ATP concentration. Simultaneous CASP5c knockdown prevented these growth effects.

Disease-associated analyses were suggestive but not causal. CASP5 and CASP4 expression increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining cell-type distributions. Colon or rectal adenocarcinomas carried CASP5 mutations in about 3% of cases in the analyzed context, comparable to AXIN1. CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with other Wnt pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were more common than APC mutations in the cited dataset comparison. These findings define future questions rather than a clinical mechanism or therapeutic target.

## Discussion

This work assigns CASP5 a function distinct from CASP4-mediated noncanonical inflammasome activation. The central finding is not that CASP5 substitutes for CASP4, nor that all CASP5 isoforms promote Wnt signaling. Rather, CASP5C acts in intestinal epithelial cells as a catalytic Wnt amplifier. CASP5C lacks the CARD, shows higher enzymatic activity than CARD-containing CASP5 isoforms, depends on its catalytic cysteine for Wnt activation, and links to the pathway by associating with DVL proteins and cleaving APC.

The mechanism fits the architecture of canonical Wnt signaling. DVL proteins connect receptor-proximal Wnt activation to destruction-complex regulation. APC helps maintain beta-catenin phosphorylation and ubiquitination when Wnt signaling is low. CASP5C binding to DVL proteins positions the protease near this regulatory machinery, and APC cleavage at D556 weakens beta-catenin degradation. CASP5C therefore modulates Wnt output rather than replacing canonical regulation.

The cell-type distribution provides biological context. CASP5C enrichment in transit-amplifying cells suggests a local means of sustaining Wnt responsiveness as epithelial progenitors move through the crypt. The later rise of CASP5A and CASP5B during differentiation, together with their ability to compete for DVL2 binding, suggests an isoform switch that dampens CASP5C activity as cells mature.

The evidence is mechanistically strong but has limits. It relies heavily on organoids, cell lines, overexpression, knockdown, and biochemical assays. Human tissue and public datasets support localization and association, but do not prove in vivo causality. Mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce CASP5C Wnt activity, limiting direct mouse-to-human inference. The APC D556 site is strongly supported as a primary cleavage site, but additional sites may contribute. Ulcerative colitis and colorectal cancer observations remain associative and hypothesis-generating.

## Conclusion

CASP5C is a CARD-less human CASP5 isoform that amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin signaling in intestinal epithelial transit-amplifying cells. By binding dishevelled proteins and cleaving APC at D556, CASP5C disrupts the beta-catenin destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination, and promotes organoid proliferation. CASP5A and CASP5B counter this activity by competing for dishevelled binding. These findings expand inflammatory caspase biology beyond innate immune activation and identify an isoform-specific mechanism for tuning intestinal epithelial renewal and repair.
