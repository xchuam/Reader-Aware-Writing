# A CARD-less CASP5 isoform amplifies epithelial Wnt signaling through APC cleavage

## Abstract

CASP5 is a human inflammatory caspase-family protease related to CASP4, but its function has remained unclear because CASP4 carries the dominant tested role in noncanonical inflammasome activation. We investigated whether CASP5 has a distinct function in human intestinal epithelium, where Wnt/beta-catenin signaling controls renewal, progenitor expansion, and repair. Integrated analyses of expression datasets, human intestinal organoids, macrophages, epithelial cell lines, proximity labeling, biochemical cleavage assays, and human tissue showed that CASP5 is not required for the tested bacterial inflammasome responses. Instead, a CARD-less isoform, CASP5C, amplifies Wnt signaling. CASP5C binds dishevelled proteins, cleaves APC at D556, weakens the beta-catenin destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination, and promotes organoid proliferation. CASP5A and CASP5B also bind dishevelled but restrain CASP5C through competition. CASP5C is enriched in transit-amplifying cells, identifying an epithelial homeostatic function for CASP5 that is distinct from CASP4-mediated innate immune activation.

## Introduction

Human CASP4 and CASP5 are orthologues of mouse caspase 11, a mediator of noncanonical inflammasome activation after cytosolic lipopolysaccharide detection. CASP4 is more similar to mouse caspase 11 and has retained this ancestral role. CASP5 arose after primate-specific duplication, shares high sequence similarity with CASP4, and contains longer isoforms with a caspase activation and recruitment domain (CARD), yet its biological role has remained ambiguous.

The intestine provides a relevant setting for resolving this ambiguity. Wnt/beta-catenin signaling governs epithelial development, regeneration, and the balance between stem-cell renewal and differentiation. Transit-amplifying cells, proliferative progenitors between crypt-base stem cells and differentiated enterocytes, remain dependent on Wnt signaling as they move away from the Wnt-rich niche. A local Wnt-amplifying mechanism in these cells could therefore support homeostasis and repair.

Mechanistically, Wnt signaling is organized around dishevelled proteins and the beta-catenin destruction complex. Dishevelled, or DVL, is a signaling hub linking Wnt receptor activation to downstream beta-catenin regulation. The destruction complex, which includes AXIN1 and APC, promotes beta-catenin phosphorylation and ubiquitination. APC is a scaffold in this complex and a tumor suppressor frequently altered in colorectal cancer. When destruction complex activity is reduced, beta-catenin accumulates, enters the nucleus, and drives TCF-dependent transcription, measurable with the TOPFlash reporter. This study asked whether CASP5 regulates this pathway in human intestinal epithelial cells, which isoform is responsible, and how the effect is produced.

## Methods

The study combined computational, cellular, organoid, biochemical, tissue, and public-dataset analyses. Sequence and phylogenetic comparisons evaluated human CASP4, human CASP5, and mouse caspase 11. Human Protein Atlas and single-cell datasets were used to compare CASP4 and CASP5 expression across tissues and epithelial cell states. Human colonic and small intestinal organoids modeled epithelial CASP5 expression, while monocyte-derived macrophages served as an inflammatory caspase comparator.

Inflammasome assays tested requirements for CASP4 and CASP5 during intracellular Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri responses in organoid-derived epithelial cells and during phagocytosis of live bacteria in macrophages. Wnt pathway function was examined in HEK293T, HCT116, MODE-K, APC-knockout HEK293T cells, and primary human organoids using TOPFlash/FOPFlash reporters, beta-catenin localization, Wnt-responsive gene expression, beta-catenin ubiquitination, and organoid growth assays. BioID2 proximity labeling, mass spectrometry, co-immunoprecipitation, domain mapping, and immunoblotting identified CASP5-associated complexes. Catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A), CARD-deleted CASP5 constructs, APC(D556A), APC fragments, and recombinant CASP5C tested the enzymatic mechanism. Irradiated colonic organoids modeled epithelial injury and recovery.

## Results

CASP4, not CASP5, mediated the tested noncanonical inflammasome responses. Although CASP4 and CASP5 share sequence similarity and longer CASP5 isoforms contain CARD regions capable of lipopolysaccharide binding, CASP4 was closer to mouse caspase 11. In human colonic organoid-derived epithelial cells, activation of noncanonical inflammasome responses and clearance of intracellular Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri depended on CASP4 rather than CASP5. Monocyte-derived macrophages showed the same division during bacterial phagocytosis and clearance. These findings motivated a search for a non-inflammasome CASP5 function.

Expression analyses identified the intestinal epithelium as a major CASP5 context. CASP4 transcripts were broadly distributed, whereas CASP5 transcripts were concentrated mainly in the intestinal tract and appendix and, at single-cell resolution, largely in intestinal epithelial cells and monocytes/macrophages. Healthy colonic epithelial fractions and colonic organoids expressed more CASP5 than monocyte-derived macrophages, while CASP4, CASP1, and PYCARD showed the opposite pattern. Human colonic and small intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B, and CASP5C. CASP5A is about 49.7 kDa, CASP5B about 43.2 kDa, and CASP5C about 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CASP5A CARD region, whereas CASP5C lacks the entire CARD. siRNA experiments supported CASP5C as a distinct isoform rather than a cleavage product. CASP5 increased after Salmonella Typhimurium, IFN-beta, or IFN-gamma stimulation in organoids, but lipopolysaccharide alone did not induce epithelial CASP5, consistent with low TLR4 expression.

Proximity labeling linked CASP5 to Wnt signaling. BioID2 mass spectrometry in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2, and DVL3 among leading CASP5-associated proteins, and Wnt-related processes were enriched among CASP5 interactors. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed endogenous CASP5 association with DVL proteins and detected AXIN1 and beta-catenin in CASP5-DVL2 complexes. Domain mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, while leaving unresolved whether binding was direct or mediated by additional proteins.

Only CASP5C promoted Wnt signaling. In HEK293T and HCT116 cells lacking endogenous CASP5, CASP5C increased TOPFlash activity with or without WNT3A, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1, and CASP4 did not. CASP5C increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and promoted nuclear beta-catenin accumulation. Co-expression of CASP5A or CASP5B reduced CASP5C-mediated Wnt activity and competed with CASP5C for DVL2 interaction. Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 isoforms did not regulate Wnt in MODE-K or HEK293T cells, suggesting that this activity emerged after primate CASP5 duplication.

In human colonic and small intestinal organoids, CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity, cytosolic and nuclear beta-catenin, and Wnt-responsive and proliferation-associated markers. In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 about 3-fold, TCF7 about 1.4-fold, MYC about 1.3-fold, LGR5 about 1.2-fold, and MKI67 about 2.4-fold, while reducing ALPI and MUC2 by about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids had more viable cells, were about 40% larger, contained more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. CASP5C also enabled prolonged Wnt-independent growth and cell-cycle re-entry after Wnt restoration. After irradiation, CASP5c transcript increased about 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with about 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b, and CASP5C-expressing organoids showed rapid increases in LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7, and MYC.

CASP5C disrupted the beta-catenin destruction complex through catalytic activity. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC, and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. The catalytic mutant CASP5C(C173A) lost Wnt reporter, beta-catenin localization, and gene-expression effects. CASP5C showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and CARD deletion from CASP5A or CASP5B increased activity and Wnt signaling, supporting the view that CARD absence enables CASP5C activity.

APC was the key substrate connecting CASP5C to Wnt amplification. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC were concentrated in the N-terminal region. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, or CASP5C(C173A), generated APC cleavage products in cells, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. APC(D556A) abolished the major cleavage products, establishing D556 as a primary site. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the destruction complex and enhance Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) reduced this effect. APC fragments mimicking cleavage products decreased beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity, although additional cleavage sites may also contribute.

CASP5C localized to the epithelial compartment in which Wnt amplification would be expected to matter. In healthy human colon single-cell data, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent in LGR5-positive stem cells, increased in transit-amplifying cells, and were most enriched in enterocytes, while CASP4 was more uniform. In situ hybridization showed CASP5a and CASP5b more luminally, whereas CASP5c localized deeper in crypts and overlapped with MKI67-positive transit-amplifying zones and AXIN2. Immunohistochemistry, CIBERSORTx, and pseudotime analyses similarly associated CASP5C with proliferative early-lineage states and CASP5A/CASP5B with mature or differentiating epithelium.

Endogenous perturbation supported a competitive isoform model. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with mature markers. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C enhanced Wnt reporter activity in colonic and small intestinal organoids, reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination by about 50% in colonic organoids, increased organoid size by about 60%, and increased EdU-positive and Ki-67-positive cells and ATP concentration. Simultaneous CASP5c knockdown prevented these effects, indicating that CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL binding.

## Discussion

These findings separate CASP5 biology from a simple CASP4-like inflammasome model. CASP4 mediated the tested bacterial noncanonical inflammasome responses, whereas CASP5 was enriched in intestinal epithelial cells and showed isoform-specific control of Wnt signaling. The data identify CASP5C as a local amplifier of beta-catenin signaling in transit-amplifying cells rather than as the main driver of intestinal Wnt activity.

The proposed mechanism is direct and internally consistent. CASP5C lacks the inhibitory CARD region, has higher enzymatic activity, associates with DVL-containing Wnt complexes, and cleaves APC at D556. Because APC scaffolds the beta-catenin destruction complex, this cleavage reduces complex function, lowers beta-catenin ubiquitination, and increases Wnt transcriptional output. CASP5A and CASP5B bind DVL but do not reproduce this effect; instead, they compete with CASP5C and become more prominent in differentiated epithelial states. This arrangement provides a plausible way to sustain Wnt responsiveness in transit-amplifying cells while allowing restraint during differentiation.

The study has important limits. Much of the causal evidence comes from cell lines, organoids, overexpression, knockdown, and biochemical assays. Human tissue and public datasets support localization and disease-associated patterns but do not prove in vivo causal function in the human intestine. Mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce CASP5C Wnt activity in the tested systems, limiting mouse-to-human inference. Ulcerative colitis and colorectal cancer observations, including CASP5 expression changes and mutation patterns, are hypothesis-generating only. They do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer, and they do not validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.

## Conclusion

CASP5C is a CARD-less human CASP5 isoform that amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin signaling in intestinal epithelial transit-amplifying cells. By binding DVL proteins and cleaving APC at D556, CASP5C disrupts the beta-catenin destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination, and supports organoid proliferation and repair-associated transcriptional programs. CASP5A and CASP5B counterbalance this activity through competitive DVL binding. CASP5 diversification therefore produced an epithelial homeostatic function distinct from CASP4-mediated noncanonical inflammasome activation.
