# A CARD-less CASP5 isoform amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling

## Abstract

Human CASP5 is an inflammatory caspase-family protease with a less defined
function than the related noncanonical inflammasome protease CASP4.
Here we identify a non-inflammasome role for CASP5 in human intestinal
epithelial cells.
Across organoids, cell lines, biochemical assays, tissue analyses and public
datasets, CASP5C emerged as a Wnt pathway amplifier in proliferative epithelial
progenitors.
CASP5C is a distinct isoform that lacks the caspase activation and recruitment
domain, or CARD, found in CASP5A and CASP5B.
CASP5C bound dishevelled proteins and cleaved APC at D556, reducing assembly of
the beta-catenin destruction complex.
This decreased beta-catenin ubiquitination and increased beta-catenin-dependent
transcription.
In human intestinal organoids, CASP5C promoted growth, proliferation and
recovery after irradiation.
CASP5A and CASP5B also bound dishevelled but restrained CASP5C activity by
competition.
These findings identify CASP5C as an epithelial Wnt modulator.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases are best known as innate immune proteases that link
microbial sensing to cell death and inflammatory mediator release.
In humans, CASP4 and CASP5 are related to mouse caspase 11, a mediator of
noncanonical inflammasome activation after cytosolic lipopolysaccharide
detection.
CASP4 is closer to mouse caspase 11 and retained this ancestral role.
CASP5 arose after primate-specific gene duplication and shares high sequence
similarity with CASP4, yet its main biological function has been unclear.

The intestinal epithelium provides a relevant setting for resolving this
question.
It is exposed to microbes and inflammatory signals, but it also depends on
Wnt/beta-catenin signalling for renewal.
When Wnt activity is low, the beta-catenin destruction complex, including AXIN1
and APC, promotes beta-catenin phosphorylation, ubiquitination and loss.
When Wnt receptors are activated, dishevelled proteins, or DVL proteins, relay
signals to the destruction complex and permit beta-catenin accumulation.
Beta-catenin then enters the nucleus and activates TCF-dependent transcription.

Transit-amplifying cells are proliferative intestinal epithelial progenitors
between stem cells and differentiated enterocytes.
We asked whether CASP5 contributes to this epithelial programme independently of
CASP4-mediated noncanonical inflammasome activation, and if so which CASP5
isoform is responsible.

## Methods

We combined sequence, expression, biochemical, cellular, organoid, tissue and
public-dataset analyses.
Sequence and phylogenetic comparisons assessed human CASP4, human CASP5 and
mouse caspase 11.
Expression datasets compared CASP4 and CASP5 across tissues and cell types.

Human colonic and small intestinal organoids were used to examine epithelial
CASP5 expression and function.
Human monocyte-derived macrophages provided an inflammatory comparator for
bacterial inflammasome responses.

Mechanistic experiments used HEK293T, HCT116, MODE-K, APC-knockout HEK293T
cells and primary human organoids.
TOPFlash and FOPFlash reporter assays measured beta-catenin/TCF-dependent Wnt
transcriptional activity.
Beta-catenin localisation, gene expression, beta-catenin ubiquitination and
organoid growth assays assessed pathway output.
BioID2 proximity labelling, mass spectrometry, co-immunoprecipitation and domain
mapping identified CASP5-associated proteins.

Isoform and enzymatic requirements were tested with CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP5C,
CARD-deleted CASP5 constructs and catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A).
CARD denotes a caspase activation and recruitment domain, an N-terminal domain
present in CASP5A and CASP5B but absent from CASP5C.
APC cleavage was evaluated with candidate-site prediction, APC fragments,
wild-type APC, APC(D556A) and recombinant CASP5C.
Irradiated human colonic organoids modelled epithelial injury and recovery.
Human tissue and disease datasets placed the mechanism in cellular contexts.

## Results

CASP4, rather than CASP5, mediated the tested noncanonical inflammasome
responses.
In human intestinal epithelial cells derived from colonic organoids,
noncanonical inflammasome activation and clearance of intracellular Salmonella
Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri depended on CASP4, not CASP5.
Macrophage assays with live bacteria gave the same conclusion.
These results separated CASP5 from the main CASP4 inflammasome function.

Expression analyses pointed to an epithelial role for CASP5.
CASP4 transcripts were broadly distributed across tissues and cell types.
CASP5 transcripts were concentrated mainly in the intestinal tract and appendix,
and at single-cell resolution were largely restricted to intestinal epithelial
cells and monocytes or macrophages.
Healthy colonic epithelial fractions and colonic organoids contained more CASP5
transcript than monocyte-derived macrophages, whereas CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD
showed the opposite pattern.
Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A of about 49.7 kDa, CASP5B of about
43.2 kDa and CASP5C of about 33.3 kDa.
CASP5B lacks part of the CASP5A CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the whole CARD.
siRNA experiments supported CASP5C as a distinct isoform.
Salmonella Typhimurium, IFN-beta and IFN-gamma increased CASP5 in organoids,
whereas lipopolysaccharide alone did not.

Proximity labelling identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 among the leading
CASP5-associated proteins in human colonic organoids.
Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed endogenous CASP5-DVL interactions.
AXIN1 and beta-catenin were present in CASP5-DVL2 complexes.
Domain mapping placed the interaction at the DVL DEP domain and CASP5 catalytic
domain, although direct binding was not proven.

CASP5C, but not the other tested inflammatory caspases, amplified Wnt output.
In HEK293T and HCT116 cells lacking endogenous CASP5, CASP5C increased TOPFlash
reporter activity with or without WNT3A.
TOPFlash reports beta-catenin/TCF-dependent Wnt transcription.
CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce comparable activation.
CASP5C increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and promoted nuclear beta-catenin.
Co-expression of CASP5A or CASP5B reduced CASP5C-mediated Wnt activity and
competed with CASP5C for DVL2 interaction.
Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 isoforms did not regulate Wnt, suggesting
that CASP5C specialization arose after primate CASP5 duplication.

Primary organoids reproduced these isoform-specific effects.
In human intestinal organoids, CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity,
beta-catenin accumulation and proliferative markers.
In colonic organoids, it increased AXIN2 about 3-fold and MKI67 about 2.4-fold,
with smaller increases in TCF7, MYC and LGR5.
It reduced ALPI and MUC2 by about 57% and 53%, respectively.
CASP5C-expressing colonic organoids had more viable cells, were about 40% larger
and had more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells than controls.
CASP5C also enabled prolonged Wnt-independent growth and allowed organoids to
re-enter the cell cycle after Wnt was restored.
After irradiation, CASP5c transcript increased about 42.4-fold within 24 hours,
compared with about 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b.
CASP5C-expressing irradiated organoids rapidly induced LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and
MYC, consistent with enhanced Wnt-dependent repair in this model.

The Wnt-amplifying effect required CASP5C enzymatic activity.
CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and
beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination.
The C173A catalytic mutant lost the ability to promote Wnt reporter activity,
beta-catenin localisation and Wnt-responsive gene expression.
CASP5C displayed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B.
Deleting the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and Wnt
reporter signalling.

APC was the key CASP5C substrate identified in the destruction complex.
Candidate-site prediction highlighted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC, mainly in
the N-terminal region.
CASP5C generated APC cleavage products in cells and directly cleaved truncated
APC in vitro.
Mutation of APC D556 to alanine abolished the major CASP5C-mediated cleavage
products, establishing D556 as a primary cleavage site.
In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed CASP5C
to disrupt the destruction complex and enhance Wnt reporter activity.
APC(D556A) reduced this effect.
APC fragments mimicking the cleavage products decreased beta-catenin in the
destruction complex and increased Wnt reporter activity.
Other APC cleavage sites may contribute.

CASP5C localised to the cell population in which a Wnt amplifier would be
useful.
In healthy human colon single-cell data, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent
from LGR5-positive stem cells and increased in transit-amplifying cells.
In situ hybridisation placed CASP5c deeper in crypts, overlapping with MKI67
and AXIN2 more than CASP5a or CASP5b.
Immunohistochemistry and computational analyses supported CASP5C enrichment in
proliferative crypt states, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B were more associated with
differentiated enterocytes.

Endogenous isoform perturbation supported a competitive model.
During organoid differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with
MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased later.
Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C enhanced Wnt reporter
activity in colonic and small intestinal organoids.
Concurrent CASP5c knockdown returned reporter activity towards baseline.
Preserving CASP5C while reducing CASP5A and CASP5B reduced beta-catenin
ubiquitination by about 50% and increased organoid size by about 60%.
It also increased EdU- and Ki-67-positive proliferating cells and ATP
concentration.
Simultaneous CASP5c knockdown prevented these growth effects.
Together, the data indicate that high CASP5C in transit-amplifying cells
favours DVL2 binding and Wnt amplification, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B can occupy
DVL2 and restrain CASP5C in more differentiated cells.

Disease-associated analyses were suggestive.
CASP5 and CASP4 expression increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while
retaining their cell-type distributions.
Colon or rectal adenocarcinomas carried CASP5 mutations in about 3% of cases.
CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with other Wnt pathway mutations and were
mutually exclusive with APC mutations.
In microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were more
common than APC mutations in the dataset comparison, at about 68% versus 17%.
These observations generate hypotheses about disease relevance but do not show
that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer.

## Discussion

This study assigns CASP5 a role in human intestinal epithelial biology that is
separate from CASP4-mediated noncanonical inflammasome activation.
The central mechanism is isoform specific.
CASP5C lacks the CARD region, has higher enzymatic activity, binds DVL proteins
through its catalytic domain and cleaves APC at D556.
This cleavage weakens the beta-catenin destruction complex, reduces
beta-catenin ubiquitination and raises Wnt-dependent transcription.
CASP5A and CASP5B share DVL-binding capacity but do not amplify Wnt in the same
way.
They can compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and dampen CASP5C-driven signalling.

The cellular distribution gives the mechanism biological coherence.
CASP5C enrichment in transit-amplifying cells suggests a local means of
sustaining Wnt output along the crypt axis.
Later induction of CASP5A and CASP5B during differentiation could reduce that
signal by limiting CASP5C access to DVL complexes.
This model does not replace canonical Wnt control by ligands, receptors and the
destruction complex.
Rather, CASP5C acts as an epithelial amplifier that modulates the existing
pathway.

Several caveats define the scope of the conclusions.
The mechanistic evidence spans biochemical assays, engineered cells and primary
human organoids, but it relies heavily on overexpression, knockdown and ex vivo
models.
Human tissue and public datasets support localisation and disease associations,
but they do not prove causal in vivo function.
Mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce CASP5C Wnt activity in the tested systems,
which limits direct mouse-to-human inference.
The APC D556 site is well supported as a primary CASP5C cleavage site, but
additional APC cleavage sites may contribute.
The cancer and ulcerative colitis observations should be treated as starting
points for future work, not as evidence that CASP5C is a therapeutic target or
disease cause.

## Conclusion

CASP5C is a CARD-less human CASP5 isoform that links an inflammatory
caspase-family protease to epithelial Wnt regulation.
By binding DVL proteins and cleaving APC at D556, CASP5C disrupts the
beta-catenin destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination and
amplifies Wnt-dependent transcription.
Its enrichment in transit-amplifying cells and its effects on organoid
proliferation and injury recovery support a role in intestinal epithelial
homeostasis.
CASP5A and CASP5B provide an opposing layer by competing for DVL binding.
Isoform balance offers a mechanism by which human intestinal epithelial cells
can tune Wnt signalling during renewal and repair.
