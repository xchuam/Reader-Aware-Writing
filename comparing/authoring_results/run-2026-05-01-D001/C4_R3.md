# A CARD-less CASP5 Isoform Amplifies Intestinal Epithelial Wnt Signaling

## Abstract

Human CASP5 is closely related to CASP4, the principal human mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses, but the specific function of CASP5 has remained unclear. This study identifies CASP5C, a CARD-less CASP5 isoform, as an epithelial amplifier of Wnt/beta-catenin signaling. CASP5C was enriched in human intestinal epithelial transit-amplifying cells, associated with dishevelled proteins, cleaved APC at D556, reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination, and promoted Wnt-responsive transcription. CASP5A and CASP5B bound dishevelled but did not activate Wnt signaling; instead, they restrained CASP5C by competition. In human colonic and small intestinal organoids, CASP5C increased beta-catenin accumulation, proliferation, growth, and injury-associated recovery markers. These findings support a model in which primate CASP5 acquired an isoform-specific, non-inflammasome role in epithelial homeostasis and repair.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases are cysteine proteases best known for innate immune functions. Human CASP4 and CASP5 are orthologues of mouse caspase 11, but CASP4 is more similar to mouse caspase 11 and retained the ancestral role in noncanonical inflammasome activation after cytosolic lipopolysaccharide detection. CASP5 arose after primate-specific gene duplication, shares high sequence similarity with CASP4, and includes CARD-containing isoforms that can bind lipopolysaccharide. Its distinct biological role, however, has remained unresolved.

The intestinal epithelium offered a plausible setting for a CASP5-specific function because CASP5 transcripts are enriched in intestinal tissue and epithelial fractions. Intestinal renewal depends on Wnt/beta-catenin signaling, which maintains stem-cell and progenitor proliferation while permitting differentiation along the crypt axis. Transit-amplifying cells are proliferative epithelial progenitors located between stem cells and mature enterocytes; they continue to require Wnt input as they move away from the Wnt-rich crypt base.

Wnt signaling is coordinated by dishevelled proteins (DVL1, DVL2, and DVL3), hub proteins that link receptor activation to downstream beta-catenin control. In low-Wnt conditions, the beta-catenin destruction complex, containing AXIN1 and the APC scaffold and tumor suppressor, promotes beta-catenin phosphorylation, ubiquitination, and degradation. Wnt activation stabilizes beta-catenin, allowing nuclear beta-catenin/TCF transcription measured by TOPFlash reporter assays. This study asked whether CASP5 has a non-inflammasome function in human intestinal epithelial cells and, if so, which isoform and mechanism are responsible.

## Methods

The investigation combined computational, biochemical, cellular, organoid, tissue, and public-dataset approaches. Sequence and phylogenetic analyses compared human CASP4, human CASP5, and mouse caspase 11. Human Protein Atlas and single-cell expression datasets were used to map CASP4 and CASP5 distribution across tissues and epithelial cell states.

Human colonic and small intestinal organoids modeled epithelial CASP5 expression and function. Human monocyte-derived macrophages served as inflammatory comparators. Noncanonical inflammasome activation and bacterial clearance were tested in organoid-derived epithelial cells and macrophages after exposure to Salmonella Typhimurium, Shigella flexneri, or live virulent and avirulent bacteria.

Mechanistic assays used HEK293T, HCT116, MODE-K, APC-knockout HEK293T cells, and human organoids. Wnt output was measured using TOPFlash/FOPFlash reporters, beta-catenin localization, Wnt-responsive gene expression, beta-catenin ubiquitination, and organoid growth and proliferation assays. BioID2 proximity labeling, mass spectrometry, co-immunoprecipitation, domain mapping, and immunoblotting tested CASP5-associated proteins and destruction-complex composition. CASP5C(C173A), CARD-deleted CASP5 constructs, APC(D556A), APC fragments, and recombinant CASP5C tested catalytic requirements and APC cleavage. Irradiated colonic organoids modeled epithelial injury and recovery. Human intestinal tissue, ulcerative colitis datasets, colorectal cancer datasets, CIBERSORTx, and pseudotime analyses evaluated localization and disease-associated patterns.

## Results

CASP4, not CASP5, mediated the tested noncanonical inflammasome responses. In human intestinal epithelial cells derived from colonic organoids, noncanonical inflammasome activation and clearance of intracellular Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri depended on CASP4. Human monocyte-derived macrophages showed the same CASP4 dependence during bacterial phagocytosis and clearance. These data argued against simple redundancy between CASP4 and CASP5 and prompted analysis of epithelial CASP5 function.

CASP5 was concentrated in the human intestinal epithelium and expressed as three isoforms. CASP4 transcripts were broadly distributed, whereas CASP5 transcripts were mainly enriched in intestinal tract and appendix and, at single-cell resolution, largely in intestinal epithelial cells and monocytes/macrophages. CASP5 transcripts were more abundant in healthy colonic epithelial fractions and colonic organoids than in monocyte-derived macrophages, while CASP4, CASP1, and PYCARD showed the opposite pattern. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B, and CASP5C. CASP5A is approximately 49.7 kDa, CASP5B approximately 43.2 kDa, and CASP5C approximately 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD, the caspase activation and recruitment domain, whereas CASP5C lacks the entire CARD. siRNA experiments supported CASP5C as a distinct isoform rather than a cleavage product.

CASP5-associated proteins pointed to Wnt signaling. BioID2 proximity labeling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2, and DVL3 among leading CASP5-associated proteins, and gene ontology analysis enriched Wnt-related processes. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed endogenous CASP5 interactions with all three dishevelled proteins. AXIN1 and beta-catenin were present in CASP5-DVL2 complexes. Domain mapping implicated the dishevelled DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although the interaction may be direct or indirect.

CASP5C uniquely promoted Wnt signaling. In HEK293T and HCT116 cells lacking endogenous CASP5, CASP5C increased TOPFlash activity with or without WNT3A, increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression, and promoted nuclear beta-catenin accumulation. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1, and CASP4 did not produce comparable activation. Co-expression of CASP5A or CASP5B reduced CASP5C-mediated reporter activity and competed with CASP5C for DVL2 binding. Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 isoforms did not regulate Wnt in MODE-K or HEK293T cells, consistent with a primate CASP5C specialization.

Human organoids confirmed the epithelial consequence. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity in primary colonic and small intestinal organoids. CASP5C caused cytosolic and nuclear beta-catenin accumulation and increased Wnt-responsive and proliferation-associated markers. In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 about 3-fold, TCF7 about 1.4-fold, MYC about 1.3-fold, LGR5 about 1.2-fold, and MKI67 about 2.4-fold, while reducing differentiated markers ALPI and MUC2 by about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing colonic organoids had more viable cells, were about 40% larger, contained more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and showed fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. CASP5C also enabled prolonged Wnt-independent growth and cell-cycle re-entry after Wnt restoration. After irradiation, CASP5c transcript increased about 42.4-fold within 24 hours, exceeding CASP5a and CASP5b induction, and CASP5C-expressing organoids showed rapid increases in LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7, and MYC.

Mechanistically, CASP5C disrupted the beta-catenin destruction complex through catalytic APC cleavage. CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC, and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. The catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A) lost Wnt reporter, beta-catenin localization, and gene-expression effects. CASP5C showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, while CARD deletion from CASP5A or CASP5B increased activity and Wnt reporter signaling, supporting an inhibitory role for the CARD.

APC was a direct functional substrate. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites clustered mainly in the APC N-terminal region. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, or CASP5C(C173A), generated APC cleavage products in cells, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 to alanine abolished the major CASP5C-mediated cleavage products. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduced wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the destruction complex and enhance TOPFlash activity, whereas APC(D556A) reduced this effect. APC fragments mimicking cleavage products lowered beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity, although additional cleavage sites may contribute.

CASP5C localized to the epithelial compartment where Wnt amplification would be useful. In healthy human colon single-cell data, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent in LGR5-positive stem cells, increased in transit-amplifying cells, and were most enriched in enterocytes, while CASP4 was more uniform. In situ hybridization showed CASP5a and CASP5b more luminally, whereas CASP5c localized deeper in crypts and overlapped with MKI67-positive transit-amplifying zones and AXIN2. Immunohistochemistry, CIBERSORTx, and pseudotime analyses similarly associated CASP5C with proliferative early-lineage states and CASP5A/B with mature or differentiating states.

Endogenous knockdown experiments supported a competitive isoform model. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiated markers. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C enhanced Wnt reporter activity, reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination by about 50%, increased organoid size by about 60%, increased EdU-positive and Ki-67-positive cells, and increased ATP concentration. Concurrent CASP5c knockdown returned these effects toward baseline.

## Discussion

The data identify CASP5C as a non-inflammasome amplifier of Wnt/beta-catenin signaling in human intestinal epithelial cells. CASP5C differs from CASP5A and CASP5B by lacking the CARD region, displaying higher enzymatic activity, and requiring catalytic function to promote Wnt output. Its binding to dishevelled proteins positions it near the Wnt regulatory machinery, and cleavage of APC at D556 provides a mechanism for weakening the AXIN1-containing beta-catenin destruction complex.

This model explains why CASP5 biology could not be inferred from CASP4 similarity alone. CASP4 mediated the tested bacterial noncanonical inflammasome responses, whereas CASP5 showed epithelial enrichment and isoform-specific Wnt regulation. CASP5C does not replace canonical Wnt control; rather, it appears to tune pathway strength in transit-amplifying cells as they move away from the crypt-base niche. CASP5A and CASP5B provide a counterweight by competing for dishevelled binding, potentially helping attenuate CASP5C activity as epithelial cells differentiate.

Several limitations remain. The mechanistic evidence relies heavily on cell-line, organoid, overexpression, knockdown, and biochemical assays. Human tissue and public datasets support localization, but they do not prove causal function in vivo in human intestine. Mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce CASP5C Wnt activity in the tested systems, limiting direct mouse-to-human inference. APC D556 is strongly supported as a primary cleavage site, but other CASP5C cleavage events may also contribute.

Disease associations should be interpreted cautiously. CASP5 and CASP4 expression increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type distributions. CASP5 mutations occurred in about 3% of colon or rectal adenocarcinomas, tended to co-occur with other Wnt pathway mutations, and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were more common than APC mutations in the cited dataset comparison. These observations generate hypotheses but do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer, nor do they validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.

## Conclusion

CASP5 has an intestinal epithelial function distinct from CASP4-mediated noncanonical inflammasome activation. The CARD-less isoform CASP5C binds dishevelled proteins, cleaves APC at D556, disrupts the beta-catenin destruction complex, and amplifies Wnt signaling in transit-amplifying epithelial contexts. CASP5A and CASP5B restrain this activity by competing for dishevelled binding. Together, the findings expand the functional scope of inflammatory caspase-family proteins and define CASP5 isoform balance as a potential regulator of epithelial homeostasis and repair.
