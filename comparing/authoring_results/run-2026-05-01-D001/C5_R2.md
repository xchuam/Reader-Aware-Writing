# A CARD-less CASP5 isoform amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling

## Abstract

Human CASP5 resembles CASP4, the main human mediator of noncanonical
inflammasome activation, but its function has remained unclear.
We investigated whether CASP5 has a separate role in human intestinal epithelial
cells, where Wnt/beta-catenin signalling maintains renewal.
CASP4, not CASP5, mediated the tested bacterial inflammasome responses in
organoid-derived epithelial cells and monocyte-derived macrophages.
Human organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with CASP5C lacking the
N-terminal CARD.
Proteomic and biochemical assays showed that CASP5 isoforms bind dishevelled
proteins, Wnt signalling hubs that connect receptor activity to downstream
beta-catenin regulation.
Only CASP5C amplified beta-catenin/TCF-dependent Wnt transcription.
CASP5C required catalytic activity, reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination, cleaved
APC at D556 and disrupted the beta-catenin destruction complex.
In organoids, CASP5C promoted proliferation, growth and recovery after
irradiation.
Tissue and dataset analyses placed CASP5C in transit-amplifying cells.
These findings support a model in which CASP5C provides local Wnt amplification,
while CASP5A and CASP5B restrain it by competing for dishevelled binding.

## Introduction

Human CASP4 and CASP5 are orthologues of mouse caspase 11, yet their functional
relationship is unequal.
CASP4 is closer to mouse caspase 11 and retains the ancestral role in
noncanonical inflammasome activation after cytosolic lipopolysaccharide
detection.
CASP5 arose after primate-specific gene duplication and shares high sequence
similarity with CASP4.
Despite this, its biological contribution has remained unclear.

The human intestine provides a relevant setting for resolving this question.
CASP5 transcripts are concentrated mainly in the intestinal tract and appendix,
and single-cell data place CASP5 expression largely in intestinal epithelial
cells and monocytes or macrophages.
At the same time, epithelial renewal depends on graded Wnt/beta-catenin
signalling along the crypt.
Wnt activity is highest near the crypt base, but proliferative progeny must
continue dividing as they move away from that Wnt-rich niche.
These transit-amplifying cells sit between stem cells and differentiated
enterocytes.
Mechanisms that tune Wnt signalling in this compartment are therefore central to
epithelial homeostasis and repair.

Canonical Wnt signalling is controlled by the beta-catenin destruction complex,
a multiprotein assembly that includes AXIN1 and APC.
APC is a scaffold protein and tumour suppressor that helps promote
beta-catenin phosphorylation and ubiquitination.
When Wnt signalling is active, dishevelled proteins, also called DVL proteins,
link receptor activation to downstream regulation of this destruction complex.
Stabilised beta-catenin enters the nucleus and drives TCF-dependent
transcription, which is measured by the TOPFlash reporter.

We asked whether CASP5 has a non-inflammasome function in human intestinal
epithelial cells, which isoform mediates that function and how it connects to
Wnt pathway regulation.
The evidence identifies CASP5C, a CARD-less CASP5 isoform, as a Wnt amplifier
that acts through dishevelled binding and APC cleavage.

## Methods

The study combined sequence analysis, public datasets, human organoids,
cell-line assays, biochemical reconstitution and tissue analysis.
Sequence and phylogenetic comparisons assessed human CASP4, human CASP5 and
mouse caspase 11.
Human Protein Atlas and single-cell datasets compared tissue and cell-type
expression.
Human colonic and small intestinal organoids modelled epithelial CASP5 function,
with monocyte-derived macrophages as an inflammatory comparator.
HEK293T, HCT116, MODE-K and APC-knockout HEK293T cells supported reporter,
overexpression, knockdown, cleavage and rescue experiments.
TOPFlash and FOPFlash measured Wnt transcriptional output.
Beta-catenin localisation, Wnt-responsive gene expression, ubiquitination and
organoid growth assays measured pathway activity.
BioID2 labelling, mass spectrometry, co-immunoprecipitation, domain mapping and
immunoblotting identified CASP5-associated complexes.
Mechanistic tests used CASP5C(C173A), CARD-deleted CASP5 constructs, APC
fragments, APC(D556A) and recombinant CASP5C.
Irradiated human colonic organoids modelled epithelial injury and recovery.
Human tissue and disease datasets assessed localisation and associations.

## Results

CASP4 accounted for the tested noncanonical inflammasome responses.
Although CASP4 and CASP5 share sequence similarity and can bind
lipopolysaccharide through CARD regions, CASP4 was more similar to mouse caspase
11.
In human intestinal epithelial cells derived from colonic organoids, clearance
of intracellular Salmonella Typhimurium and Shigella flexneri depended on
CASP4 rather than CASP5.
The same pattern was observed in monocyte-derived macrophages, where live
bacteria activated noncanonical inflammasome responses and bacterial clearance
through CASP4.
Thus, CASP5 did not explain the tested inflammasome phenotypes, supporting a
search for another function.

Expression analyses pointed to the intestinal epithelium as the main setting for
CASP5 biology.
CASP4 transcripts were broadly distributed, whereas CASP5 transcripts were
concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix.
Healthy colonic epithelial fractions and colonic organoids contained more CASP5
transcript than monocyte-derived macrophages.
Human organoids expressed three isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C.
CASP5A is about 49.7 kDa, CASP5B about 43.2 kDa and CASP5C about 33.3 kDa.
CASP5B lacks part of the CASP5A CARD, whereas CASP5C lacks the entire CARD.
The CARD is a caspase activation and recruitment domain at the N terminus of
CASP5A and CASP5B.

Proteomic analysis connected CASP5 to Wnt signalling.
BioID2 proximity labelling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and
DVL3 among leading CASP5-associated proteins.
Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that endogenous CASP5 isoforms interact with
DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 in colonic organoids.
AXIN1 and beta-catenin were present in CASP5-DVL2 complexes.
Domain mapping linked this interaction to the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5
catalytic domain.

Functional assays separated the three CASP5 isoforms.
In HEK293T and HCT116 cells lacking endogenous CASP5, CASP5C increased TOPFlash
activity with or without WNT3A.
CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not show the same activity.
CASP5C increased expression of Wnt-responsive genes including AXIN2 and TCF7
and promoted nuclear beta-catenin accumulation.
Co-expression of CASP5A or CASP5B reduced CASP5C-mediated reporter activation
and competed with CASP5C for DVL2 interaction.
Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 isoforms did not regulate Wnt activity,
suggesting that CASP5C acquired a specialised function after primate CASP5
duplication.

CASP5C also amplified Wnt signalling in primary human organoids.
In colonic and small intestinal organoids, CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B,
increased Wnt reporter activity.
CASP5C increased cytosolic and nuclear beta-catenin and raised Wnt-responsive
and stem or proliferation-associated markers.
In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 about 3-fold and MKI67 about
2.4-fold, with smaller increases in TCF7, MYC and LGR5.
It reduced ALPI and MUC2 and produced larger, more proliferative organoids with
fewer CK20-positive enterocytes.
After irradiation, CASP5c transcript increased about 42.4-fold within 24 hours,
and CASP5C-expressing organoids showed rapid induction of repair-associated Wnt
targets.

The mechanism required CASP5C enzymatic activity.
CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and
beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation.
It reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination.
The catalytic mutant CASP5C(C173A) failed to promote Wnt reporter activity,
beta-catenin localisation and Wnt-responsive gene expression.
CASP5C showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and deleting the
CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased activity and Wnt reporter signalling.
These results indicate that absence of the inhibitory CARD permits CASP5C
enzymatic activity.

APC was the key substrate linking CASP5C activity to the destruction complex.
CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, produced APC cleavage products in cells, and
CASP5C(C173A) did not.
Mutation of APC D556 to alanine abolished the major CASP5C-mediated cleavage
products, establishing D556 as a primary cleavage site.
In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduced wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to
disrupt the destruction complex and increase TOPFlash activity.
APC(D556A) reduced this effect.
APC fragments mimicking cleavage products decreased beta-catenin in the
destruction complex and increased Wnt reporter activity, although additional APC
cleavage sites may also contribute.

Cell-type analyses placed CASP5C in a proliferative epithelial compartment.
In situ hybridisation showed that CASP5a and CASP5b were more luminal, whereas
CASP5c localised deeper in crypts and overlapped strongly with MKI67-positive
transit-amplifying zones.
CIBERSORTx and pseudotime analyses similarly associated CASP5c with
early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and CASP5a and CASP5b with mature or
differentiating epithelial states.

Endogenous isoform perturbation supported a competitive model.
During organoid differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with
MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased later with differentiation markers.
Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C enhanced Wnt reporter
activity, reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination by about 50% in colonic organoids
and increased organoid growth.
Concurrent CASP5c knockdown returned reporter activity towards baseline and
prevented these growth effects.
Together, the data suggest that high CASP5C in transit-amplifying cells favours
DVL2 binding and Wnt amplification, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B compete for DVL2
in more differentiated cells and restrain CASP5C.

## Discussion

These findings assign CASP5 a role in human intestinal epithelial biology that
is distinct from CASP4-mediated noncanonical inflammasome activation.
The central distinction is isoform-specific: CASP5C lacks the CARD region, shows
greater enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and depends on catalytic
activity to amplify Wnt signalling.
By binding dishevelled proteins and cleaving APC at D556, CASP5C reduces
destruction-complex integrity, lowers beta-catenin ubiquitination and increases
beta-catenin-dependent transcription.

The cell-type localisation helps explain why this mechanism may be useful in
epithelial homeostasis.
Transit-amplifying cells proliferate after leaving the crypt-base stem-cell
niche, where Wnt ligands are most abundant.
CASP5C could help sustain Wnt responsiveness in this intermediate zone without
replacing canonical Wnt regulation.
The later rise of CASP5A and CASP5B during differentiation provides a possible
brake, because these isoforms bind dishevelled but do not activate Wnt in the
same way.
Competition among isoforms may therefore tune the duration of Wnt amplification
as epithelial cells move towards differentiated states.

The disease-associated observations are suggestive but not definitive.
CASP5 and CASP4 expression increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while
retaining their cell-type distributions.
Colorectal cancer datasets showed CASP5 mutations in a minority of colon or
rectal adenocarcinomas and in many microsatellite instability-high tumours.
These patterns motivate further study, but they do not establish that CASP5C
causes inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer.
They also do not validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.

Several caveats shape the interpretation.
The mechanistic evidence is strong across biochemical, cellular and organoid
systems, but it relies heavily on overexpression, knockdown and in vitro assays.
Human tissue and public datasets support localisation and associations, yet they
do not prove causal function in the human intestine in vivo.
Mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce CASP5C Wnt activity in the tested systems,
which limits direct mouse-to-human inference.
Finally, APC D556 is strongly supported as a primary CASP5C cleavage site, but
other CASP5C cleavage sites may also contribute.

## Conclusion

CASP5C is a CARD-less human CASP5 isoform that amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin
signalling in intestinal epithelial systems.
It acts outside the tested CASP4-dependent noncanonical inflammasome pathway by
binding dishevelled proteins, cleaving APC at D556, weakening the
beta-catenin destruction complex and reducing beta-catenin ubiquitination.
This activity promotes organoid proliferation and repair-associated responses
and is enriched in transit-amplifying cells.
CASP5A and CASP5B provide an opposing influence by competing for dishevelled
binding.
The resulting model places CASP5C as a human epithelial Wnt amplifier that helps
link inflammatory caspase evolution to intestinal homeostasis.
