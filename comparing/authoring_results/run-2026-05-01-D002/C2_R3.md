# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in human intestinal epithelial models

## Abstract

CASP5 is an inflammatory caspase-family protease related to CASP4 and mouse caspase 11, but its function has been unclear. In human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages, CASP4, not CASP5, acted as the main mediator of the tested noncanonical inflammasome response to cytosolic LPS and bacteria. This raised the possibility that CASP5 has a different role, particularly in the intestine, where its expression is enriched. Human intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and the CARD-less CASP5C. Proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation linked CASP5 isoforms to DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3, placing CASP5 near Wnt/beta-catenin signalling machinery. Among the isoforms, CASP5C selectively increased Wnt reporter activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, increased Wnt target-gene expression and enhanced organoid proliferation. CASP5C required catalytic activity and cleaved APC, a destruction-complex scaffold. APC D556 was a major cleavage site, although other sites may contribute. CASP5A and CASP5B did not reproduce this activity and could restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2 binding. These data support a model in which CASP5C links inflammatory caspase biology to Wnt-dependent intestinal epithelial homeostasis.

## Introduction

The intestinal epithelium must renew itself continuously while responding to microbial and inflammatory cues. Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to intestinal development, stem-cell renewal, differentiation and repair. Inflammatory caspases, by contrast, are usually studied as innate immune proteases. CASP5 sits at the intersection of these topics because it is related to inflammatory caspases yet does not behave like the main human counterpart of mouse caspase 11 in the tested noncanonical inflammasome setting.

Several observations suggested that CASP5 might have an epithelial function. CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and appeared in epithelial cells as well as some monocyte and macrophage populations. In colonic organoids, CASP5 was higher than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophages, whereas CASP4 was broader. CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella infection and after IFN-beta or IFN-gamma exposure in organoids, but LPS alone caused little epithelial induction, probably because TLR4 was low.

Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate sizes of 49.7, 43.2 and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD entirely. siRNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B. The study therefore examined whether these isoforms have distinct epithelial signalling roles.

This isoform question was especially important because intestinal epithelial outcomes depend on dosage and timing of Wnt activity. Too little signalling can compromise renewal, whereas sustained or misplaced signalling can alter differentiation and growth. A CASP5 isoform that changes Wnt output would therefore be biologically meaningful only if its effect, localization and mechanism were defined separately from the other CASP5 products.

## CASP5 associates with Wnt pathway proteins

BioID2 proximity labelling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms appeared in the interactor analysis. This was a notable result because DVL proteins connect Wnt receptor activation to downstream control of beta-catenin. Co-immunoprecipitation supported the proximity data: CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near the beta-catenin destruction complex.

Mapping experiments implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain in the interaction. The data are consistent with direct binding, but do not fully prove it. Even so, the interaction offered a plausible route by which a caspase-family protein could affect Wnt signalling. In the destruction complex, APC and AXIN1 help coordinate beta-catenin phosphorylation, ubiquitination and degradation. Disruption of this system allows beta-catenin to accumulate and activate transcriptional programmes linked to proliferation and epithelial renewal.

## CASP5C selectively activates Wnt signalling

Reporter assays showed that the Wnt effect was specific to CASP5C. In HEK293T and HCT116 cells, CASP5C increased TOPFlash activity with or without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not have the same effect. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and moved beta-catenin into the nucleus and cytosol, consistent with pathway activation.

This isoform specificity is central to the interpretation. The results do not show that all CASP5 isoforms promote Wnt signalling. CASP5C amplified the pathway, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B lacked this activity and could reduce the CASP5C effect by competing for DVL2. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce the Wnt reporter activation, suggesting, without proving, that CASP5C may represent a primate-specialized function after duplication.

The same pattern appeared in human organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. CASP5C-expressing organoids had more viable cells, were about 40% larger, contained more Ki-67- and EdU-positive cells and had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated ALPI and MUC2 decreased by about 57% and 53%.

CASP5C also altered organoid responses to Wnt withdrawal and injury-like stress. CASP5C-expressing organoids survived longer without Wnt and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored. After irradiation of human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 h, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC then rose faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These findings suggest a role in regenerative signalling in organoid models, but they do not prove repair activity in the human body.

## CASP5C cleaves APC to weaken the destruction complex

CASP5C required protease activity to activate Wnt signalling. The catalytic mutant CASP5C(C173A) lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased activity and Wnt reporter signalling. Thus, the CARD-less structure of CASP5C is linked to both enzymatic activity and pathway output.

Mechanistic experiments pointed to the destruction complex. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes. It also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. These results fit a model in which CASP5C weakens the machinery that normally keeps beta-catenin unstable.

APC emerged as a direct substrate. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites occurred mostly in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. APC D556A removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary site, though not necessarily the only functional site. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduced wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the complex and increase Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) weakened this response. APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity.

Together, these findings support a mechanism in which CASP5C cleaves APC, loosens the destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination and thereby amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin transcriptional signalling.

## Isoform localization and competition

CASP5 isoform expression varied along the epithelial lineage. In one single-cell RNA analysis, total CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent in LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. At the isoform level, however, CASP5c was deeper in crypts and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b. CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and more associated with differentiated or CK20-positive cells.

Pseudotime analysis and CIBERSORTx supported this division: CASP5c aligned with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states, while CASP5a and CASP5b aligned with mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiation markers.

Knockdown experiments tested whether the longer isoforms restrain CASP5C. Reducing CASP5a and CASP5b while keeping CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP, EdU and Ki-67. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. This supports a competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B limit CASP5C-driven Wnt activation, at least partly through DVL2 competition.

## Disease associations and discussion

The disease data are suggestive but not causal. CASP5 and CASP4 increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations in colon or rectal cancer were around 3%, similar to AXIN1 in that analysis. CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison, about 68% versus 17%. These observations do not show that CASP5C causes ulcerative colitis, inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, and they do not establish CASP5C as a validated drug target.

The stronger conclusion is that CASP5C has a specific epithelial signalling function that differs from CASP4-like noncanonical inflammasome activity. CASP5C associates with DVL proteins, requires catalytic activity, cleaves APC and increases Wnt/beta-catenin output. In organoids, this biochemical mechanism corresponds with increased Wnt target expression, proliferation, larger organoid size and improved recovery of proliferative programmes after Wnt restoration or irradiation. CASP5A and CASP5B provide an important counterpoint because they do not activate the pathway and can restrain CASP5C.

Several limits remain. The CASP5-DVL interaction has not been fully proven to be direct. APC D556 is a major CASP5C cleavage site, but other cleavage events may contribute. Organoid experiments support a role in epithelial renewal-like states, but they do not establish in vivo human repair. Within these boundaries, the data define CASP5C as a CARD-less CASP5 isoform that amplifies intestinal epithelial Wnt signalling by cleaving APC and weakening beta-catenin destruction-complex function.
