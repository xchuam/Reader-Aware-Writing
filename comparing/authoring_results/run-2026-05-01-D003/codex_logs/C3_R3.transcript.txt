+# Caspase 5c amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling through APC cleavage
+
+## Abstract
+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known for their roles in innate immune responses, but the functions of human caspase 5 have remained comparatively unclear. Although CASP5 is related to CASP4 and mouse caspase 11, human CASP4 more closely resembled caspase 11 in sequence, phylogeny and activity in the tested noncanonical inflammasome response to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. CASP5 did not perform this dominant inflammasome function in human intestinal epithelial cells or macrophages, raising the possibility that it has a distinct role. Here, evidence from human intestinal organoids and cell-based assays identifies the CARD-less CASP5C isoform as a regulator of Wnt/beta-catenin signalling. CASP5 expression was enriched in intestinal tissues and epithelial populations, and human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C. Proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation linked CASP5 isoforms to the DVL proteins, central mediators of Wnt receptor signalling. Among the CASP5 isoforms tested, CASP5C selectively increased Wnt reporter activity, induced Wnt target genes, promoted beta-catenin redistribution and supported organoid growth and proliferation. Mechanistically, CASP5C required catalytic activity and cleaved APC, including at a major site around D556, thereby weakening destruction-complex assembly and reducing beta-catenin ubiquitination. CASP5A and CASP5B did not activate Wnt signalling in the same way and could compete with CASP5C for DVL2, restraining its effect. These findings support a model in which human CASP5 isoforms are functionally diversified, with CASP5C acting as an epithelial amplifier of Wnt signalling during intestinal homeostasis and repair-associated states.
+
+## Introduction
+
+Human inflammatory caspases form a small family of related proteases that connect microbial sensing to inflammatory and epithelial responses. CASP4 and CASP5 are both related to mouse caspase 11, but their individual activities have been difficult to separate because of their sequence similarity and overlapping expression in some contexts. In tests of the noncanonical inflammasome pathway, however, CASP4 behaved as the closer human counterpart of mouse caspase 11. It was the principal mediator of responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria in the human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages examined. CASP5 did not substitute for CASP4 in those assays, suggesting that its main biological role may lie outside the canonical view of inflammatory caspase function.
+
+Several observations pointed toward the intestine as a setting in which CASP5 might have a specialized role. CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and was detected in epithelial cells as well as in some monocyte and macrophage populations. In human colonic organoids, CASP5 was expressed at higher levels than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed a broader pattern. CASP5 expression also increased in organoids after Salmonella infection or interferon-beta and interferon-gamma exposure. Lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelium, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 expression. These data positioned CASP5 as an epithelial-enriched inflammatory caspase whose expression can respond to infection-associated and cytokine-associated cues.
+
+The intestinal epithelium depends on Wnt/beta-catenin signalling for development, stem-cell renewal, repair and differentiation. Wnt pathway activity is coordinated by receptors, DVL proteins and the beta-catenin destruction complex, in which APC acts as a scaffold and tumour suppressor. Because this pathway controls the balance between proliferative crypt compartments and differentiated epithelial lineages, even moderate changes in pathway output can alter organoid growth, marker expression and recovery after injury. The work summarized here identifies a specific CASP5 isoform, CASP5C, as a Wnt amplifier in human intestinal epithelial models.
+
+## CASP5 isoforms in intestinal epithelium
+
+Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7, 43.2 and 33.3 kDa, respectively. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, whereas CASP5C lacks the CARD altogether. Knockdown experiments supported the conclusion that CASP5C is not merely a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B, but an independently expressed isoform.
+
+The isoforms also differed in epithelial localization and lineage association. Single-cell analyses indicated that total CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes in one dataset. Isoform-resolved patterns refined this view: CASP5C was detected deeper in crypt-like regions and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5A or CASP5B, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated CK20-positive cells. Pseudotime and deconvolution analyses similarly connected CASP5C with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and CASP5A/B with mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5C peaked early with MKI67, while CASP5A and CASP5B rose later with differentiation markers.
+
+These patterns suggested that CASP5 isoforms may not be functionally redundant. Instead, differential domain architecture and lineage association implied that CASP5C might operate in proliferative epithelial compartments, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B might reflect or support more differentiated states.
+
+## CASP5C engages DVL and activates Wnt signalling
+
+To identify candidate CASP5-associated pathways in human colonic organoids, BioID2 proximity labelling was used to map proteins near CASP5. DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 were detected in proximity to CASP5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched in the interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation experiments confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. DVL2-containing complexes also included AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing the CASP5 interaction near the core Wnt signalling machinery. The interaction involved the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although direct binding was not fully established.
+
+Functional assays distinguished CASP5C from the other isoforms. In TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity both with and without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same effect. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 and shifted beta-catenin into nuclear and cytosolic fractions. In organoids, CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity.
+

+## Disease associations and implications
+
