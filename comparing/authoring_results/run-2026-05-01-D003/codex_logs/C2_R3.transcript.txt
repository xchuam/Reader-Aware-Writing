+# Caspase 5c links inflammatory caspase diversification to Wnt amplification in intestinal epithelium
+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known as proteases that connect intracellular microbial sensing to pyroptosis and cytokine release. Within this family, human CASP4 has been the clearer functional counterpart of mouse caspase 11 in noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and invasive bacteria. CASP5 is closely related to CASP4 and caspase 11, but its physiological role has been harder to define. The work summarized here reframes CASP5 as more than a redundant inflammatory caspase and identifies a specific CASP5 isoform, CASP5C, as an amplifier of Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in intestinal epithelial cells.
+
+This distinction is important because CASP5 did not behave as the dominant noncanonical inflammasome protease in the tested human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages. Instead, CASP4 more closely matched mouse caspase 11 by sequence and phylogeny and carried the main activity in responses to cytosolic LPS and bacteria. CASP5 therefore appeared to have a different functional emphasis. Its expression was enriched in the intestinal tract and appendix, with signals in epithelial cells and in selected monocyte or macrophage populations. In colonic organoids, CASP5 expression exceeded CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD relative to macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed a broader distribution. These observations suggested that CASP5 might have a specialized epithelial role, particularly in the intestine.
+
+Human intestinal organoids from colon and small intestine expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. The isoforms differ substantially at the N terminus. CASP5B lacks part of the caspase activation and recruitment domain, and CASP5C lacks the CARD entirely. Knockdown experiments argued that CASP5C was not merely a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B, supporting its interpretation as a distinct transcript-derived isoform. CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella infection and after IFN-beta or IFN-gamma treatment in organoids, whereas LPS alone had only a limited effect in epithelial cells, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 responsiveness.
+
+An unbiased proximity-labelling approach in human colonic organoids provided the first clue to the pathway affected by CASP5. BioID2 analysis identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched among candidate interactors. Co-immunoprecipitation experiments then showed that CASP5 isoforms associated with all three DVL proteins. DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near the core Wnt destruction complex machinery. Mapping experiments implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain in the interaction. The data support close physical association and may be consistent with direct binding, although the dossier does not establish direct binding as the only possible interpretation.
+
+Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to intestinal development, epithelial repair, stem-cell maintenance and differentiation. DVL proteins transmit receptor-proximal Wnt signals to downstream control of beta-catenin, while APC acts as a scaffold and tumour suppressor within the beta-catenin destruction complex. In the absence of sufficient Wnt pathway activation, beta-catenin is phosphorylated, ubiquitinated and degraded. When the pathway is activated, beta-catenin accumulates, enters the nucleus and supports transcriptional programmes that sustain proliferation and intestinal epithelial renewal. A CASP5 interaction with DVL therefore suggested a route by which an inflammatory caspase-related protein could influence epithelial homeostasis independently of canonical inflammasome execution.
+
+Functional reporter assays narrowed this activity to CASP5C. In HEK293T and HCT116 cells, CASP5C increased TOPFlash Wnt reporter activity both with and without WNT3A stimulation. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not reproduce this effect. CASP5C also increased expression of Wnt target and proliferation-associated genes, including AXIN2 and TCF7, and promoted accumulation of beta-catenin in cytosolic and nuclear fractions. By contrast, CASP5A and CASP5B could blunt the CASP5C effect when co-expressed, apparently by competing for DVL2. CARD-less engineered forms of mouse caspase 11 did not mimic CASP5C-driven Wnt activation, suggesting that this activity may reflect a primate CASP5 specialization after gene duplication. That evolutionary conclusion should remain cautious, because the experiments tested selected engineered forms rather than all possible evolutionary intermediates.
+
+The same isoform specificity was evident in human intestinal organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. CASP5C-expressing organoids contained more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger and showed increased Ki-67 and EdU staining, consistent with enhanced proliferation. They also had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes, suggesting a shift away from terminal differentiation. Transcriptional changes fit this pattern: CASP5C increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold, while reducing the differentiation-associated markers ALPI and MUC2 by about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids could also persist longer after Wnt withdrawal and re-enter the cell cycle after Wnt was restored. These findings support a role in epithelial growth competence in organoids, but they do not by themselves prove a repair function in human tissue in vivo.
+
+The isoform competition model was strengthened by selective knockdown experiments. Reducing CASP5A and CASP5B while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP content and EdU and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5C was also knocked down, these effects fell back. This pattern argues that CASP5A and CASP5B can restrain CASP5C-dependent Wnt amplification, likely through competition for DVL2-containing complexes. The relationship among isoforms is therefore not simply additive. CASP5C is the Wnt-amplifying isoform, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B can oppose or limit that effect.
+
+Cell-state analyses added another layer to this model. In one single-cell RNA analysis, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. At first, this seemed difficult to reconcile with a proliferative CASP5C function. Isoform-level and spatial patterns helped resolve the apparent conflict. CASP5C was detected deeper in crypts and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5A or CASP5B, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated, CK20-positive regions. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses likewise linked CASP5C to early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and linked CASP5A and CASP5B to mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5C peaked early with MKI67, while CASP5A and CASP5B rose later with differentiation markers. These data suggest that the same gene locus can encode isoforms with distinct positions along the epithelial differentiation trajectory.
+
+Mechanistic experiments placed APC cleavage downstream of CASP5C catalytic activity. CASP5C reduced the abundance of DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation, and it reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. A catalytic-site mutant, CASP5C(C173A), lost Wnt reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C also showed greater enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased their activity and gave stronger Wnt reporter signalling. These findings indicate that CARD loss is functionally important, but they also show that the catalytic domain and enzymatic activity are required for the CASP5C phenotype.
+
+APC emerged as a relevant substrate. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated mostly in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and the catalytic mutant CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro, supporting APC as a direct substrate under the tested conditions. Mutation of APC aspartate 556 to alanine removed the major cleavage products. In APC knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction complex composition and increase Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) weakened these effects. APC fragments resembling CASP5C-generated cleavage products reduced beta-catenin within the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. Aspartate 556 therefore appears to be a primary cleavage site, but other APC cleavage sites may also contribute and should not be excluded.
+
+The model that emerges is that CASP5C binds near DVL-containing Wnt signalling complexes and, through its catalytic activity, cleaves APC in a way that weakens beta-catenin destruction complex function. Reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination and altered AXIN1-associated complex composition then permit greater beta-catenin accumulation and transcriptional output. CASP5A and CASP5B, which retain full or partial CARD regions and are less catalytically active in this context, do not activate Wnt signalling in the same way and can restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. This creates an isoform-sensitive rheostat rather than a simple on-off role for the CASP5 locus.
+
+The injury experiments are consistent with this homeostatic interpretation. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased 5.6-fold and 6.6-fold. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC rose more rapidly in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data suggest that CASP5C can support a faster restoration of Wnt-associated regenerative programmes in organoid culture. The appropriate conclusion is restrained: CASP5C promotes proliferative and Wnt-responsive states in human intestinal organoids after damage-like stress, but the evidence does not establish that manipulating CASP5C would improve repair in patients.
+
+Disease-associated observations extend the relevance of the pathway but remain correlative. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while largely maintaining their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations appeared in roughly 3% of colon or rectal cancers in the reported analysis, similar to AXIN1. CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations but were mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In one MSI-high colorectal cancer comparison, CASP5 mutations were reported more frequently than APC mutations, at about 68% versus 17%. These findings raise questions about how CASP5 variation intersects with Wnt pathway alterations in inflammatory disease and cancer, but they do not show that CASP5C causes ulcerative colitis, inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer, and they do not validate CASP5C as a drug target.
+
+Overall, these findings assign a specific epithelial function to an inflammatory caspase family member whose role had been unclear. CASP5C, a CARD-less CASP5 isoform enriched in proliferative intestinal epithelial contexts, amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin signalling by associating with DVL complexes and cleaving APC, with aspartate 556 serving as a major but probably not exclusive cleavage site. CASP5A and CASP5B differ from CASP5C functionally and can limit its activity, emphasizing that CASP5 biology must be interpreted at the isoform level. The study connects caspase diversification to intestinal epithelial renewal and provides a mechanistic framework for future work on how inflammatory signalling modules are repurposed in tissue homeostasis.

This pattern argues that CASP5A and CASP5B can restrain CASP5C-dependent Wnt amplification, likely through competition for DVL2-containing complexes. The relationship among isoforms is therefore not simply additive. CASP5C is the Wnt-amplifying isoform, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B can oppose or limit that effect.
+
+Cell-state analyses added another layer to this model. In one single-cell RNA analysis, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. At first, this seemed difficult to reconcile with a proliferative CASP5C function. Isoform-level and spatial patterns helped resolve the apparent conflict. CASP5C was detected deeper in crypts and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5A or CASP5B, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated, CK20-positive regions. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses likewise linked CASP5C to early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and linked CASP5A and CASP5B to mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5C peaked early with MKI67, while CASP5A and CASP5B rose later with differentiation markers. These data suggest that the same gene locus can encode isoforms with distinct positions along the epithelial differentiation trajectory.
+
+Mechanistic experiments placed APC cleavage downstream of CASP5C catalytic activity. CASP5C reduced the abundance of DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation, and it reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. A catalytic-site mutant, CASP5C(C173A), lost Wnt reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C also showed greater enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased their activity and gave stronger Wnt reporter signalling. These findings indicate that CARD loss is functionally important, but they also show that the catalytic domain and enzymatic activity are required for the CASP5C phenotype.
+
+APC emerged as a relevant substrate. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated mostly in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and the catalytic mutant CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro, supporting APC as a direct substrate under the tested conditions. Mutation of APC aspartate 556 to alanine removed the major cleavage products. In APC knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction complex composition and increase Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) weakened these effects. APC fragments resembling CASP5C-generated cleavage products reduced beta-catenin within the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. Aspartate 556 therefore appears to be a primary cleavage site, but other APC cleavage sites may also contribute and should not be excluded.
+
+The model that emerges is that CASP5C binds near DVL-containing Wnt signalling complexes and, through its catalytic activity, cleaves APC in a way that weakens beta-catenin destruction complex function. Reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination and altered AXIN1-associated complex composition then permit greater beta-catenin accumulation and transcriptional output. CASP5A and CASP5B, which retain full or partial CARD regions and are less catalytically active in this context, do not activate Wnt signalling in the same way and can restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. This creates an isoform-sensitive rheostat rather than a simple on-off role for the CASP5 locus.
+
+The injury experiments are consistent with this homeostatic interpretation. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased 5.6-fold and 6.6-fold. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC rose more rapidly in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data suggest that CASP5C can support a faster restoration of Wnt-associated regenerative programmes in organoid culture. The appropriate conclusion is restrained: CASP5C promotes proliferative and Wnt-responsive states in human intestinal organoids after damage-like stress, but the evidence does not establish that manipulating CASP5C would improve repair in patients.
+
+Disease-associated observations extend the relevance of the pathway but remain correlative. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while largely maintaining their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations appeared in roughly 3% of colon or rectal cancers in the reported analysis, similar to AXIN1. CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations but were mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In one MSI-high colorectal cancer comparison, CASP5 mutations were reported more frequently than APC mutations, at about 68% versus 17%. These findings raise questions about how CASP5 variation intersects with Wnt pathway alterations in inflammatory disease and cancer, but they do not show that CASP5C causes ulcerative colitis, inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer, and they do not validate CASP5C as a drug target.
+
+Overall, these findings assign a specific epithelial function to an inflammatory caspase family member whose role had been unclear. CASP5C, a CARD-less CASP5 isoform enriched in proliferative intestinal epithelial contexts, amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin signalling by associating with DVL complexes and cleaving APC, with aspartate 556 serving as a major but probably not exclusive cleavage site. CASP5A and CASP5B differ from CASP5C functionally and can limit its activity, emphasizing that CASP5 biology must be interpreted at the isoform level. The study connects caspase diversification to intestinal epithelial renewal and provides a mechanistic framework for future work on how inflammatory signalling modules are repurposed in tissue homeostasis.
