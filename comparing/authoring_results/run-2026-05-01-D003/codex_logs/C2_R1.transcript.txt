+# Caspase 5c amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling through APC cleavage
+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known as proteases that sense microbial danger and initiate innate immune responses. Human CASP4 and CASP5 are closely related to mouse caspase 11, and all three have been discussed in the context of noncanonical inflammasome activation. Yet CASP5 has remained difficult to place. Although it resembles CASP4 and caspase 11 by sequence, experiments in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages indicated that CASP4, rather than CASP5, was the main mediator of the tested response to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. This separation raised a simple question: if CASP5 is not the dominant caspase in this setting, does it have a different function?
+
+The answer that emerges from the work summarized here is that one CASP5 isoform, CASP5C, acts in intestinal epithelial cells as an amplifier of Wnt signalling. The mechanism links CASP5C to Dishevelled proteins and to cleavage of the Wnt destruction-complex scaffold APC. This activity is not shared equally by CASP5 isoforms. CASP5A and CASP5B do not reproduce the effect and can restrain CASP5C, apparently by competing for Dishevelled binding. The findings therefore recast CASP5 not as a single inflammatory-caspase activity, but as an isoform-resolved regulator of epithelial signalling with a potential role in intestinal homeostasis and repair.
+
+CASP5 expression was enriched in the intestinal tract and appendix, with signal in epithelial cells and in some monocyte or macrophage populations. Human colonic organoids expressed CASP5 more strongly relative to CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD than did macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed a broader pattern. Organoids from human colon and small intestine expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD entirely. Knockdown experiments argued that CASP5C was not merely a cleavage product of longer CASP5 isoforms. Instead, it is an independently expressed transcript and protein species.
+
+Expression of CASP5 in organoids increased after Salmonella infection and after exposure to interferon beta or interferon gamma. Lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelial cultures, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 responsiveness. These induction patterns connected CASP5 to inflammatory or microbial contexts, but the isoform structure suggested that CASP5 might also have functions outside the canonical inflammatory-caspase pathway.
+
+An unbiased proximity-labelling strategy in human colonic organoids provided a clue. BioID2 analysis identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms appeared in interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL proteins. DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near the machinery that regulates beta-catenin stability. Binding mapped to the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain. The data support a close physical relationship between CASP5 and Dishevelled proteins, although they do not by themselves prove that every interaction is direct.
+
+The functional consequence was highly isoform-specific. In TOPFlash reporter assays, CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both in the presence and absence of WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same activation. CASP5C also increased expression of Wnt target genes, including AXIN2 and TCF7, and shifted beta-catenin into cytosolic and nuclear fractions. In contrast, CASP5A and CASP5B could reduce the CASP5C effect, apparently by competing with CASP5C for DVL2 binding. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 variants did not reproduce the Wnt effect, suggesting that this activity may reflect a specialization of primate CASP5 after gene duplication, although the evidence does not support a broader evolutionary claim beyond that interpretation.
+
+Human organoid experiments extended these observations into a more physiological epithelial system. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity in organoids. Organoids expressing CASP5C contained more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger, and showed increased Ki-67 and EdU positivity. They also contained fewer CK20-positive enterocytes, consistent with a shift away from terminal differentiation. Quantitatively, CASP5C increased markers associated with Wnt signalling and proliferation: AXIN2 rose about threefold, TCF7 about 1.4-fold, MYC about 1.3-fold, LGR5 about 1.2-fold and MKI67 about 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated markers moved in the opposite direction, with ALPI and MUC2 reduced by about 57% and 53%, respectively.
+
+The behaviour of CASP5C-expressing organoids under Wnt limitation further supported a role in epithelial fitness. These organoids survived longer after Wnt withdrawal and could re-enter the cell cycle when Wnt was restored. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased sharply, rising 42.4-fold within 24 hours, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased 5.6-fold and 6.6-fold. After irradiation, stem-cell and Wnt-associated genes such as LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC rose faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These results are consistent with a repair-associated epithelial program in organoid culture. They do not establish that CASP5C is sufficient to drive repair in the human intestine in vivo.
+
+Mechanistically, CASP5C acted on the beta-catenin destruction complex. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced the presence of DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes. CASP5C also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. These observations fit with a model in which CASP5C weakens the destruction complex, thereby allowing beta-catenin stabilization and downstream transcriptional activation.
+
+Protease activity was required. A catalytic mutant, CASP5C(C173A), lost the ability to activate the Wnt reporter and to produce the beta-catenin effects. CASP5C showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B. Moreover, removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased their activity and enabled stronger Wnt reporter signalling. These results suggest that the absence of the CARD in CASP5C is not merely a structural difference but is linked to the enzymatic and signalling properties of the isoform.
+
+APC emerged as an important substrate. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC were concentrated mainly in the N-terminal region. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro, supporting APC as a direct substrate. Mutation of APC residue D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary cleavage site. However, the data also leave room for additional APC sites to contribute.
+
+APC-reconstitution experiments strengthened this model. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, adding wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex organization and increase Wnt reporter activity. APC(D556A) weakened this effect. APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin association with the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. Together, these results support a mechanism in which CASP5C cleaves APC, alters destruction-complex assembly, reduces beta-catenin turnover and amplifies Wnt transcriptional output.
+
+The cellular distribution of CASP5 isoforms adds another layer to the interpretation. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell RNA analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. This might seem at odds with a role in Wnt-associated epithelial renewal. Isoform-resolved analyses clarified the picture. CASP5c was found deeper in the crypt and overlapped more strongly with MKI67 and AXIN2, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b were more luminal and associated with differentiated or CK20-positive states. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses similarly linked CASP5c to early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and linked CASP5a and CASP5b to mature or differentiating states.
+
+Organoid differentiation experiments supported this temporal separation. After Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early, together with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiation markers. Knockdown experiments further supported an antagonistic relationship among isoforms. When CASP5a and CASP5b were reduced while CASP5C was preserved, organoids showed higher Wnt reporter activity, larger size, increased ATP and more EdU- and Ki-67-positive cells. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects declined. This pattern supports a competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C, at least in part through shared DVL2 binding.
+
+The disease-associated observations are more tentative. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets but retained their cell-type patterns. In colon and rectal cancer datasets, CASP5 mutations were found in roughly 3% of cases, similar to AXIN1 in that analysis. CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations but were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC mutations in one comparison, approximately 68% versus 17%. These associations suggest that CASP5 variation can appear in disease contexts involving intestinal inflammation or Wnt-pathway disruption, but they do not show that CASP5C causes ulcerative colitis, inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer. They also do not validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.
+
+The central conclusion is narrower and stronger: CASP5C is a CARD-less CASP5 isoform that amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in human intestinal epithelial models. It does so through a protease-dependent mechanism involving DVL association, APC cleavage and disruption of beta-catenin destruction-complex function. CASP5A and CASP5B lack this activity and can oppose CASP5C, indicating that CASP5 biology cannot be interpreted accurately without isoform resolution. By linking an inflammatory-caspase-family protein to Wnt control in organoids, these findings provide a mechanistic bridge between epithelial stress responses and the signalling programs that maintain intestinal tissue architecture.

+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known as proteases that sense microbial danger and initiate innate immune responses.
