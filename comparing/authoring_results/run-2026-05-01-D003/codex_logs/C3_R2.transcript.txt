Scientific writing is a process for communicating research with precision and clarity. Write manuscripts using IMRAD structure, citations (APA/AMA/Vancouver), figures/tables, and reporting guidelines (CONSORT/STROBE/PRISMA). Apply this skill for research papers and journal submissions.
Critical Principle: Always write in full paragraphs with flowing prose. Never submit bullet points in the final manuscript. Use a two-stage process: first create section outlines with key points using research-lookup, then convert those outlines into complete paragraphs.

## Core Capabilities
### 1. Manuscript Structure and Organization
IMRAD Format: Guide papers through the standard Introduction, Methods, Results, And Discussion structure used across most scientific disciplines. This includes:
- Introduction: Establish research context, identify gaps, state objectives
- Methods: Detail study design, populations, procedures, and analysis approaches
- Results: Present findings objectively without interpretation
- Discussion: Interpret results, acknowledge limitations, propose future directions
For detailed guidance on IMRAD structure, refer to references/imrad_structure.md.
Alternative Structures: Support discipline-specific formats including:
- Review articles (narrative, systematic, scoping)
- Case reports and case series
- Meta-analyses and pooled analyses
- Theoretical/modeling papers

### 2. Section-Specific Writing Guidance
Abstract Composition: Craft concise, standalone summaries (100-250 words) that capture the paper's purpose, methods, results, and conclusions. Support both structured abstracts (with labeled sections) and unstructured single-paragraph formats.
Introduction Development: Build compelling introductions that:
- Establish the research problem's importance
- Review relevant literature systematically
- Identify knowledge gaps or controversies
- State clear research questions or hypotheses
- Explain the study's novelty and significance
Methods Documentation: Ensure reproducibility through:
- Detailed participant/sample descriptions
- Clear procedural documentation
- Statistical methods with justification
- Equipment and materials specifications
- Ethical approval and consent statements
Results Presentation: Present findings with:
- Logical flow from primary to secondary outcomes
- Integration with figures and tables
- Statistical significance with effect sizes
- Objective reporting without interpretation
Discussion Construction: Synthesize findings by:
- Relating results to research questions
- Comparing with existing literature
- Acknowledging limitations honestly
- Proposing mechanistic explanations
- Suggesting practical implications and future research
### 3. Citation and Reference Management

Major Citation Styles:
- AMA (American Medical Association): Numbered superscript citations, common in medicine

- Chicago: Notes-bibliography or author-date, humanities and sciences
- IEEE: Numbered square brackets, engineering and computer science

Objectivity:

### 7. Writing Process: From Outline to Full Paragraphs
CRITICAL: Always write in full paragraphs, never submit bullet points in scientific papers.
Scientific papers must be written in complete, flowing prose. Use this two-stage approach for effective writing:
Stage 1: Create Section Outlines with Key Points
When starting a new section:
- Use the research-lookup skill to gather relevant literature and data

- Main arguments or findings to present
- Key studies to cite
- Data points and statistics to include

Example outline (Introduction section):
```
- Background: AI in drug discovery gaining traction
* Cite recent reviews (Smith 2023, Jones 2024)
* Traditional methods are slow and expensive
- Gap: Limited application to rare diseases
* Only 2 prior studies (Lee 2022, Chen 2023)
* Small datasets remain a challenge
- Our approach: Transfer learning from common diseases
* Novel architecture combining X and Y
- Study objectives: Validate on 3 rare disease datasets
```
Stage 2: Convert Key Points to Full Paragraphs
Once the outline is complete, expand each bullet point into proper prose:
- Transform bullet points into complete sentences with subjects, verbs, and objects
- Add transitions between sentences and ideas (however, moreover, in contrast, subsequently)
- Integrate citations naturally within sentences, not as lists
- Expand with context and explanation that bullet points omit
- Ensure logical flow from one sentence to the next within each paragraph
- Vary sentence structure to maintain reader engagement
Example conversion to prose:
```
Artificial intelligence approaches have gained significant traction in drug discovery
pipelines over the past decade (Smith, 2023; Jones, 2024). While these computational
methods show promise for accelerating the identification of therapeutic candidates,
traditional experimental approaches remain slow and resource-intensive, often requiring
years of laboratory work and substantial financial investment. However, the application
of AI to rare diseases has been limited, with only two prior studies demonstrating
proof-of-concept results (Lee, 2022; Chen, 2023). The primary obstacle has been the
scarcity of training data for conditions affecting small patient populations.
To address this challenge, we developed a transfer learning approach that leverages
knowledge from well-characterized common diseases to predict therapeutic targets for
rare conditions. Our novel neural architecture combines convolutional layers for
molecular feature extraction with attention mechanisms for protein-ligand interaction
modeling. The objective of this study was to validate our approach across three
independent rare disease datasets, assessing both predictive accuracy and biological
interpretability of the results.
```

+## Introduction
+
+Canonical Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to intestinal epithelial biology. A core regulatory feature of the pathway is the beta-catenin destruction complex, in which APC acts as both scaffold and tumour suppressor. +
+Inflammatory caspases are usually considered in the context of host defence and inflammasome-related responses. Human CASP4 resembles mouse caspase 11 by sequence and phylogeny and functions as a principal mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. By contrast, the function of CASP5 has been unclear. Experiments in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages did not support CASP5 as the main executor of the tested noncanonical response, raising the possibility that CASP5 has a distinct function, particularly in tissues where it is preferentially expressed.
+

+

+## Methods
+
+Human intestinal epithelial organoids from colon and small intestine were used to examine CASP5 isoform expression, inducibility and function. Expression patterns were compared with macrophage-associated inflammatory caspase components and with single-cell transcriptomic patterns across epithelial states. Stimulation conditions included bacterial infection with Salmonella, interferon beta and interferon gamma, as well as lipopolysaccharide exposure. The response to lipopolysaccharide was interpreted in the context of low epithelial TLR4 expression.
+
+Protein-proximity and interaction studies were used to identify CASP5-associated pathways. BioID2 analysis in human colonic organoids identified proteins in the vicinity of CASP5, and co-immunoprecipitation experiments tested associations among CASP5 isoforms, Dishevelled proteins, AXIN1 and beta-catenin. Domain-mapping experiments assessed the contribution of the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain to the interaction. Wnt pathway activity was measured using TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T cells, HCT116 cells and organoids, with and without WNT3A stimulation. The activity of CASP5 isoforms was compared with that of CASP1, CASP4 and engineered CARD-less forms of mouse caspase 11.
+
+Organoid phenotypes were assessed by reporter activity, viability, size, proliferative markers and differentiation markers. Ki-67 and EdU were used to measure proliferative compartments, whereas CK20, ALPI and MUC2 were used to evaluate differentiation-associated outputs. Wnt withdrawal and re-addition experiments tested whether CASP5C altered organoid persistence and cell-cycle re-entry under transient loss of Wnt support. Irradiated human colonic organoids were used to examine induction of CASP5 isoforms and subsequent expression of stem-cell and Wnt-associated genes.
+
+Mechanistic experiments focused on the destruction complex and APC cleavage. AXIN1-containing complexes were analysed after Wnt stimulation to assess the abundance of DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP. Beta-catenin ubiquitination was evaluated as a downstream consequence of destruction-complex regulation. Catalytic dependence was tested using CASP5C(C173A), and APC cleavage was tested using predicted CASP5 cleavage sites, recombinant CASP5C and truncated APC substrates in vitro. APC knockout HEK293T cells were reconstituted with wild-type APC or APC(D556A) to assess whether the D556 site contributed to CASP5C-dependent reporter activation and destruction-complex disruption. APC fragments resembling cleavage products were also examined for effects on beta-catenin complex association and reporter activity.

+## Results
+
+CASP5 showed intestinal epithelial enrichment and inducibility consistent with a role beyond canonical inflammatory-caspase responses. CASP5 expression was concentrated in intestinal and appendiceal tissues, and human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C. CASP5 increased after Salmonella infection, interferon beta and interferon gamma in organoids, whereas lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelium, consistent with low TLR4 expression. These observations distinguished epithelial CASP5 regulation from a simple cytosolic-LPS response and motivated tests for epithelial-specific functions.
+
+Proximity mapping linked CASP5 to Wnt pathway components. BioID2 analysis in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms emerged from interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near the machinery that controls beta-catenin stability. The interaction involved the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain. Although these experiments support a close molecular association, they do not by themselves prove a direct binary interaction between CASP5 and DVL.
+
+Functional assays revealed a striking isoform specificity. CASP5C increased TOPFlash Wnt reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both in the presence and absence of WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not reproduce this effect. CASP5C also increased expression of Wnt-responsive genes including AXIN2 and TCF7 and promoted redistribution of beta-catenin into nuclear and cytosolic compartments. In contrast, CASP5A and CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduce the CASP5C-mediated Wnt response. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce the CASP5C effect, suggesting, without proving, that this activity may reflect a primate CASP5 specialization after gene duplication.
+
+The same isoform specificity was observed in organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. CASP5C expression increased viable cell numbers, produced organoids approximately 40% larger than controls, increased Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. Gene-expression changes were consistent with increased Wnt and proliferative tone: AXIN2 rose about threefold, TCF7 1.4-fold, MYC 1.3-fold, LGR5 1.2-fold and MKI67 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated transcripts were reduced, with ALPI down by about 57% and MUC2 down by about 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids also persisted longer after Wnt withdrawal and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored, consistent with enhanced regenerative competence in the organoid system.
+
+Injury-associated experiments further supported a link between CASP5C and epithelial repair programs. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC increased more rapidly in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data indicate that CASP5C can accompany and potentiate repair-associated Wnt responses in vitro, although they do not establish that CASP5C is sufficient or necessary for repair in the human intestine in vivo.
+
+CASP5C altered destruction-complex composition in a manner consistent with beta-catenin stabilization. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced the abundance of DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes. It also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Catalytic activity was required, because CASP5C(C173A) lost both Wnt reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and conferred stronger Wnt reporter signalling. These findings suggest that CARD architecture restrains catalytic access or activity in the longer isoforms, whereas CASP5C is configured to act on pathway components.
+
+APC emerged as a key substrate linking CASP5C activity to Wnt activation. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated largely in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved a truncated APC substrate in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, and in APC knockout HEK293T cells reconstitution with wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the complex and increase Wnt reporter activity more effectively than reconstitution with APC(D556A). APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin abundance in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. D556 therefore appears to be an important cleavage site, but the available data leave room for additional APC cleavage sites to contribute.
+
+Cell-state analyses placed CASP5 isoforms in distinct epithelial contexts. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. However, CASP5c showed a deeper crypt distribution and greater overlap with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and more associated with differentiated CK20-positive cells. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses supported an association of CASP5c with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and of CASP5a and CASP5b with mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased later with differentiation markers.
+
+Isoform-competition experiments supported the functional relevance of these expression patterns. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 staining. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects declined. This result supports a competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C partly by competing for DVL2, thereby limiting the ability of CASP5C to amplify Wnt signalling.
+
+## Discussion
+
+These findings define CASP5C as an epithelial Wnt amplifier rather than a redundant copy of CASP4 or mouse caspase 11. The distinction is important because CASP5 has often been considered through the lens of inflammatory caspase biology. In the tested systems, CASP4 was more consistent with the noncanonical cytosolic-LPS response, whereas CASP5C acted through a separate pathway involving DVL-associated destruction complexes and APC cleavage. CASP5C therefore expands the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteins into epithelial homeostasis and regeneration-associated signalling.
+
+The proposed mechanism is coherent with several independent observations. CASP5 isoforms localize near DVL proteins, CASP5C uniquely activates Wnt reporters, catalytic inactivation abolishes the effect, CASP5C reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination, and APC cleavage products phenocopy aspects of pathway activation. APC D556 contributes substantially to the cleavage pattern and functional output, but the data do not require D556 to be the only relevant site. A restrained interpretation is that CASP5C cleaves APC in a way that weakens destruction-complex function and permits beta-catenin-dependent transcription, with D556 as a major contributor.
+
+The longer CASP5 isoforms appear to have a different role. CASP5A and CASP5B did not activate Wnt signalling in the same way as CASP5C and could restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. Their more differentiated epithelial distribution suggests that isoform balance may help tune Wnt signalling as cells move from proliferative crypt compartments toward mature luminal states. In this model, CASP5c supports early-lineage or transit-amplifying programs, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b provide a counterweight during differentiation.
+
+The disease-associated observations are intriguing but should be interpreted cautiously. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations occurred in about 3% of colon or rectal cancers in the reported analysis, similar to AXIN1, and tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations while being mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison, approximately 68% versus 17%. These associations are compatible with a relationship between CASP5 and Wnt-pathway biology, but they do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.
+
+Overall, the evidence supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C couples epithelial stress and repair contexts to Wnt/beta-catenin amplification through APC cleavage. The work clarifies why CASP5 may have remained difficult to place within conventional inflammasome frameworks and suggests that human CASP5 diversification produced a CARD-less isoform with a specialized epithelial signalling function. Future work will need to determine how CASP5C activity is regulated in vivo, whether APC cleavage occurs during physiological repair, and how isoform balance is altered in chronic inflammation and neoplasia.

+## Abstract
+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known as innate immune proteases, yet the biological function of human CASP5 has remained less defined than that of CASP4 or mouse caspase 11. +## Introduction
+
+Canonical Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to intestinal epithelial biology. It supports intestinal development, crypt maintenance, stem-cell renewal, transit-amplifying proliferation and repair after injury, while requiring tight control to preserve differentiation and tissue architecture. A core regulatory feature is the beta-catenin destruction complex, in which APC acts as scaffold and tumour suppressor. Dishevelled proteins link receptor-proximal Wnt events to downstream remodelling of this complex.

+Inflammatory caspases are usually considered in the context of host defence and inflammasome-related responses. +Inflammatory caspases are best known as innate immune proteases, yet the biological function of human CASP5 has remained less defined than that of CASP4 or mouse caspase 11. Here, human intestinal epithelial models reveal an isoform-specific role for CASP5C in regulation of Wnt/beta-catenin signalling. CASP5 was enriched in intestinal tissues and epithelial organoids, where three isoforms, CASP5A, CASP5B and the CARD-lacking CASP5C, were detected. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 or CASP4, increased Wnt reporter activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, induced Wnt target genes and expanded proliferative organoid compartments. Mechanistically, CASP5 isoforms associated with Dishevelled proteins through interactions involving the Dishevelled DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain. CASP5C enzymatic activity was required for pathway activation and was linked to cleavage of the destruction-complex scaffold APC, with D556 contributing to major cleavage products. CASP5A and CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and restrain its signalling effect. These findings support a model in which a primate CASP5 isoform couples inflammatory-caspase biology to epithelial Wnt tone and regenerative competence.
+
+## Introduction
+
+Canonical Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to intestinal epithelial biology. It supports intestinal development, crypt maintenance, stem-cell renewal, transit-amplifying proliferation and repair after injury, while requiring tight control to preserve differentiation and tissue architecture. A core regulatory feature is the beta-catenin destruction complex, in which APC acts as scaffold and tumour suppressor. Dishevelled proteins link receptor-proximal Wnt events to downstream remodelling of this complex.
+
+Inflammatory caspases are usually considered in the context of host defence and inflammasome-related responses. Human CASP4 resembles mouse caspase 11 by sequence and phylogeny and functions as a principal mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. By contrast, the function of CASP5 has been unclear. Experiments in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages did not support CASP5 as the main executor of the tested noncanonical response, raising the possibility that CASP5 has a distinct function, particularly in tissues where it is preferentially expressed.
+
+The intestine provided a compelling setting to examine this question. CASP5 expression was concentrated in intestinal tract and appendix and was detected in epithelial cells as well as in some monocyte and macrophage populations. In colonic organoids, CASP5 expression exceeded that of CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophage expression patterns, whereas CASP4 showed broader distribution. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7, 43.2 and 33.3 kDa, respectively. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD altogether. The CARD-less CASP5C isoform was not explained as a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B in siRNA experiments, indicating that it should be considered as a distinct isoform with potentially distinct function.
+
+Here, the evidence supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C amplifies epithelial Wnt/beta-catenin signalling by enzymatically modifying APC-containing destruction complexes. The findings separate CASP5C from CASP5A and CASP5B, distinguish CASP5 from CASP4 in intestinal epithelial biology, and identify a mechanism by which a caspase-family protease may influence epithelial renewal without acting as the primary noncanonical inflammasome protease in the tested systems.
+
+## Methods
+
+Human colon and small-intestinal organoids were used to examine CASP5 isoform expression, inducibility and function. Expression patterns were compared with macrophage-associated inflammatory caspase components and with single-cell transcriptomic patterns across epithelial states. Stimulation conditions included Salmonella, interferon beta, interferon gamma and lipopolysaccharide.
+
+BioID2 and co-immunoprecipitation were used to identify CASP5-associated pathways and test associations among CASP5 isoforms, Dishevelled proteins, AXIN1 and beta-catenin. Domain mapping assessed the DVL DEP domain and CASP5 catalytic domain. Wnt activity was measured using TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T cells, HCT116 cells and organoids, with and without WNT3A. CASP5 isoforms were compared with CASP1, CASP4 and engineered CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms.
+
+Organoid phenotypes were assessed by reporter activity, viability, size, Ki-67, EdU and differentiation-associated markers including CK20, ALPI and MUC2. Wnt withdrawal and re-addition experiments tested persistence and cell-cycle re-entry. Irradiated colonic organoids were used to examine induction of CASP5 isoforms and expression of stem-cell and Wnt-associated genes.
+
+Mechanistic experiments focused on destruction-complex composition and APC cleavage. AXIN1-containing complexes were analysed after Wnt stimulation, beta-catenin ubiquitination was measured, and catalytic dependence was tested using CASP5C(C173A). APC cleavage was examined with predicted CASP5 sites, recombinant CASP5C, truncated APC substrates, APC(D556A) and APC fragments resembling cleavage products.
+
+## Results
+
+CASP5 showed intestinal epithelial enrichment and inducibility consistent with a role beyond canonical inflammatory-caspase responses. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C. CASP5 increased after Salmonella infection, interferon beta and interferon gamma, whereas lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelium, consistent with low TLR4 expression.
+
+Proximity mapping linked CASP5 to Wnt pathway components. BioID2 analysis identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms emerged from interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL proteins. DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near machinery that controls beta-catenin stability. The interaction involved the DVL DEP domain and CASP5 catalytic domain, although direct binary binding was not fully proven.
+
+Functional assays revealed a striking isoform specificity. CASP5C increased TOPFlash Wnt reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both in the presence and absence of WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not reproduce this effect. CASP5C also increased expression of Wnt-responsive genes including AXIN2 and TCF7 and promoted redistribution of beta-catenin into nuclear and cytosolic compartments. In contrast, CASP5A and CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduce the CASP5C-mediated Wnt response. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce the CASP5C effect, suggesting, without proving, that this activity may reflect a primate CASP5 specialization after gene duplication.
+
+The same isoform specificity was observed in organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. CASP5C increased viable cell numbers, produced organoids approximately 40% larger than controls, increased Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. AXIN2 rose about threefold, TCF7 1.4-fold, MYC 1.3-fold, LGR5 1.2-fold and MKI67 2.4-fold, whereas ALPI and MUC2 fell by about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids also persisted longer after Wnt withdrawal and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored.
+
+Injury-associated experiments further supported a link with repair programs. In irradiated colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC then increased more rapidly in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data indicate that CASP5C can potentiate repair-associated Wnt responses in vitro, without establishing sufficiency or necessity in the human intestine in vivo.
+
+CASP5C altered destruction-complex composition in a manner consistent with beta-catenin stabilization. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Catalytic activity was required, because CASP5C(C173A) lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and Wnt reporter signalling.
+
+APC emerged as a key substrate linking CASP5C activity to Wnt activation. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated largely in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved a truncated APC substrate in vitro. APC D556A removed the major cleavage products. In APC knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the complex and increase Wnt reporter activity more effectively than APC(D556A). APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. D556 therefore appears important, but additional APC cleavage sites may contribute.
+
+Cell-state analyses placed CASP5 isoforms in distinct epithelial contexts. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. However, CASP5c showed a deeper crypt distribution and greater overlap with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and more associated with differentiated CK20-positive cells. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses supported an association of CASP5c with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and of CASP5a and CASP5b with mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased later with differentiation markers.
+
+Isoform-competition experiments supported the functional relevance of these expression patterns. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 staining. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects declined. This result supports a competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C partly by competing for DVL2, thereby limiting the ability of CASP5C to amplify Wnt signalling.
+
+## Discussion
+
+These findings define CASP5C as an epithelial Wnt amplifier rather than a redundant copy of CASP4 or mouse caspase 11. The distinction is important because CASP5 has often been considered through the lens of inflammatory caspase biology. In the tested systems, CASP4 was more consistent with the noncanonical cytosolic-LPS response, whereas CASP5C acted through a separate pathway involving DVL-associated destruction complexes and APC cleavage. CASP5C therefore expands the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteins into epithelial homeostasis and regeneration-associated signalling.
+
+The proposed mechanism is coherent with several independent observations. CASP5 isoforms localize near DVL proteins, CASP5C uniquely activates Wnt reporters, catalytic inactivation abolishes the effect, CASP5C reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination, and APC cleavage products phenocopy aspects of pathway activation. APC D556 contributes substantially to the cleavage pattern and functional output, but the data do not require D556 to be the only relevant site. A restrained interpretation is that CASP5C cleaves APC in a way that weakens destruction-complex function and permits beta-catenin-dependent transcription, with D556 as a major contributor.
+
+The longer CASP5 isoforms appear to have a different role. CASP5A and CASP5B did not activate Wnt signalling in the same way as CASP5C and could restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. Their more differentiated epithelial distribution suggests that isoform balance may help tune Wnt signalling as cells move from proliferative crypt compartments toward mature luminal states. In this model, CASP5c supports early-lineage or transit-amplifying programs, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b provide a counterweight during differentiation.
+
+The disease-associated observations are intriguing but should be interpreted cautiously. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations occurred in about 3% of colon or rectal cancers in the reported analysis, similar to AXIN1, and tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations while being mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison, approximately 68% versus 17%. These associations are compatible with a relationship between CASP5 and Wnt-pathway biology, but they do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.
+
+Overall, the evidence supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C couples epithelial stress and repair contexts to Wnt/beta-catenin amplification through APC cleavage. The work clarifies why CASP5 may have remained difficult to place within conventional inflammasome frameworks and suggests that human CASP5 diversification produced a CARD-less isoform with a specialized epithelial signalling function. Future work will need to determine how CASP5C activity is regulated in vivo, whether APC cleavage occurs during physiological repair, and how isoform balance is altered in chronic inflammation and neoplasia.

+## Discussion
+
+These findings define CASP5C as an epithelial Wnt amplifier rather than a redundant copy of CASP4 or mouse caspase 11. In the tested systems, CASP4 was more consistent with the noncanonical cytosolic-LPS response, whereas CASP5C acted through a separate pathway involving DVL-associated destruction complexes and APC cleavage. CASP5C therefore expands the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteins into epithelial homeostasis and regeneration-associated signalling.
+
+The proposed mechanism is coherent with several independent observations. CASP5 isoforms localize near DVL proteins, CASP5C uniquely activates Wnt reporters, catalytic inactivation abolishes the effect, CASP5C reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination, and APC cleavage products phenocopy aspects of pathway activation. APC D556 contributes substantially to the cleavage pattern and functional output, but the data do not require D556 to be the only relevant site. +
+Overall, the evidence supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C couples epithelial stress and repair contexts to Wnt/beta-catenin amplification through APC cleavage. The work clarifies why CASP5 may have remained difficult to place within conventional inflammasome frameworks and suggests that human CASP5 diversification produced a CARD-less isoform with a specialized epithelial signalling function. Future work will need to determine how CASP5C activity is regulated in vivo, whether APC cleavage occurs during physiological repair, and how isoform balance is altered in chronic inflammation and neoplasia.

+# Caspase 5c amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal homeostasis
+
+## Abstract
+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known as innate immune proteases, yet the biological function of human CASP5 has remained less defined than that of CASP4 or mouse caspase 11. Here, human intestinal epithelial models reveal an isoform-specific role for CASP5C in Wnt/beta-catenin regulation. CASP5 was enriched in intestinal tissues and epithelial organoids, where three isoforms, CASP5A, CASP5B and CARD-lacking CASP5C, were detected. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 or CASP4, increased Wnt reporter activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, induced Wnt target genes and expanded proliferative organoid compartments. CASP5 isoforms associated with Dishevelled proteins through interactions involving the Dishevelled DEP domain and CASP5 catalytic domain. CASP5C enzymatic activity was required for pathway activation and was linked to APC cleavage, with D556 contributing to major cleavage products. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and restrained its signalling effect.
+
+## Introduction
+
+Canonical Wnt/beta-catenin signalling supports intestinal development, crypt maintenance, stem-cell renewal, transit-amplifying proliferation and repair after injury, while requiring tight control to preserve differentiation. A core regulatory feature is the beta-catenin destruction complex, in which APC acts as scaffold and tumour suppressor. Dishevelled proteins link receptor-proximal Wnt events to downstream remodelling of this complex.
+
+Inflammatory caspases are usually considered in the context of host defence and inflammasome-related responses. Human CASP4 resembles mouse caspase 11 by sequence and phylogeny and functions as a principal mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. By contrast, the function of CASP5 has been unclear. Experiments in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages did not support CASP5 as the main executor of the tested noncanonical response, raising the possibility that CASP5 has a distinct function, particularly in tissues where it is preferentially expressed.
+
+The intestine provided a compelling setting to examine this question. CASP5 expression was concentrated in intestinal tract and appendix and was detected in epithelial cells as well as in some monocyte and macrophage populations. In colonic organoids, CASP5 expression exceeded that of CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophage patterns, whereas CASP4 showed broader distribution. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7, 43.2 and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD altogether. CASP5C was not explained as a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B in siRNA experiments.
+
+Here, the evidence supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C amplifies epithelial Wnt/beta-catenin signalling by enzymatically modifying APC-containing destruction complexes. The findings separate CASP5C from CASP5A and CASP5B, distinguish CASP5 from CASP4 in intestinal epithelial biology, and identify a mechanism by which a caspase-family protease may influence epithelial renewal without acting as the primary noncanonical inflammasome protease in the tested systems.
+
+## Methods
+
+Human colon and small-intestinal organoids were used to examine CASP5 isoform expression, inducibility and function. Expression patterns were compared with macrophage-associated inflammatory caspase components and with single-cell transcriptomic patterns across epithelial states. Stimulation conditions included Salmonella, interferon beta, interferon gamma and lipopolysaccharide.
+
+BioID2 and co-immunoprecipitation were used to identify CASP5-associated pathways and test associations among CASP5 isoforms, Dishevelled proteins, AXIN1 and beta-catenin. Domain mapping assessed the DVL DEP domain and CASP5 catalytic domain. Wnt activity was measured using TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T cells, HCT116 cells and organoids, with and without WNT3A. CASP5 isoforms were compared with CASP1, CASP4 and engineered CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms.
+
+Organoid phenotypes were assessed by reporter activity, viability, size, Ki-67, EdU and differentiation-associated markers including CK20, ALPI and MUC2. Wnt withdrawal and re-addition experiments tested persistence and cell-cycle re-entry. Irradiated colonic organoids were used to examine induction of CASP5 isoforms and expression of stem-cell and Wnt-associated genes.
+
+Mechanistic experiments focused on destruction-complex composition and APC cleavage. AXIN1-containing complexes were analysed after Wnt stimulation, beta-catenin ubiquitination was measured, and catalytic dependence was tested using CASP5C(C173A). APC cleavage was examined with predicted CASP5 sites, recombinant CASP5C, truncated APC substrates, APC(D556A) and APC fragments resembling cleavage products.
+
+## Results
+
+CASP5 showed intestinal epithelial enrichment and inducibility consistent with a role beyond canonical inflammatory-caspase responses. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C. CASP5 increased after Salmonella infection, interferon beta and interferon gamma, whereas lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelium, consistent with low TLR4 expression.
+
+Proximity mapping linked CASP5 to Wnt pathway components. BioID2 analysis identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms emerged from interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL proteins. DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near machinery that controls beta-catenin stability. The interaction involved the DVL DEP domain and CASP5 catalytic domain, although direct binary binding was not fully proven.
+
+Functional assays revealed a striking isoform specificity. CASP5C increased TOPFlash Wnt reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both in the presence and absence of WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not reproduce this effect. CASP5C also increased expression of Wnt-responsive genes including AXIN2 and TCF7 and promoted redistribution of beta-catenin into nuclear and cytosolic compartments. In contrast, CASP5A and CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduce the CASP5C-mediated Wnt response. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce the CASP5C effect, suggesting, without proving, that this activity may reflect a primate CASP5 specialization after gene duplication.
+
+The same isoform specificity was observed in organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. CASP5C increased viable cell numbers, produced organoids approximately 40% larger than controls, increased Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. AXIN2 rose about threefold, TCF7 1.4-fold, MYC 1.3-fold, LGR5 1.2-fold and MKI67 2.4-fold, whereas ALPI and MUC2 fell by about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids also persisted longer after Wnt withdrawal and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored.
+
+Injury-associated experiments further supported a link with repair programs. In irradiated colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC then increased more rapidly in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data indicate that CASP5C can potentiate repair-associated Wnt responses in vitro, without establishing sufficiency or necessity in the human intestine in vivo.
+
+CASP5C altered destruction-complex composition in a manner consistent with beta-catenin stabilization. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Catalytic activity was required, because CASP5C(C173A) lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and Wnt reporter signalling.
+
+APC emerged as a key substrate linking CASP5C activity to Wnt activation. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated largely in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved a truncated APC substrate in vitro. APC D556A removed the major cleavage products. In APC knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the complex and increase Wnt reporter activity more effectively than APC(D556A). APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. D556 therefore appears important, but additional APC cleavage sites may contribute.
+
+Cell-state analyses placed CASP5 isoforms in distinct epithelial contexts. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. However, CASP5c showed a deeper crypt distribution and greater overlap with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and more associated with differentiated CK20-positive cells. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses supported an association of CASP5c with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and of CASP5a and CASP5b with mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased later with differentiation markers.
+
+Isoform-competition experiments supported the functional relevance of these expression patterns. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 staining. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects declined. This result supports a competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C partly by competing for DVL2, thereby limiting the ability of CASP5C to amplify Wnt signalling.
+
+## Discussion
+
+These findings define CASP5C as an epithelial Wnt amplifier rather than a redundant copy of CASP4 or mouse caspase 11. The distinction is important because CASP5 has often been considered through the lens of inflammatory caspase biology. In the tested systems, CASP4 was more consistent with the noncanonical cytosolic-LPS response, whereas CASP5C acted through a separate pathway involving DVL-associated destruction complexes and APC cleavage. CASP5C therefore expands the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteins into epithelial homeostasis and regeneration-associated signalling.
+
+The proposed mechanism is coherent with several independent observations. CASP5 isoforms localize near DVL proteins, CASP5C uniquely activates Wnt reporters, catalytic inactivation abolishes the effect, CASP5C reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination, and APC cleavage products phenocopy aspects of pathway activation. APC D556 contributes substantially to the cleavage pattern and functional output, but the data do not require D556 to be the only relevant site. A restrained interpretation is that CASP5C cleaves APC in a way that weakens destruction-complex function and permits beta-catenin-dependent transcription, with D556 as a major contributor.
+
+The longer CASP5 isoforms appear to have a different role. CASP5A and CASP5B did not activate Wnt signalling in the same way as CASP5C and could restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. Their more differentiated epithelial distribution suggests that isoform balance may help tune Wnt signalling as cells move from proliferative crypt compartments toward mature luminal states. In this model, CASP5c supports early-lineage or transit-amplifying programs, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b provide a counterweight during differentiation.
+
+The disease-associated observations are intriguing but should be interpreted cautiously. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations occurred in about 3% of colon or rectal cancers, similar to AXIN1, and tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations while being mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison, approximately 68% versus 17%. These associations do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.
+
+Overall, the evidence supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C couples epithelial stress and repair contexts to Wnt/beta-catenin amplification through APC cleavage. The work clarifies why CASP5 may have remained difficult to place within conventional inflammasome frameworks and suggests that human CASP5 diversification produced a CARD-less isoform with a specialized epithelial signalling function. Future work will need to determine how CASP5C activity is regulated in vivo, whether APC cleavage occurs during physiological repair, and how isoform balance is altered in chronic inflammation and neoplasia.
