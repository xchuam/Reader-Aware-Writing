# Source Notes

These notes are the source packet for one article. They are not arranged in
article order. Some wording is rough, some items repeat, and some transitions

- CASP5 is one of the inflammatory caspase-family proteases. It is similar to
  CASP4 and to mouse caspase 11, but what CASP5 actually does has not been
  very clear.
- Human CASP4 looked more like mouse caspase 11 in sequence/phylogeny and was
  the main actor in the tested noncanonical inflammasome response to cytosolic
  LPS and bacteria. CASP5 did not do this job in the experiments with human
  intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages.
- So the question was maybe CASP5 has another function, esp in intestine.
  CASP5 expression was more concentrated in intestinal tract/appendix and in
  epithelial cells plus some monocyte/macrophage populations.
- In colonic organoids CASP5 was higher than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD compared
  with macrophages. CASP4 was broader.
- Organoids from human colon and small intestine expressed three isoforms:
  CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP5C. Approx sizes: 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa, 33.3 kDa.
- CASP5B is missing part of the CARD region. CASP5C has no CARD. It was not
  just a cleavage product of CASP5A/B according to siRNA experiments.
- CASP5 increased after Salmonella, IFN-beta, IFN-gamma in organoids, but LPS
  alone did not raise it much in epithelium, probably because TLR4 low.
- BioID2 in human colonic organoids found DVL1, DVL2, DVL3 near CASP5.
  Wnt-related terms appeared in the interactor analysis.
- CASP5 isoforms co-immunoprecipitated with DVL1/2/3. DVL2 complexes also had
  AXIN1 and beta-catenin. The binding involved DVL DEP domain and CASP5
  catalytic domain, maybe direct but not fully proven.
- Wnt/beta-catenin controls intestinal development, repair, stem-cell renewal
  and differentiation. DVL connects Wnt receptor events to downstream beta
  catenin control. APC is scaffold/tumor suppressor in the destruction complex.
- TOPFlash assays: CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116
  cells, with or without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1, CASP4 did not do the
  same.
- CASP5C increased AXIN2 and TCF7 and moved beta-catenin into nucleus/cytosol.
  CASP5A or CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduce this effect.
- Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not copy the Wnt effect, so maybe
  this is primate CASP5 specialization after duplication. Do not over say this.
- In organoids, CASP5C but not CASP5A/B increased Wnt reporter activity.
  CASP5C made more viable cells, around 40% bigger organoids, more Ki-67 and
  EdU positive cells, fewer CK20 enterocytes.
- CASP5C increased some Wnt/proliferation markers in colonic organoids:
  AXIN2 about 3-fold, TCF7 1.4-fold, MYC 1.3-fold, LGR5 1.2-fold, MKI67

  cycle after Wnt came back. This sounds like it helps repair but do not say
  proven in human body.

# Reader Aware Writing

## Overview

Use this skill to make writing land with the person who will read it. The core move is to build a lightweight reader model, revise around that reader's job, and preserve truth while improving clarity, cohesion, trust, and actionability.

For scientific writing, treat reader-centeredness as a form of rigor: the text should prepare the reader for each concept, make the argument's logic visible, distinguish evidence from interpretation, and avoid forcing the reader to infer missing links.

## Workflow

1. Identify the reader and their job.
   - Capture the reader role, expertise level, purpose for reading, decision or action needed, likely constraints, objections, and desired tone.
   - If the reader is unclear, infer a plausible reader from the artifact and state the assumption briefly. Ask only when the missing reader changes the work materially.
   - For scientific articles, build a compact reader contract before drafting:
     reader role, what the reader already knows, what they likely do not know, the reader's first question, the reader's likely confusion, and the article promise.
   - For complex or high-stakes writing, read [reader-model.md](references/reader-model.md).

2. Choose the work mode and artifact type.
   - Compose: create new writing from goals and context.
   - Revise: improve an existing draft while preserving meaning.
   - Critique: diagnose where the text mismatches the reader.
   - Transform: adapt one artifact for another audience or channel.
   - Compare: explain how multiple reader versions differ.
   - For abstracts, introductions, results, discussions, or full manuscripts, read [scientific-article.md](references/scientific-article.md).

3. Build the reader path before drafting.
   - Privately map each paragraph or section to the reader question it answers.
   - Sequence those questions so each answer prepares the next question.
   - Identify any concept, method, variable, result, or implication that needs a bridge before it appears.
   - For scientific articles, make the paragraph path explicit enough to test: each paragraph should have one reader question, one governing answer, and one handoff to the next reader question.
   - If two paragraphs answer the same reader question, merge or sharpen their roles. If one paragraph answers two unrelated questions, split it.
   - Omit this planning map from the final answer unless the user asks for reasoning or critique.

4. Make the reader path obvious.
   - Start with what the reader needs to know, decide, or do.
   - Put context before details, but do not bury the point.
   - Match density to expertise: define terms for newcomers, compress basics for experts, and expose assumptions for decision-makers.
   - Replace author-centered phrasing with reader-centered stakes, actions, and consequences.
   - Preserve required facts, caveats, citations, constraints, and the user's intended voice.
   - For academic introductions, paragraphs, cohesion, or coherence, read [academic-writing.md](references/academic-writing.md) and [cohesion-coherence.md](references/cohesion-coherence.md).

5. Revise for trust.
   - Separate facts from interpretation.
   - Name uncertainty without over-apologizing.
   - Prefer concrete claims, examples, and decision criteria over vague reassurance.
   - Remove jargon unless it is native to the reader's working vocabulary.
   - Avoid inventing evidence, metrics, policies, or citations.
   - For scientific articles, silently classify important statements on a claim ladder: observation, result, mechanistic evidence, model-supported interpretation, implication, speculation, and forbidden upgrade. Downgrade or remove any statement that exceeds the supplied evidence.
   - For sentence-level scientific clarity, active/passive choices, nominalization, modifiers, parallelism, and punctuation, read [scientific-style.md](references/scientific-style.md).

6. Finish with a reader check.
   - Use [revision-rubric.md](references/revision-rubric.md) for substantive revisions, high-stakes writing, or explicit review requests.
   - For scientific article drafting, run the paragraph gates before returning: orientation, unity, bridge, evidence/interpretation order, exit handoff, and claim strength.
   - If useful, provide a concise change note explaining the reader-specific choices.

## Scientific Article Operating Protocol

Use this protocol when composing a scientific article from notes, a dossier, a paper, or experimental results. Keep the protocol hidden unless the user requests an audit or reasoning.

1. Reader contract.
   - Reader role: who is reading and why.
   - Already knows: concepts the reader can be expected to know.
   - Likely gaps: concepts, methods, organisms, variables, or stakes that need preparation.
   - First question: what the reader needs answered before details matter.
   - Likely confusion: where the reader may lose the thread.
   - Article promise: what the article will make clear without exceeding the evidence.

2. Paragraph-question path.
   - Draft a one-line question for each planned paragraph or section.
   - Put the questions in the order the reader should ask them.
   - Ensure each answer prepares the next question.
   - Do not include a paragraph whose reader question is unclear.

3. Claim ladder.
   - Mark major claims internally as observation, result, mechanistic evidence, model-supported interpretation, implication, speculation, or forbidden upgrade.
   - Keep observations and results close to their evidence.
   - Signal model-supported interpretations with bounded language.
   - Remove forbidden upgrades: causal, clinical, mechanistic, translational, or novelty claims not supported by the source.

4. Paragraph gates.
   - Orientation: the first sentence tells the reader why this paragraph exists.
   - Unity: every sentence serves the same reader question.
   - Bridge: new concepts, methods, variables, subgroups, mechanisms, and limitations are introduced before they are used.
   - Evidence order: evidence appears before interpretation when the reader needs proof; interpretation appears before details when the reader first needs the point.
   - Handoff: the final sentence closes the local point or prepares the next paragraph.
   - Term discipline: repeat stable technical terms until the reader has a firm thread; avoid premature synonym swaps.

5. Final reader-path pass.
   - Read only the first sentence of each paragraph. They should form a coherent mini-outline.
   - Read only the final sentence of each paragraph. The handoffs should not feel abrupt.
   - Check that the central claim can be stated in one sentence and that every section serves it.
   - Return the article only unless the user asked for the hidden reader contract, paragraph path, or audit table.

## Reader-First Rules

Use these rules as defaults when drafting or revising:

- No unexplained novelty: introduce a new idea only after it has been named, implied, or explicitly motivated.
- One paragraph, one reader question: each paragraph should answer or develop one question the reader has at that point.
- Old before new: begin sentences with familiar information when possible; place new, complex, or emphasized information later.
- Signpost structural shifts: mark contrasts, turns, implications, changes of scale, and moves from evidence to interpretation.
- Handoff between sections: end or begin sections with enough shared language that the reader understands why the next section follows.
- Repeat key terms before varying them: premature synonym use can break the reader's thread.
- Do not simplify by default: expert readers may need more precision, stronger caveats, or clearer methods rather than easier vocabulary.

## Output Rules

- When the user asks for rewritten text, lead with the rewritten text.
- When the user asks for critique, lead with the highest-impact reader mismatches.
- When the user asks for multiple audiences, label each version by reader and keep the differences intentional.
- Keep meta commentary short unless the user asks for reasoning.
- Do not turn every edit into a generic simplification; reader-aware writing may become more technical, more direct, warmer, shorter, or more detailed depending on the reader.
- For scientific revisions, do not invent data, methods, results, citations, mechanisms, limitations, or implications. If evidence is missing, mark the gap or phrase the claim conditionally.

# Scientific Article Writing

Use this reference for abstracts, introductions, results, discussions, full scientific manuscripts, journal-facing revisions, and critiques of article logic.

## Article-Level Reader Path

Before drafting or revising, identify the reader's question at each level:

- Title: Is this article relevant to my problem or field?
- Abstract: Should I read the full paper, cite it, review it, or act on it?
- Introduction: What problem matters, what is missing, and what does this study contribute?
- Methods: Could I trust, evaluate, or reproduce what was done?
- Results: What was found, and which findings answer the research question?
- Discussion: What do the findings mean, how far do they generalize, and what remains uncertain?
- Conclusion: What should the reader remember or do with this knowledge?

## Drafting Protocol

For a composed article or article section, use this protocol before writing:

1. Build a hidden reader contract:
   - Reader role and purpose.
   - What the reader already knows.
   - What the reader likely does not know.
   - The reader's first question.
   - The reader's likely confusion.
   - The article promise.
2. Write a hidden reader-question outline for the requested sections.
3. Put each paragraph under one question only.
4. Check that each paragraph's first sentence gives the reader familiar context or a clear frame.
5. Check that each paragraph's final sentence advances the article toward the next reader question.
6. Add a bridge before any new variable, method, subgroup, mechanism, limitation, or implication that has not been prepared.
7. Run the claim ladder and paragraph gates before finalizing.
8. Remove the outline from the final output unless the user asks to see the reasoning.

Strong scientific prose should feel like a sequence of prepared questions, not a container of facts.

## Reader Contract Template

Use this compact template internally before drafting:

```text
Reader:
Already knows:
Likely gaps:
First question:
Likely confusion:
Article promise:
```

Example:

```text
Reader: life-science researcher outside this exact subfield
Already knows: Wnt/beta-catenin, epithelial organoids, inflammatory caspases
Likely gaps: why CASP5C differs from CASP4 and CASP5A/B
First question: what is the central claim and why should I trust it?
Likely confusion: how an inflammatory caspase connects mechanistically to Wnt
Article promise: explain the CASP5C-APC mechanism without overclaiming disease relevance
```

## Claim Ladder

Use the claim ladder to keep scientific trust visible:

- Observation: what was seen or measured.
- Result: a specific finding from the supplied study.
- Mechanistic evidence: evidence that supports how the result may occur.
- Model-supported interpretation: what the evidence supports in the tested system.
- Implication: what may matter if the interpretation holds.
- Speculation: a possible extension that must be marked as unproven.
- Forbidden upgrade: any causal, clinical, mechanistic, translational, or novelty claim not supported by the source.

Before finalizing, downgrade or remove forbidden upgrades. Do not let a result in cells or organoids silently become a claim about human disease, clinical use, or in vivo causality.

## Paragraph Gates

Each paragraph in a drafted article should pass these gates:

- Orientation: the first sentence tells the reader why this paragraph exists.
- Unity: the paragraph answers one reader question.
- Bridge: new concepts, methods, variables, subgroups, mechanisms, and limitations are introduced before use.
- Evidence order: evidence appears before interpretation when the reader needs proof; interpretation appears before details when the reader first needs the point.
- Handoff: the final sentence closes the local point or prepares the next paragraph.
- Term discipline: key terms are repeated until the reader has a stable thread.

## Abstracts

A reader-centered abstract lets a busy expert decide quickly whether the article is relevant and credible.

- Include the problem or context, the gap or purpose, the approach, the key result, and the main implication.
- Make the research question or objective explicit.
- Give concrete findings when available; avoid vague claims such as "important implications" without naming the implication.
- Keep background brief. The abstract should not become a miniature introduction.
- Avoid overclaiming beyond the design, data, population, or method.
- For structured abstracts, respect the required headings and put each move under the heading where the reader expects it.

## Introductions

Use a three-move structure when the field or journal does not require another pattern:

1. Establish the territory.
   - Orient the reader to the research area and its importance.
   - Review only the literature needed to make the problem intelligible.
   - Keep the chain of prior work visible instead of listing studies without a point.

2. Establish the niche.
   - Identify the gap, unresolved tension, limitation, extension, or unanswered question.
   - Make the missing link explicit before presenting the study aim.
   - Use contrast language carefully: "however", "despite", "although", "few studies", or field-appropriate equivalents.

3. Occupy the niche.
   - State the aim, research question, hypothesis, or objective.
   - Preview the contribution, approach, or article structure only when it helps the reader.
   - Do not imply novelty or importance that the manuscript cannot support.

## Methods

Methods sections should let readers evaluate trustworthiness and reproducibility.

- Order procedures in the sequence the reader needs to understand or replicate them.
- Define materials, participants, datasets, instruments, variables, and analytic choices before using them.
- Keep passive voice when the procedure matters more than the actor; use active voice when responsibility or choice matters.
- Explain non-obvious exclusions, thresholds, transformations, controls, and statistical choices.
- Avoid burying important constraints in long noun strings or dense parentheticals.
- End or begin the Methods with language that links the design choices to the Results the reader is about to see.

## Results

The Results section answers: What were the findings?

- Highlight important findings and observations that answer the research question.
- Use text to guide the reader through tables and figures; do not merely repeat every number.
- Present results in a deliberate order: most important to least important, least important building to most important, or simple findings before complex findings.
- Keep interpretation out of traditional Results sections unless the field, journal, or combined Results/Discussion format expects it.
- Use past tense for completed findings and present tense when referring to what a table or figure contains.
- Refer to every table and figure in the text, and ensure captions can stand alone.
- When presenting multiple findings, make the relationship among them visible: main finding, supporting finding, subgroup pattern, exception, or uncertainty.

## Discussion

The Discussion section answers: What do the findings mean?

- Start by stating the principal finding or answer to the research question, not by repeating all results.
- Interpret the findings in relation to the study aim, prior literature, mechanisms, theory, or practice.
- Explain unexpected findings instead of hiding them.
- Name limitations that affect interpretation, generalization, measurement, bias, or uncertainty.
- Separate supported implications from speculation.
- End with the strongest defensible takeaway, not a broader claim than the evidence allows.

## Section Handoffs

Use handoffs to preserve coherence across sections:

- Introduction to Methods: connect the research question to the design or comparison used to answer it.
- Methods to Results: connect the measured variables or comparisons to the order of findings.
- Results to Discussion: restate the principal finding as the basis for interpretation, then move to meaning or limits.
- Discussion to Conclusion: narrow the implication to what the evidence can support.

+# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models
+
+## Abstract
+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known for their roles in innate immune sensing, but the function of human CASP5 has remained less clear than that of CASP4 and mouse caspase 11. These findings support a model in which a primate CASP5 isoform amplifies Wnt signalling in intestinal epithelial systems by modifying the beta-catenin destruction complex, while leaving open how this pathway operates in vivo and in disease.
+
+## Introduction
+
+Human CASP5 belongs to the inflammatory caspase family, a group of proteases that also includes CASP4 and the mouse orthologue-like caspase 11. CASP4 and caspase 11 are well established as effectors of noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and intracellular bacteria. CASP5 is related to these proteins, but its biological role has been less settled. In the systems examined here, human CASP4 more closely resembled mouse caspase 11 in sequence and phylogenetic analyses and acted as the principal mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses, whereas CASP5 did not perform that role in human intestinal epithelial cells or macrophages. This separation raised a basic question: if CASP5 is not simply a redundant noncanonical inflammasome caspase, what does it do?
+
+The intestine provided a plausible context for this question because CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and was detected in epithelial cells as well as some monocyte and macrophage populations. In human colonic organoids, CASP5 was expressed at higher levels than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed a broader distribution. CASP5 expression also increased in organoids after Salmonella infection and after exposure to interferon-beta or interferon-gamma. Lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelial organoids, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 expression. These observations suggested that intestinal epithelial CASP5 might have a function distinct from canonical inflammatory caspase responses.
+
+Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is a central regulator of intestinal development, epithelial repair, stem-cell renewal and differentiation. Dishevelled proteins connect Wnt receptor activation to downstream control of beta-catenin, whereas APC acts as a tumour-suppressive scaffold within the beta-catenin destruction complex. Because intestinal epithelial homeostasis depends on the balance between Wnt-driven proliferation and differentiation, any epithelial CASP5 activity at this pathway would be biologically important. We therefore asked whether CASP5 isoforms interact with Wnt pathway components and whether any isoform changes beta-catenin signalling in intestinal epithelial models.
+
+## Experimental approach
+
+The study combined human tissue and cell-expression analyses, intestinal organoid models, proteomic interaction mapping, biochemical assays and Wnt reporter systems. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids were used to define CASP5 isoform expression and epithelial responses. BioID2 proximity labelling in human colonic organoids identified proteins near CASP5, and co-immunoprecipitation tested physical association with candidate pathway components. Wnt pathway activity was measured with beta-catenin-dependent reporter assays in HEK293T, HCT116 and organoid systems, with and without WNT3A stimulation where appropriate. Organoid viability, size, proliferation and differentiation were assessed using ATP, Ki-67, EdU and lineage-marker measurements. Mechanistic tests used catalytic mutants, CARD deletions, APC knockout cells, APC cleavage-site mutations, APC fragment expression and in vitro cleavage of recombinant APC substrates.
+
+This design allowed three related questions to be separated. First, which CASP5 isoform is present in intestinal epithelial cells? Second, does any isoform alter Wnt/beta-catenin signalling? Third, if CASP5C alters Wnt signalling, does the effect depend on catalytic activity and the destruction-complex scaffold APC?
+
+## Results
+
+Human intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacked part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacked the CARD entirely. Small interfering RNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B, supporting its treatment as a distinct isoform. This isoform structure was important because the absence of the CARD domain correlated with a functional behaviour not shared by the longer isoforms.
+
+Proximity labelling provided the first link between CASP5 and Wnt signalling. BioID2 in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched in the interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. DVL2-containing complexes also included AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing the CASP5-associated complex in the vicinity of the beta-catenin destruction machinery. Domain mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain in the association, although the available evidence does not by itself prove direct binding between purified proteins.
+
+Functional assays showed that Wnt amplification was specific to CASP5C. In TOPFlash reporter assays, CASP5C increased Wnt/beta-catenin reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both with and without WNT3A stimulation. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same response. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and shifted beta-catenin into the nuclear and cytosolic fractions. CARD-less forms of mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce this Wnt effect, suggesting that CASP5C may represent a primate-specialized activity after gene duplication, although that evolutionary interpretation remains bounded by the tested comparisons.
+
+The isoform specificity was also evident in human intestinal organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. Organoids expressing CASP5C contained more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger, and showed increased Ki-67 and EdU positivity, indicating greater proliferative activity in this model. CASP5C also reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. At the transcript level, CASP5C increased Wnt and proliferation-associated markers, including AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated ALPI and MUC2 decreased by about 57% and 53%, respectively. Together, these results indicate that CASP5C favours a Wnt-high, proliferative organoid state rather than a differentiated epithelial state.
+
+CASP5C also affected organoid behaviour during Wnt withdrawal and restoration. CASP5C-expressing organoids maintained growth for longer in the absence of Wnt and re-entered the cell cycle more readily when Wnt was restored. In irradiated human colonic organoids, the CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC rose faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data are consistent with a role for CASP5C in epithelial regenerative programs in organoid systems, but they do not establish that CASP5C is required for repair in the human intestine in vivo.
+
+Single-cell and differentiation analyses helped place the isoforms along epithelial maturation trajectories. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell RNA-sequencing analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. Within that broader pattern, CASP5c was detected deeper in crypt regions and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than did CASP5a or CASP5b. CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and more associated with differentiated or CK20-positive states. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses similarly linked CASP5c to early-lineage or transit-amplifying populations and CASP5a/b to mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b peaked later with differentiation markers.
+
+The longer CASP5 isoforms did not merely lack activity; they could restrain CASP5C. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduced CASP5C-dependent beta-catenin signalling. Consistent with this competition model, knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while retaining CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. Thus, the balance among CASP5 isoforms, rather than total CASP5 expression alone, appears to determine the direction of epithelial Wnt output.
+
+Mechanistic experiments connected CASP5C activity to the beta-catenin destruction complex. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced the amounts of DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. These findings indicate that CASP5C requires catalytic activity and that the CARD-containing N-terminal regions of the longer isoforms may limit this activity.
+

Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary cleavage site, although other APC sites may also contribute. APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. These results support a model in which CASP5C cleaves APC and thereby attenuates destruction-complex control of beta-catenin.
+
+## Discussion
+
+This study identifies an epithelial, isoform-specific function for human CASP5 that is distinct from the tested noncanonical inflammasome role of CASP4. CASP5C, the CARD-less isoform, amplified Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in human cell and organoid systems, increased proliferative organoid features and reduced differentiation markers. The activity required the catalytic cysteine and was linked mechanistically to APC cleavage and altered composition of AXIN1-containing destruction-complex assemblies. CASP5A and CASP5B, by contrast, did not amplify Wnt in the same assays and could restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. The central conclusion is therefore not that CASP5 generally promotes Wnt signalling, but that CASP5C can do so in a manner shaped by isoform balance.
+
+The data also clarify why intestinal context matters for CASP5 biology. CASP5 expression was enriched in intestinal epithelial settings, induced by infection-associated and interferon signals, and strongly increased after irradiation in organoids. CASP5c was associated with crypt-like, MKI67-positive and AXIN2-positive compartments more than the longer isoforms, whereas CASP5a/b aligned more with differentiating states. These patterns fit the functional assays in which CASP5C supported a proliferative, Wnt-responsive organoid state. At the same time, the single-cell data were not a simple stem-cell expression pattern, because total CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one analysis. CASP5C may therefore act most prominently in transit-amplifying or early-lineage epithelial states rather than in the most canonical LGR5-positive stem-cell compartment.
+
+The proposed mechanism places CASP5C upstream of beta-catenin stabilization through proteolytic modification of APC. APC has a central scaffolding role in the destruction complex, and CASP5C-dependent APC cleavage provides a plausible route by which beta-catenin ubiquitination and destruction-complex association were reduced. The APC D556A experiments identify D556 as an important site for the major observed cleavage products, but they do not exclude additional APC cleavage sites or parallel contributions from other complex components. Similarly, the DVL interaction explains how CASP5 isoforms may be recruited near Wnt regulatory machinery, while the directness and structural details of the DVL-CASP5 interaction remain to be fully resolved.
+
+The disease-related observations are best interpreted as hypotheses-generating associations. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while preserving their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations appeared in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers in the reported analysis, a frequency similar to AXIN1, and tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations while being mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison. These findings are consistent with a relationship between CASP5 alterations and Wnt-pathway states, but they do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they establish CASP5C as a validated therapeutic target.
+
+Overall, the findings expand the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteins by assigning CASP5C a noncanonical role in epithelial Wnt regulation. In human intestinal organoids and engineered cell systems, CASP5C cleaves APC, weakens destruction-complex control of beta-catenin and promotes a proliferative Wnt-responsive state. The work leaves important next questions: how CASP5 isoform balance is regulated in vivo, whether CASP5C is required for epithelial repair in intact tissue, and how CASP5 mutations influence Wnt-pathway behaviour in colorectal disease. The strongest supported takeaway is that CASP5C is a distinct CASP5 isoform with the capacity to amplify Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models.

+

+# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models
+
+## Abstract
+
+Inflammatory caspases are best known for innate immune sensing, but the function of human CASP5 has remained less clear than that of CASP4 and mouse caspase 11. Here, we define an isoform-specific activity of CASP5 in intestinal epithelial models. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and the CARD-less CASP5C. CASP5 expression was enriched in intestinal tissues and epithelial populations, and CASP5C was induced strongly after epithelial injury. Proteomic and biochemical analyses linked CASP5 isoforms to Dishevelled proteins. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 or CASP4, increased beta-catenin-dependent reporter activity, promoted Wnt and proliferation-associated genes, expanded organoid growth, and reduced differentiation markers. CASP5C required its catalytic cysteine and cleaved APC, with D556 contributing to a major cleavage event. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and restrained its signalling effect. These findings support a model in which CASP5C amplifies Wnt signalling in intestinal epithelial systems by modifying the beta-catenin destruction complex.
+
+## Introduction
+
+Human CASP5 belongs to the inflammatory caspase family, which also includes CASP4 and the mouse orthologue-like caspase 11. CASP4 and caspase 11 are established effectors of noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and intracellular bacteria. CASP5 is related to these proteins, but its biological role has been less settled. In the systems examined here, human CASP4 more closely resembled mouse caspase 11 in sequence and phylogenetic analyses and acted as the principal mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses, whereas CASP5 did not perform that role in human intestinal epithelial cells or macrophages. This separation raised a basic question: if CASP5 is not simply a redundant noncanonical inflammasome caspase, what does it do?
+
+The intestine provided a plausible context for this question because CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and was detected in epithelial cells as well as some monocyte and macrophage populations. In human colonic organoids, CASP5 was expressed at higher levels than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed a broader distribution. CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella infection and after interferon-beta or interferon-gamma, while lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelial organoids, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 expression. These observations suggested that intestinal epithelial CASP5 might have a function distinct from canonical inflammatory caspase responses.
+
+Wnt/beta-catenin signalling regulates intestinal development, epithelial repair, stem-cell renewal and differentiation. Dishevelled proteins connect Wnt receptor activation to downstream beta-catenin control, whereas APC acts as a tumour-suppressive scaffold within the destruction complex. Because epithelial homeostasis depends on the balance between Wnt-driven proliferation and differentiation, we asked whether CASP5 isoforms interact with Wnt pathway components and whether any isoform changes beta-catenin signalling in intestinal epithelial models.
+
+## Experimental approach
+
+The study combined human tissue and cell-expression analyses, intestinal organoid models, proteomic interaction mapping, biochemical assays and Wnt reporter systems. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids defined CASP5 isoform expression and epithelial responses. BioID2 proximity labelling identified proteins near CASP5, and co-immunoprecipitation tested association with candidate pathway components. Wnt activity was measured with beta-catenin-dependent reporter assays in HEK293T, HCT116 and organoid systems. Organoid viability, size, proliferation and differentiation were assessed using ATP, Ki-67, EdU and lineage-marker measurements. Mechanistic tests used catalytic mutants, CARD deletions, APC knockout cells, APC cleavage-site mutations, APC fragments and in vitro cleavage of recombinant APC substrates.
+
+## Results
+
+Human intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacked part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacked the CARD entirely. Small interfering RNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B, supporting its treatment as a distinct isoform. This isoform structure was important because the absence of the CARD domain correlated with a functional behaviour not shared by the longer isoforms.
+
+Proximity labelling provided the first link between CASP5 and Wnt signalling. BioID2 in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched in the interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. DVL2 complexes also included AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near the beta-catenin destruction machinery. Domain mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although the evidence does not by itself prove direct binding between purified proteins.
+
+Functional assays showed that Wnt amplification was specific to CASP5C. In TOPFlash reporter assays, CASP5C increased Wnt/beta-catenin reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both with and without WNT3A stimulation. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same response. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and shifted beta-catenin into the nuclear and cytosolic fractions. CARD-less forms of mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce this Wnt effect, suggesting that CASP5C may represent a primate-specialized activity after gene duplication, although that evolutionary interpretation remains bounded by the tested comparisons.
+
+The isoform specificity was also evident in human intestinal organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. Organoids expressing CASP5C contained more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger, and showed increased Ki-67 and EdU positivity. CASP5C also reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. At the transcript level, CASP5C increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold, whereas ALPI and MUC2 decreased by about 57% and 53%. Together, these results indicate that CASP5C favours a Wnt-high, proliferative organoid state rather than a differentiated epithelial state.
+
+CASP5C also affected organoid behaviour during Wnt withdrawal and restoration. CASP5C-expressing organoids maintained growth for longer without Wnt and re-entered the cell cycle more readily when Wnt was restored. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC rose faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data are consistent with a role for CASP5C in epithelial regenerative programs in organoid systems, but they do not establish that CASP5C is required for repair in vivo.
+
+Single-cell and differentiation analyses placed the isoforms along epithelial maturation trajectories. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell RNA-sequencing analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. Within that pattern, CASP5c was detected deeper in crypt regions and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than did CASP5a or CASP5b. CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated or CK20-positive states. Pseudotime, CIBERSORTx and Wnt-withdrawal differentiation analyses similarly linked CASP5c to early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and CASP5a/b to mature or differentiating states.
+
+The longer CASP5 isoforms did not merely lack activity; they could restrain CASP5C. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduced CASP5C-dependent beta-catenin signalling. Consistent with this model, knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while retaining CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. Thus, isoform balance, rather than total CASP5 expression alone, appears to shape epithelial Wnt output.
+
+Mechanistic experiments connected CASP5C activity to the beta-catenin destruction complex. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. A catalytic mutant, CASP5C(C173A), lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C also had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and Wnt reporter signalling. These findings indicate that CASP5C requires catalytic activity and that CARD-containing N-terminal regions may limit this activity.
+
+APC emerged as a key substrate linking CASP5C catalysis to Wnt amplification. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC were concentrated mainly in the N-terminal region. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary site, although other APC sites may also contribute. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex assembly and increase Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) weakened these effects. APC fragments resembling cleavage products also reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. These results support a model in which CASP5C cleaves APC and attenuates destruction-complex control of beta-catenin.
+
+## Discussion
+
+This study identifies an epithelial, isoform-specific function for human CASP5 that is distinct from the tested noncanonical inflammasome role of CASP4. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary site, although other APC sites may also contribute. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex assembly and increase Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) weakened these effects. APC fragments resembling cleavage products also reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. These results support a model in which CASP5C cleaves APC and attenuates destruction-complex control of beta-catenin.
+
+## Discussion
+
+This study identifies an epithelial, isoform-specific function for human CASP5 that is distinct from the tested noncanonical inflammasome role of CASP4. CASP5C amplified Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in human cell and organoid systems, increased proliferative organoid features and reduced differentiation markers. The activity required the catalytic cysteine and was linked to APC cleavage and altered AXIN1-containing destruction-complex assemblies. CASP5A and CASP5B, by contrast, did not amplify Wnt and could restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. The central conclusion is therefore not that CASP5 generally promotes Wnt signalling, but that CASP5C can do so in a manner shaped by isoform balance.
+
+The data also clarify why intestinal context matters for CASP5 biology. CASP5 expression was enriched in intestinal epithelial settings, induced by infection-associated and interferon signals, and strongly increased after irradiation in organoids. CASP5c aligned with crypt-like, MKI67-positive and AXIN2-positive compartments more than the longer isoforms, whereas CASP5a/b aligned more with differentiating states. Because total CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one analysis, CASP5C may act most prominently in transit-amplifying or early-lineage epithelial states rather than in the most canonical LGR5-positive stem-cell compartment.
+
+The proposed mechanism places CASP5C upstream of beta-catenin stabilization through APC cleavage. APC has a central scaffolding role in the destruction complex, and CASP5C-dependent APC cleavage provides a plausible route by which beta-catenin ubiquitination and destruction-complex association were reduced. The APC D556A experiments identify D556 as important for the major observed cleavage products, but they do not exclude additional APC cleavage sites or parallel contributions from other complex components. The DVL interaction may recruit CASP5 isoforms near Wnt regulatory machinery, although its directness and structural details remain unresolved.
+
+The disease-related observations are best interpreted as hypotheses-generating associations. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while preserving their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations appeared in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations, and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison. These findings are consistent with a relationship between CASP5 alterations and Wnt-pathway states, but they do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they establish CASP5C as a validated therapeutic target.
+
+Overall, the findings expand the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteins by assigning CASP5C a noncanonical role in epithelial Wnt regulation. In human intestinal organoids and engineered cell systems, CASP5C cleaves APC, weakens destruction-complex control of beta-catenin and promotes a proliferative Wnt-responsive state. The work leaves important next questions about in vivo isoform regulation, epithelial repair and colorectal disease. The strongest supported takeaway is that CASP5C can amplify Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models.
