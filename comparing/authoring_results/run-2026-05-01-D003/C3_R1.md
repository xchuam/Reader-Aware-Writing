# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in human intestinal epithelial cells

## Abstract

Inflammatory caspases are best known as innate immune proteases, but the functions of human caspase 5 (CASP5) have remained less defined than those of CASP4 and mouse caspase 11. Here, evidence from human intestinal epithelial models identifies the CARD-less isoform CASP5C as a positive regulator of Wnt/beta-catenin signalling. CASP5 was enriched in the intestinal tract and appendix, and human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C. In contrast to CASP4, CASP5 was not the main executor of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide or bacteria. Instead, proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation linked CASP5 isoforms to Dishevelled proteins. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 or CASP4, increased Wnt reporter activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution and expanded proliferative organoid states. Mechanistically, CASP5C catalytic activity enabled cleavage of adenomatous polyposis coli protein (APC), reducing beta-catenin association with destruction-complex components and dampening beta-catenin ubiquitination. CASP5A and CASP5B restrained this activity by competing for DVL binding. These data support a model in which CASP5C couples inflammatory-caspase diversification to epithelial Wnt tuning.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases link microbial or danger sensing to innate immune effector programs. Human CASP4 and CASP5 are related to mouse caspase 11, but their biological roles are not equivalent. Sequence and phylogenetic comparisons place CASP4 closer to mouse caspase 11, and functional experiments in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages identified CASP4 as the principal mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and intracellular bacteria. CASP5 did not perform this role in those settings, leaving open the possibility that it has diverged toward a distinct function.

The intestinal epithelium is a plausible site for such specialization. CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix, with expression in epithelial compartments and subsets of monocytes and macrophages. In human colonic organoids, CASP5 was higher relative to CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD than in macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed broader distribution. These observations suggested that CASP5 might act in epithelial biology rather than serving primarily as an inflammatory executor.

Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to intestinal development, crypt maintenance, epithelial repair, stem-cell renewal and differentiation. Dishevelled (DVL) proteins transmit receptor-proximal Wnt signals to downstream beta-catenin control, whereas APC functions as a scaffold and tumour suppressor within the beta-catenin destruction complex. The findings summarized here define CASP5C as a Wnt-amplifying CASP5 isoform and distinguish this activity from the longer CASP5A and CASP5B variants.

## Results

### CASP5 isoforms are expressed in human intestinal epithelium

Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate molecular masses of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa, respectively. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD, whereas CASP5C lacks a CARD entirely. Small interfering RNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B.

CASP5 abundance increased in organoids after Salmonella infection and after exposure to interferon-beta or interferon-gamma. Lipopolysaccharide alone produced little induction in intestinal epithelial cells, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 expression. Thus, CASP5 responds to inflammatory and infectious contexts in epithelial models without behaving as the dominant mediator of cytosolic lipopolysaccharide sensing.

### CASP5 isoforms associate with DVL proteins

BioID2 proximity labelling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched in interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. DVL2-containing complexes also included AXIN1 and beta-catenin. Mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although direct binding was not fully established.

The functional consequences were isoform specific. In TOPFlash assays, CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells with and without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and promoted redistribution of beta-catenin into nuclear and cytosolic fractions. By contrast, CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduced CASP5C-dependent signalling.

The CARD-less state alone did not fully explain the phenotype across species. Tested CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce CASP5C-mediated Wnt activation, supporting but not proving a primate-specific specialization.

### CASP5C promotes proliferative states in intestinal organoids

The Wnt-amplifying activity of CASP5C was also observed in intestinal epithelial organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity in organoid cultures. CASP5C expression increased viable cell number, enlarged organoids by approximately 40%, increased Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. Consistent with a shift toward Wnt-high and proliferative states, CASP5C increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated markers were reduced, with ALPI and MUC2 decreasing by approximately 57% and 53%, respectively.

CASP5C-expressing organoids also tolerated Wnt withdrawal for longer periods and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored. These findings are consistent with support for regenerative competence in vitro, without establishing improved repair in the human intestine in vivo.

Irradiation provided a second stress context. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript abundance increased 42.4-fold within 24 h, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b increased 5.6-fold and 6.6-fold. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC increased more rapidly in CASP5C-expressing organoids.

### CASP5C catalytic activity disrupts the beta-catenin destruction complex

The requirement for protease activity separated CASP5C from a purely scaffolding role. A catalytic mutant, CASP5C(C173A), lost Wnt reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C also showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B. Removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased their enzymatic activity and conferred stronger Wnt reporter signalling.

After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes. CASP5C also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Together, these results indicate that CASP5C weakens the beta-catenin destruction complex and favours beta-catenin signalling.

APC emerged as a direct proteolytic substrate. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC were concentrated mainly in the N-terminal region. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC Asp556 to alanine removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary cleavage site while leaving room for other sites to contribute.

In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex composition and increase Wnt reporter activity. APC(D556A) weakened this effect. APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin association with the destruction complex and increased reporter activity.

### CASP5 isoforms mark distinct epithelial states

CASP5 isoform localization differed across epithelial states. In one single-cell RNA analysis, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. However, isoform-resolved analyses placed CASP5c deeper in crypt regions, with stronger overlap with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b. CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated, CK20-positive states.

Pseudotime analysis and CIBERSORTx deconvolution supported this division. CASP5c associated with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b associated with mature or differentiating states. During Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early alongside MKI67, while CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiation markers.

Isoform knockdown experiments tested this competition model. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while retaining CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP abundance, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects were reduced.

## Discussion

These findings assign a noncanonical epithelial signalling function to human CASP5. Rather than acting as the principal CASP4-like executor of cytosolic lipopolysaccharide responses in the tested systems, CASP5 is partitioned into isoforms with opposing effects on Wnt/beta-catenin regulation. CASP5C lacks a CARD, has relatively high catalytic activity, associates with DVL-containing complexes, cleaves APC and amplifies Wnt signalling. CASP5A and CASP5B can restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2.

The proposed mechanism connects inflammatory-caspase diversification to a core intestinal homeostasis pathway. In the working model, CASP5C is recruited near DVL and AXIN1-associated destruction-complex components. Its catalytic activity cleaves APC, with D556 serving as a primary but not exclusive site. APC cleavage weakens beta-catenin retention in the destruction complex, reduces beta-catenin ubiquitination and permits increased beta-catenin signalling.

The isoform balance may be as important as total CASP5 expression. CASP5c aligned with early-lineage and transit-amplifying features, damage-induced organoid responses and rapid induction after irradiation. CASP5a and CASP5b aligned more strongly with differentiated compartments and limited CASP5C activity.

Disease-associated observations should be interpreted cautiously. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while preserving their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations were reported in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison, about 68% versus 17%. These associations do not demonstrate that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they establish CASP5C as a validated therapeutic target.

Several limitations remain. Direct CASP5-DVL binding was suggested but not definitively proven. Most functional evidence came from cell lines and organoids, so the in vivo consequences of CASP5C induction during human epithelial injury remain to be determined. The apparent primate specialization of CASP5C also requires broader comparative testing.

Overall, the data support a model in which CASP5C is an epithelial Wnt amplifier rather than a redundant CASP4-like inflammasome protease. By cleaving APC and altering destruction-complex dynamics, CASP5C promotes beta-catenin signalling, organoid growth and proliferative recovery programs. The opposing behaviour of CASP5A and CASP5B suggests that CASP5 isoform composition may provide a local rheostat for intestinal epithelial renewal and differentiation.
