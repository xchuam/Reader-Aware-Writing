# Caspase 5c amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling through APC cleavage

## Abstract

Human CASP5 encodes an inflammatory caspase-family protease related to CASP4 and mouse caspase 11, but its function in intestinal epithelium has remained unclear. In tested human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages, CASP4, not CASP5, was the main noncanonical inflammasome effector for responses to cytosolic LPS and bacteria. CASP5 was instead enriched in intestinal and appendiceal tissues and in epithelial populations, suggesting a distinct epithelial role. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and the CARD-less CASP5C. Proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation connected CASP5 isoforms with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3, placing CASP5 near Wnt regulatory complexes. Among the isoforms, CASP5C selectively enhanced Wnt reporter activity, increased Wnt and proliferation markers, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, and supported larger, more proliferative organoids. Mechanistically, CASP5C required catalytic activity and cleaved APC, with D556 contributing to major cleavage products but not necessarily accounting for all APC processing. CASP5A and CASP5B did not activate Wnt in the same way and could restrain CASP5C, consistent with competition for DVL2-containing complexes. These findings support a model in which CASP5C amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling by weakening APC-dependent beta-catenin destruction complex function.

## Introduction

Inflammatory caspases are best known for innate immune effector functions, yet related proteases can acquire tissue-specific roles. CASP5 has often been discussed with CASP4 and mouse caspase 11 because of sequence and family relationships. However, CASP4 more closely resembled mouse caspase 11 in the tested noncanonical inflammasome response, whereas CASP5 did not perform that role in human intestinal epithelial cells or macrophages. This discrepancy raised a separate question: whether CASP5 has an epithelial function that is missed when the pathway is framed only around cytosolic LPS sensing.

The intestine provided a strong setting for this question. CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and appeared in epithelial cells as well as some monocyte and macrophage populations. In colonic organoids, CASP5 was higher than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD relative to macrophages, whereas CASP4 was more broadly expressed. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD entirely. siRNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B.

Because Wnt/beta-catenin signalling controls intestinal development, repair, stem-cell renewal, proliferation and differentiation, even a modest connection between CASP5 and Wnt complexes would have clear epithelial significance. DVL proteins transmit Wnt receptor events to downstream beta-catenin regulation, while APC acts as a scaffold and tumour suppressor within the beta-catenin destruction complex. The evidence summarized here supports an isoform-specific model in which CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, amplifies Wnt signalling through catalytic disruption of APC-dependent destruction complex function.

## CASP5C connects to DVL-dependent Wnt signalling

CASP5 responded to inflammatory and infectious cues in organoids, but not in a way that fit a simple epithelial LPS-response model. Salmonella infection, IFN-beta and IFN-gamma increased CASP5 expression, whereas LPS alone had little effect in epithelium, probably because epithelial TLR4 expression was low. These observations placed CASP5 at an interface between inflammatory stimulation and epithelial state but did not identify the pathway affected by CASP5.

BioID2 proximity labelling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms appeared in the interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation experiments supported this connection: CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3, and DVL2-containing complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin. Mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain in the interaction. These results do not prove a direct binary interaction, but they place CASP5 in the molecular neighbourhood of DVL-containing Wnt regulatory complexes.

Functional assays then separated CASP5C from the other isoforms. In TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity with or without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same effect. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 and shifted beta-catenin into nuclear and cytosolic fractions. CASP5A or CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduce the CASP5C effect, indicating that the CASP5 locus encodes isoforms with different consequences for the same pathway.

The CARD-less structure of CASP5C appeared relevant but was not sufficient to explain all activity. CASP5C had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing CARD regions from CASP5A/B increased activity and Wnt reporter signalling. However, CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce the Wnt effect. The most cautious interpretation is that CASP5C reflects a primate CASP5 specialization, rather than a generic consequence of deleting a CARD domain from any related caspase.

## CASP5C promotes proliferative organoid states

Organoid experiments showed that the Wnt reporter effect extended beyond transformed cell lines. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity in human intestinal organoids. CASP5C-expressing colonic organoids contained more viable cells, were about 40% larger, had more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. This phenotype matched the direction expected from increased Wnt-responsive proliferation and reduced differentiation under the tested culture conditions.

Marker changes reinforced this interpretation. In colonic organoids, CASP5C increased AXIN2 about threefold, TCF7 1.4-fold, MYC 1.3-fold, LGR5 1.2-fold and MKI67 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated markers moved in the opposite direction, with ALPI and MUC2 decreasing about 57% and 53%. CASP5C-expressing organoids also grew longer without Wnt and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored. After irradiation, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b, followed by faster increases in LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These findings support a repair-associated organoid program, but they do not prove repair activity in the human body.

Lineage analyses helped clarify why total CASP5 expression alone is not enough to infer function. In one single-cell analysis, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. Yet CASP5c was deeper in crypts and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a/b, whereas CASP5A/B were more luminal and associated with differentiated or CK20-positive cells. Pseudotime analysis, CIBERSORTx and Wnt-withdrawal differentiation experiments similarly linked CASP5c with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and CASP5a/b with mature or differentiating states.

Isoform knockdown experiments directly tested this balance. Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP abundance, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. This pattern supports a competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C, at least in part by competing for DVL2-containing complexes.

## CASP5C activates Wnt through APC cleavage

CASP5C required catalytic activity to amplify Wnt signalling. The catalytic mutant CASP5C(C173A) lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects, arguing against a purely scaffolding mechanism. In AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP. It also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Together, these findings are consistent with weakened beta-catenin destruction complex function.

APC was the key substrate connecting CASP5C protease activity to this pathway. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were found mostly in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary site, although other APC cleavage sites may also contribute.

APC-reconstitution experiments linked cleavage to signalling output. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex components and increase Wnt reporter activity. APC(D556A) weakened this effect. APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. These results support a sequence in which CASP5C associates with DVL-containing complexes, cleaves APC, reduces APC-dependent destruction complex function and amplifies beta-catenin signalling.

## Discussion

The central conclusion is that CASP5C has an epithelial Wnt-amplifying activity distinct from the noncanonical inflammasome function associated with CASP4. This activity is isoform-specific, catalysis-dependent and mechanistically tied to APC cleavage. CASP5A and CASP5B should therefore not be grouped with CASP5C as equivalent Wnt activators; in several experiments they failed to activate the pathway and instead restrained CASP5C.

This isoform logic also helps reconcile apparently mixed expression patterns. Total CASP5 can be high in differentiated epithelial populations, yet CASP5c more closely aligns with crypt, MKI67, AXIN2 and transit-amplifying features. The relevant variable is therefore not simply whether CASP5 is expressed, but which isoform is present and whether CASP5C activity can act within DVL- and APC-containing Wnt complexes.

Disease-associated observations are compatible with this model but do not establish disease causation. CASP5 and CASP4 increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations occurred in about 3% of colon or rectal cancers in one analysis, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In one MSI-high colorectal cancer comparison, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC mutations, about 68% versus 17%. These associations warrant further study, but they do not show that CASP5C causes ulcerative colitis or colorectal cancer, nor do they validate CASP5C as a drug target.

Several uncertainties remain. CASP5-DVL proximity and co-immunoprecipitation support shared complexes, but direct binding is not fully proven. D556 is a major APC cleavage site, but it may not be the only relevant site. Organoid data support a role for CASP5C in Wnt-responsive proliferation and stress recovery in culture, but in vivo repair and disease biology require additional evidence. Within these limits, CASP5C provides a mechanistic bridge between inflammatory caspase-family biology and epithelial Wnt control: a CARD-less CASP5 isoform can cleave APC, weaken beta-catenin destruction complex function and promote a proliferative intestinal epithelial state.
