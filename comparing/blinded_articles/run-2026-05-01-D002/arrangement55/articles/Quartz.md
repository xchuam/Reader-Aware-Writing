# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models

## Abstract

Inflammatory caspases are best known for innate immune functions, but the role of human caspase 5 remains incompletely defined. Here we describe an intestinal epithelial function for the short isoform caspase 5c (CASP5C). In tested human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages, CASP4, rather than CASP5, mediated responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. CASP5 was instead enriched in intestinal epithelial contexts, where organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C. Proximity labelling and co-immunoprecipitation linked CASP5 to Dishevelled proteins and Wnt signalling. CASP5C selectively increased Wnt reporter activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, induced Wnt target genes and enhanced organoid growth. Mechanistically, CASP5C required catalytic activity and cleaved APC, including at a major D556 site, weakening destruction-complex assembly and reducing beta-catenin ubiquitination. CASP5A and CASP5B did not reproduce this activity and could restrain CASP5C by competing for Dishevelled binding. These findings identify CASP5C as an epithelial modulator of Wnt signalling and suggest that CASP5 isoform balance may influence intestinal renewal programmes.

## Introduction

The intestinal epithelium renews rapidly while maintaining a barrier against microbes and injury. This balance depends heavily on Wnt/beta-catenin signalling, which supports intestinal development, repair, stem-cell renewal and lineage decisions. Dishevelled proteins connect Wnt receptor activation to downstream signalling, whereas APC functions as a destruction-complex scaffold and tumour suppressor that helps restrain beta-catenin. Mechanisms that tune Wnt signalling in intestinal cells are therefore central to epithelial homeostasis.

Inflammatory caspases are generally studied as innate immune proteases. Human CASP4 and CASP5 are related to mouse caspase 11, but their respective functions have been difficult to separate. Sequence and phylogenetic comparisons place CASP4 closer to mouse caspase 11, and functional assays in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages supported CASP4 as the dominant mediator of noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. CASP5 did not perform this role detectably in those experiments. This raised the possibility that CASP5 has a different function, particularly in tissues where it is enriched.

Expression patterns pointed to the intestine as a relevant site for CASP5 biology. CASP5 was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix, with signal in epithelial cells and some monocyte or macrophage populations. Colonic organoids expressed CASP5 strongly relative to several inflammasome components. Human intestinal organoids contained three CASP5 isoforms: full-length CASP5A, CASP5B, which lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C, an approximately 33.3 kDa isoform lacking the CARD. Isoform-specific knockdown indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of the longer isoforms.

Here we reconstruct the evidence that CASP5C has a specialized epithelial role. The data show that CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, amplifies Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in intestinal organoid and cell-line models. The mechanism involves binding to Dishevelled proteins and catalytic cleavage of APC, leading to reduced destruction-complex function. The findings also reveal an isoform competition model in which CASP5A and CASP5B can restrain CASP5C activity.

## Methods

Human intestinal epithelial cells, macrophages and human colon and small-intestinal organoids were used to compare inflammatory caspase function, CASP5 expression and Wnt pathway activity. CASP5 induction was assessed after Salmonella, interferon-beta and interferon-gamma stimulation, and lipopolysaccharide exposure was examined in epithelial contexts. BioID2 proximity labelling, co-immunoprecipitation and domain mapping tested CASP5 association with Dishevelled proteins. TOPFlash reporter assays, beta-catenin localization, Wnt target-gene expression and AXIN1-complex analyses measured pathway output. Organoid size, viability, ATP content, proliferation, differentiation, Wnt withdrawal and irradiation responses were used to assess epithelial phenotypes. Catalytic requirements were tested with CASP5C(C173A), APC mutants, recombinant cleavage assays and APC knockout cells reconstituted with wild-type or D556A APC. Single-cell and computational analyses related CASP5 isoforms to epithelial cell states.

## Results

### CASP5 is enriched in intestinal epithelial contexts and includes a short CARD-less isoform

CASP5 differed from CASP4 functionally and by expression pattern. In cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacterial-response assays, CASP4, not CASP5, acted as the main noncanonical inflammasome caspase in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages. CASP5 instead associated more strongly with intestinal tissues and epithelial models.

Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C. The short isoform, CASP5C, lacks the CARD domain, whereas CASP5B lacks part of this region. CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella infection and after interferon-beta or interferon-gamma treatment. Lipopolysaccharide alone produced little epithelial induction, consistent with limited TLR4 activity in this setting.

### CASP5 associates with Dishevelled proteins and the Wnt pathway

BioID2 proximity labelling in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched in the interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with Dishevelled proteins. DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin. The interaction involved the Dishevelled DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, supporting proximity to Wnt machinery without proving a direct binary interaction.

### CASP5C selectively increases Wnt/beta-catenin signalling

Functional assays separated the CASP5 isoforms sharply. CASP5C increased TOPFlash Wnt reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, with or without WNT3A. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 and promoted beta-catenin accumulation in cytosolic and nuclear fractions.

The same isoform specificity was observed in organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. CASP5C-expressing organoids had more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger, showed increased Ki-67 and EdU positivity, and contained fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. AXIN2 rose about threefold, MKI67 about 2.4-fold and TCF7, MYC and LGR5 increased more modestly, whereas ALPI and MUC2 decreased by approximately 57% and 53%.

CASP5C also altered organoid behaviour under Wnt-limited and injury-associated conditions. CASP5C-expressing organoids grew for longer after Wnt withdrawal and re-entered the cell cycle when Wnt was restored. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 h, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b, and renewal-associated genes rose faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These findings support a role for CASP5C in epithelial renewal programmes in model systems, without establishing in vivo effects.

### CASP5C catalytic activity weakens the beta-catenin destruction complex through APC cleavage

The Wnt-amplifying activity of CASP5C required its catalytic site. The CASP5C(C173A) mutant lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C showed higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and CARD removal from CASP5A or CASP5B increased activity and Wnt reporter signalling.

CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated in the N-terminal region of APC. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and catalytically inactive CASP5C(C173A) did not. Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro.

Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary, but not necessarily exclusive, cleavage site. In APC knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the destruction complex and increase Wnt reporter activity; APC(D556A) weakened these effects. APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin association with the destruction complex and increased reporter activity.

### CASP5A and CASP5B restrain CASP5C by competing for Dishevelled binding

CASP5 isoforms had opposing effects on Wnt signalling. CASP5A and CASP5B did not amplify reporter activity, and their expression could reduce the effect of CASP5C by competing for DVL2 binding. When CASP5a and CASP5b were knocked down while CASP5C was retained, Wnt reporter activity increased, organoids became larger, ATP content rose and EdU and Ki-67 positivity increased. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. These results support an isoform balance model in which longer CASP5 isoforms buffer CASP5C activity.

Cell-state analyses were consistent with this model but also showed complexity. In one single-cell RNA analysis, CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent in LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. However, CASP5c was deeper in crypts and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b. Pseudotime, CIBERSORTx and Wnt-withdrawal differentiation analyses linked CASP5c with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and CASP5a/b with mature or differentiating states.

### Disease-associated observations suggest relevance but do not establish causality

Available disease data were associative. CASP5 and CASP4 increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type expression patterns. CASP5 mutations were reported in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison, at approximately 68% versus 17%. These observations do not demonstrate that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or colorectal cancer, nor do they validate CASP5C as a therapeutic target.

## Discussion

This study identifies CASP5C as a Wnt-amplifying inflammatory caspase isoform in intestinal epithelial models. The central finding is not that all CASP5 proteins promote Wnt signalling, but that the CARD-less CASP5C isoform has a distinct catalytic activity that modifies the beta-catenin destruction complex. By associating with Dishevelled proteins and cleaving APC, CASP5C reduces destruction-complex function, decreases beta-catenin ubiquitination and increases Wnt transcriptional output. CASP5A and CASP5B have different effects: they can bind Dishevelled but do not reproduce CASP5C signalling, and they can restrain CASP5C by competition.

The work also clarifies the relationship between CASP5 and related inflammatory caspases. CASP4, rather than CASP5, performed the principal noncanonical inflammasome role in the tested human epithelial and macrophage systems. CASP5C therefore appears to represent a divergent epithelial function of the CASP5 locus rather than a redundant form of CASP4-like immune sensing. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not mimic the Wnt effect, suggesting that the activity may reflect a primate CASP5 specialization after gene duplication. That evolutionary interpretation remains tentative because the data do not establish the full comparative mechanism across species.

The organoid data suggest that CASP5C can support epithelial proliferative capacity under Wnt-limited and injury-associated conditions. CASP5C expression increased strongly after irradiation, and CASP5C-expressing organoids showed faster induction of renewal-associated genes. These findings fit a model in which CASP5C contributes to repair-associated Wnt amplification. However, the evidence comes from model systems, and the physiological magnitude, timing and safety of this activity in human tissue remain unresolved.

Several limitations constrain interpretation. Direct binding between CASP5 and Dishevelled is suggested but not fully proven. APC D556 is a major CASP5C cleavage site, yet other APC sites may contribute. Disease associations in ulcerative colitis and colorectal cancer are hypothesis-generating only, and therapeutic implications remain undefined.

Overall, the findings expand the functional range of inflammatory caspases by linking a CASP5 isoform to epithelial Wnt control. CASP5C provides a mechanism for amplifying Wnt/beta-catenin signalling through APC cleavage, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B may restrain that activity through Dishevelled competition. This isoform-specific framework helps reconcile CASP5 intestinal enrichment with its limited role in the tested noncanonical inflammasome response and provides a basis for future studies of epithelial repair, differentiation and disease-associated Wnt regulation.
