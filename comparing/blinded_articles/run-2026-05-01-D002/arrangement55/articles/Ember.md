# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling in intestinal epithelial models

## Abstract

Inflammatory caspases are best known for innate immune sensing, but the
function of human caspase 5 has remained unclear.
Although caspase 5 is related to caspase 4 and mouse caspase 11, caspase 4 was
the main mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic
lipopolysaccharide and bacteria.
Here, human intestinal organoid and cell-based assays support a role for the
caspase 5c isoform in Wnt signalling.
Caspase 5c interacted with Dishevelled proteins, increased Wnt reporter
activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, and induced Wnt target genes.
In organoids, caspase 5c increased proliferation-associated markers, increased
organoid growth, and reduced differentiation markers.
Mechanistic experiments implicated the catalytic activity of caspase 5c and
cleavage of the Wnt destruction-complex scaffold APC, with aspartate 556 acting
as a major cleavage site.
Caspase 5a and caspase 5b did not reproduce these effects and could restrain
caspase 5c activity by competing for Dishevelled binding.
These findings define an isoform-specific epithelial function for caspase 5 and
suggest that inflammatory-caspase diversification in humans includes regulation
of intestinal Wnt homeostasis.

## Introduction

The intestinal epithelium depends on a controlled balance between renewal,
differentiation, and repair.
Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to this balance because it supports
intestinal development, stem-cell renewal, epithelial repair, and lineage
decisions.
In the canonical pathway, Dishevelled proteins connect receptor-level Wnt
signals to downstream control of beta-catenin.
The destruction complex, which includes APC and AXIN1, limits beta-catenin
activity when Wnt input is low.
Changes in this system can therefore alter epithelial proliferation and
differentiation.

Caspase 5 belongs to the inflammatory caspase family and is related to human
caspase 4 and mouse caspase 11.
Yet functional tests in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages
identified caspase 4, not caspase 5, as the main actor in cytosolic
lipopolysaccharide and bacterial responses.
This distinction left open a different role for caspase 5.

Expression patterns pointed to the intestine as a plausible site for that role.
Caspase 5 was enriched in the intestinal tract and appendix, with expression in
epithelial cells and in some monocyte or macrophage populations.
In human colonic organoids, caspase 5 exceeded caspase 4, caspase 1, and
PYCARD when compared with macrophages, whereas caspase 4 was broader.
Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three caspase 5 isoforms:
caspase 5a, caspase 5b, and caspase 5c.
These isoforms differ in N-terminal CARD content.
Caspase 5b lacks part of the CARD region, and caspase 5c lacks the CARD.
Caspase 5c was not simply a cleavage product of the longer isoforms.

Caspase 5 also responded to inflammatory and microbial signals in organoids.
Salmonella infection, interferon beta, and interferon gamma increased caspase 5
expression.
Lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelial models, consistent
with low epithelial TLR4 expression.
These observations placed caspase 5 at the interface of intestinal epithelial
biology and inflammatory signalling.

## Results

## Caspase 5c is linked to Dishevelled proteins and Wnt signalling

BioID2 analysis identified DVL1, DVL2, and DVL3 near caspase 5, and
Wnt-related terms were enriched among candidate interactors.
Each caspase 5 isoform co-immunoprecipitated with DVL1, DVL2, and DVL3.
DVL2-containing complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin.
Mapping experiments implicated the Dishevelled DEP domain and the caspase 5
catalytic domain in the interaction, although direct physical binding was not
fully established.

In TOPFlash reporter assays, caspase 5c increased Wnt reporter activity in
HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both with and without WNT3A stimulation.
Caspase 5a, caspase 5b, caspase 1, and caspase 4 did not produce the same
response.
Caspase 5c also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and promoted beta-catenin
accumulation in nuclear and cytosolic fractions.
By contrast, caspase 5a and caspase 5b could reduce the caspase 5c effect,
consistent with competition for DVL2 binding.

The catalytic mutant caspase 5c C173A lost the ability to activate the Wnt
reporter and to alter beta-catenin behaviour.
Caspase 5c showed higher enzymatic activity than caspase 5a and caspase 5b.
Removal of the CARD from caspase 5a or caspase 5b increased their activity and
conferred stronger Wnt reporter signalling.
CARD-less forms of mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce this effect, so the data
are consistent with, but do not prove, a primate-specific specialisation of
caspase 5 after duplication.

## Caspase 5c promotes proliferative behaviour in human intestinal organoids

In these organoids, caspase 5c, but not caspase 5a or caspase 5b, increased Wnt
reporter activity.
Caspase 5c expression increased the number of viable cells, generated organoids
that were approximately 40% larger, and increased Ki-67-positive and
EdU-positive cells.
It also reduced CK20-positive enterocytes.
These changes indicate a shift towards proliferation and away from enterocyte
differentiation under the tested conditions.

In colonic organoids, caspase 5c increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by
1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold, and MKI67 by 2.4-fold.
The differentiation-associated markers ALPI and MUC2 decreased by about 57% and
53%, respectively.
Caspase 5c-expressing organoids also tolerated longer culture without Wnt and
could re-enter the cell cycle after Wnt was restored.
These findings are compatible with enhanced regenerative capacity in the model,
but they do not establish the same effect in human tissue in vivo.

After irradiation of human colonic organoids, the caspase 5c transcript
increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours.
Caspase 5a and caspase 5b increased 5.6-fold and 6.6-fold, respectively.
After this induction, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7, and MYC rose more rapidly in
caspase 5c-expressing organoids.
These data connect caspase 5c induction with faster activation of Wnt-associated
and proliferation-associated programmes after injury-like stress.

Knockdown of caspase 5a and caspase 5b, while preserving caspase 5c, increased
Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP content, EdU incorporation, and Ki-67
staining.
When caspase 5c was also knocked down, these effects returned towards baseline.
This pattern supports a competition model in which caspase 5a and caspase 5b
can restrain caspase 5c by occupying Dishevelled-containing complexes.

## Caspase 5c cleaves APC and weakens the destruction complex

After Wnt stimulation, caspase 5c reduced the amounts of DVL2, beta-catenin,
phospho-beta-catenin, APC, and beta-TrCP detected in AXIN1-containing
complexes.
It also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination.
These changes are consistent with impaired beta-catenin turnover.

Predicted caspase 5 cleavage sites were concentrated in the N-terminal region
of APC.
Caspase 5c generated APC cleavage products, whereas caspase 5a, caspase 5b,
and the catalytically inactive caspase 5c C173A mutant did not.
Recombinant caspase 5c directly cleaved a truncated APC protein in vitro.
Mutation of APC aspartate 556 to alanine removed the major cleavage products,
identifying D556 as a primary cleavage site.
The available data also leave room for additional APC cleavage sites that may
contribute to the response.

In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed
caspase 5c to disrupt the destruction complex and increase Wnt reporter
activity.
The APC D556A mutant weakened this response.
APC fragments resembling caspase 5c cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in
the destruction complex and increased reporter activity.
These experiments support a model in which caspase 5c cleaves APC, weakens the
destruction complex, and amplifies Wnt signalling.

## Isoform expression separates proliferative and differentiated epithelial
states

In one single-cell RNA analysis, total caspase 5 transcripts were nearly absent
from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells, and highest
in enterocytes.
Caspase 5c was enriched deeper in crypt-like regions and overlapped more with
MKI67 and AXIN2 than caspase 5a or caspase 5b.
Caspase 5a and caspase 5b showed a more luminal distribution and greater
association with differentiated, CK20-positive cells.

Caspase 5c tracked with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states.
Caspase 5a and caspase 5b tracked with mature or differentiating states.
During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, caspase 5c peaked early with
MKI67, whereas caspase 5a and caspase 5b rose later with differentiation
markers.
Thus, caspase 5c associated with proliferative epithelial programmes, while
caspase 5a and caspase 5b associated with differentiating states.

## Discussion

Rather than acting as the principal human equivalent of mouse caspase 11 in the
tested noncanonical inflammasome responses, caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling
in human intestinal epithelial models.
A range of assays showed an isoform-specific effect.
Caspase 5c promotes Wnt output, whereas caspase 5a and caspase 5b do not and
can oppose caspase 5c activity.

The proposed mechanism places caspase 5c at the level of APC and the
destruction complex.
By associating with Dishevelled-containing complexes and cleaving APC,
caspase 5c reduces beta-catenin retention and ubiquitination in the destruction
complex.
This allows beta-catenin to accumulate and increases Wnt target genes such as
AXIN2 and TCF7.
The D556 residue in APC is a major cleavage site, but the evidence does not
exclude additional sites or context-dependent substrates.
The requirement for catalytic activity argues against a purely scaffolding role.

The organoid data suggest a possible role in epithelial renewal and repair.
Caspase 5c increased organoid growth, viability, proliferation markers, and
recovery-associated Wnt targets after irradiation.
It also allowed organoids to better persist during Wnt withdrawal and to
restart cycling when Wnt returned.
However, organoid findings do not prove that caspase 5c drives repair in
patients.

The disease observations are therefore best treated as associations.
Caspase 5 and caspase 4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while
retaining their cell-type patterns.
Caspase 5 mutations occurred in about 3% of colon or rectal cancer samples in
the reported analysis, a frequency similar to AXIN1.
These mutations tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and to be
mutually exclusive with APC mutations.
In one MSI-high colorectal cancer comparison, caspase 5 mutations were reported
more often than APC mutations, at about 68% versus 17%.
These patterns are compatible with a relationship between caspase 5 and Wnt
biology, but they do not establish disease causality.
They also do not validate caspase 5c as a therapeutic target.

Overall, the data support a model in which human caspase 5 diversification
generated an epithelial isoform that can tune Wnt signalling.
Caspase 5c acts through catalytic cleavage of APC and consequent weakening of
the beta-catenin destruction complex.
Caspase 5a and caspase 5b provide a counterweight by competing with caspase 5c
for Dishevelled-associated binding.
The central conclusion is therefore not that all caspase 5 isoforms promote
Wnt, but that caspase 5c has a specific capacity to amplify Wnt signalling in
human intestinal epithelial models.
