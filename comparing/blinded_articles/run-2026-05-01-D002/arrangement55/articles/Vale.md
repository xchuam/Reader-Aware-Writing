# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in human intestinal epithelial models

## Abstract

Inflammatory caspases are best known for host-defence and inflammasome-associated functions, but the role of human caspase 5 has remained unclear. Although caspase 5 is related to caspase 4 and mouse caspase 11, experiments in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages indicated that caspase 4, rather than caspase 5, is the main mediator of the tested noncanonical inflammasome response to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. Here, using human intestinal organoids and cell-based assays, the source data support a model in which the CARD-less isoform caspase 5c promotes Wnt/beta-catenin signalling. Caspase 5c associated with Dishevelled proteins, activated Wnt reporters, increased beta-catenin redistribution, and promoted proliferation-associated states in organoids. Mechanistically, caspase 5c required catalytic activity and cleaved the Wnt destruction-complex scaffold APC, with D556 contributing to major cleavage products. Caspase 5a and caspase 5b did not produce the same Wnt activation and could restrain caspase 5c, apparently by competing for Dishevelled binding. These findings identify an isoform-specific epithelial function for caspase 5c.

## Introduction

Human caspase 5 belongs to the inflammatory caspase family and is closely related to human caspase 4 and mouse caspase 11. This relationship has encouraged the assumption that caspase 5 might participate in the same noncanonical inflammasome pathway that detects cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacterial invasion. However, comparative experiments in human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages did not support caspase 5 as the principal executor of that response. Instead, caspase 4 more closely resembled mouse caspase 11 in sequence, phylogenetic placement, and tested function. This left open a central question: if caspase 5 is not simply a redundant caspase 4-like sensor, what does it do?

Expression patterns suggested that the answer might lie in the intestine. Caspase 5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix, and was detected in epithelial cells as well as in some monocyte and macrophage populations. In human colonic organoids, caspase 5 was more prominent than caspase 4, caspase 1, and PYCARD when compared with macrophages. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three caspase 5 isoforms: caspase 5a, caspase 5b, and caspase 5c, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7, 43.2, and 33.3 kDa. Caspase 5b lacks part of the CARD region, and caspase 5c lacks the CARD entirely. siRNA experiments argued that caspase 5c was not merely a cleavage product of caspase 5a or caspase 5b.

The intestine depends on tightly controlled Wnt/beta-catenin signalling, which supports epithelial development, repair, stem-cell renewal, and differentiation. Dishevelled proteins connect receptor-proximal Wnt events to downstream control of beta-catenin, while APC acts as a scaffold and tumour suppressor within the beta-catenin destruction complex. The data summarized here support a role for caspase 5c as an amplifier of this pathway in human intestinal epithelial models.

## Results

### Caspase 5 is induced in intestinal epithelial contexts but does not act like caspase 4 in tested inflammasome responses

Caspase 5 expression responded to inflammatory and microbial cues in intestinal organoids. Salmonella infection, interferon beta, and interferon gamma increased caspase 5, whereas lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelium, consistent with low TLR4 expression in that compartment. These results placed caspase 5 in an inducible epithelial context, but did not establish the noncanonical inflammasome role often inferred from its family membership.

That distinction was important because caspase 4, not caspase 5, behaved as the main functional counterpart of mouse caspase 11 in the tested cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacterial-response assays. The divergence suggested another activity for caspase 5, particularly in intestinal epithelium.

### Caspase 5 isoforms associate with Dishevelled proteins

To identify candidate functions, proximity labelling with BioID2 was performed in human colonic organoids. The analysis identified DVL1, DVL2, and DVL3 near caspase 5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched among the interaction candidates. Co-immunoprecipitation experiments supported physical association between caspase 5 isoforms and DVL proteins. DVL2-containing complexes also included AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing the interaction near Wnt destruction-complex machinery.

Mapping experiments indicated that the DVL DEP domain and the caspase 5 catalytic domain contributed to binding. The available data are consistent with a close association and may indicate a direct interaction, although direct binding was not fully established.

### Caspase 5c selectively activates Wnt/beta-catenin signalling

Functional assays showed strong isoform specificity. In TOPFlash reporter assays in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, caspase 5c increased Wnt reporter activity both with and without WNT3A stimulation. Caspase 5a, caspase 5b, caspase 1, and caspase 4 did not reproduce this effect. Caspase 5c also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 and promoted beta-catenin redistribution into nuclear and cytosolic fractions.

The same specificity was observed in organoid models. In human intestinal organoids, caspase 5c increased Wnt reporter activity, whereas caspase 5a and caspase 5b did not. Caspase 5c-expressing colonic organoids had more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger, and contained more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, with fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. At the transcript level, caspase 5c increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold, and MKI67 by 2.4-fold. Differentiation-associated markers were reduced, with ALPI decreasing by about 57% and MUC2 by about 53%.

These findings indicate that caspase 5c shifted organoids toward a Wnt-responsive, proliferative state. Caspase 5c-expressing organoids also survived longer during Wnt withdrawal and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored. In irradiated human colonic organoids, caspase 5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for caspase 5a and 6.6-fold for caspase 5b. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7, and MYC increased more rapidly in caspase 5c-expressing organoids. These results are consistent with a repair-associated programme in organoids, not proof of repair function in the human body.

### Caspase 5a and caspase 5b can restrain caspase 5c activity

The nonactivating isoforms were not inert. Caspase 5a and caspase 5b could compete with caspase 5c for DVL2 and reduce its Wnt-promoting effect. When caspase 5a and caspase 5b were knocked down while caspase 5c was preserved, Wnt reporter activity increased, organoids became larger, ATP levels rose, and EdU and Ki-67 positivity increased. When caspase 5c was also knocked down, these effects declined.

These observations argue against treating caspase 5 as a single functional unit. The Wnt-amplifying activity mapped primarily to caspase 5c, while caspase 5a and caspase 5b appeared able to antagonize that activity.

### Catalytic activity enables caspase 5c to remodel the destruction complex

Caspase 5c required catalytic activity to activate Wnt signalling. A catalytic mutant, caspase 5c(C173A), lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. Caspase 5c also showed higher enzymatic activity than caspase 5a or caspase 5b. Removing the CARD from caspase 5a or caspase 5b increased their activity and enabled stronger Wnt reporter signalling, indicating that the N-terminal region may restrain the catalytic domain in the longer isoforms.

At the destruction complex, caspase 5c reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC, and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation. Caspase 5c also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Together, these findings support a model in which caspase 5c weakens destruction-complex assembly or function, allowing beta-catenin to participate in downstream Wnt transcriptional responses.

APC was a key substrate candidate. Predicted caspase 5 cleavage sites were present in APC, mainly in the N-terminal region. Caspase 5c produced APC cleavage products, whereas caspase 5a, caspase 5b, and caspase 5c(C173A) did not. Recombinant caspase 5c directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as an important cleavage site. Other APC cleavage sites may also contribute, so D556 should not be treated as the only relevant site.

The functional importance of APC cleavage was tested in APC-knockout HEK293T cells. Reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed caspase 5c to disrupt destruction-complex components and increase Wnt reporter activity. APC(D556A) weakened this effect, while APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity.

### Caspase 5 isoforms occupy distinct epithelial states

Single-cell and differentiation analyses suggested that caspase 5 isoforms are associated with different epithelial states. Total caspase 5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells, and highest in enterocytes. Within this pattern, caspase 5c showed a deeper crypt distribution and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than did caspase 5a or caspase 5b. Caspase 5a and caspase 5b were more luminal and more associated with differentiated, CK20-positive compartments.

Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx analyses supported the same pattern: caspase 5c tracked with early-lineage or transit-amplifying states, whereas caspase 5a and caspase 5b tracked with more mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, caspase 5c peaked early with MKI67, while caspase 5a and caspase 5b increased later with differentiation markers.

## Discussion

The findings support a model in which human caspase 5c acts as an isoform-specific amplifier of Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in intestinal epithelial models. This function is distinct from the tested noncanonical inflammasome activity more strongly associated with caspase 4. The key evidence is convergent: caspase 5c associates with DVL proteins, activates Wnt reporters, increases Wnt target expression, shifts beta-catenin localization, promotes proliferative organoid features, requires catalytic activity, and cleaves APC.

The isoform specificity is central. Caspase 5a and caspase 5b should not be described as Wnt activators in the same sense as caspase 5c. Instead, the longer isoforms appear to restrain caspase 5c under some conditions, likely through competition for DVL2-containing complexes and lower intrinsic catalytic activity. CARD removal increased activity in caspase 5a and caspase 5b, suggesting that structural differences tune their effects on Wnt signalling.

The data also suggest a possible evolutionary specialization. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce the Wnt effect, implying that caspase 5c may reflect a primate-specific adaptation after caspase duplication. This remains tentative because the evidence establishes functional divergence, not a complete evolutionary mechanism.

The disease-associated observations are intriguing but limited. Caspase 5 and caspase 4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type patterns. Caspase 5 mutations appeared in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers, similar to AXIN1. They tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, caspase 5 mutations were reported more often than APC mutations in one comparison, about 68% versus 17%. These associations do not show that caspase 5c causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, and they do not establish caspase 5c as a validated therapeutic target.

Overall, the work expands the functional range of inflammatory caspases. In the intestinal epithelial systems described here, caspase 5c links an inflammatory caspase-family protein to Wnt pathway control through APC cleavage. By distinguishing caspase 5c from caspase 5a, caspase 5b, and caspase 4, the results clarify why caspase 5 has been difficult to assign to a single canonical role. Caspase 5c may be best understood not as a duplicate of caspase 4, but as an epithelial isoform that can tune Wnt signalling, proliferation, and differentiation in human intestinal organoid models.
