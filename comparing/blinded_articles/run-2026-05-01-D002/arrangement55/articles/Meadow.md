# Caspase 5c amplifies epithelial Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in human intestinal models

## Abstract

Human caspase 5 is an inflammatory caspase-family protease, but its function has been less clear than that of CASP4, which more closely resembles mouse caspase 11 and mediates tested noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. Here, human intestinal organoid and cell-line experiments identify a distinct role for the CARD-less CASP5C isoform in Wnt/beta-catenin signalling. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 or CASP4, increased Wnt reporter activity, promoted beta-catenin redistribution, and induced Wnt-associated genes. CASP5C associated with Dishevelled proteins and used its catalytic activity to cleave APC, weakening beta-catenin destruction-complex function. In organoids, CASP5C increased proliferation, reduced differentiation markers, supported growth during Wnt limitation, and accelerated Wnt-response gene induction after irradiation. CASP5A and CASP5B could restrain this activity, probably by competing with CASP5C for Dishevelled-containing complexes. These findings support CASP5C as an intestinal epithelial amplifier of Wnt signalling rather than a simple paralogue of CASP4.

## Introduction

The intestinal epithelium renews continuously, requiring a controlled balance between stem-cell maintenance, transit-amplifying proliferation and differentiation. Wnt/beta-catenin signalling is central to this process. Dishevelled proteins transmit receptor-proximal Wnt signals, whereas the destruction complex, including AXIN1, APC and beta-TrCP, limits beta-catenin accumulation. Changes in this pathway can therefore alter epithelial growth, differentiation and repair-associated responses.

CASP5 raised a different question. Although it belongs to the inflammatory caspase family, tested human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages used CASP4, not CASP5, as the main caspase for noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria. CASP5 expression was also more concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix, including epithelial cells and selected monocyte or macrophage populations. In colonic organoids, CASP5 was higher than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD relative to macrophages, whereas CASP4 had a broader distribution. These observations suggested that CASP5 might have an intestine-focused epithelial function outside the usual noncanonical inflammasome frame.

Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate masses of 49.7, 43.2 and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacks part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacks the CARD entirely. siRNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B. CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella infection and after interferon-beta or interferon-gamma exposure, whereas lipopolysaccharide alone caused little epithelial induction, consistent with low epithelial TLR4. The study therefore asked whether CASP5 isoforms connect inflammatory or injury-associated cues to epithelial Wnt signalling.

## Methods

The work combined human intestinal organoids, macrophage comparisons, reporter assays, interaction mapping and biochemical cleavage experiments. Expression analyses distinguished CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C rather than treating CASP5 as a single product. BioID2 in human colonic organoids identified candidate CASP5-proximal proteins, and co-immunoprecipitation tested association with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. Additional mapping examined the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain.

Wnt activity was measured with TOPFlash reporters in HEK293T cells, HCT116 cells and organoids, with and without WNT3A. Catalytic dependence was tested using CASP5C(C173A), and CARD-dependent regulation was evaluated by comparing full-length and CARD-deleted caspase forms. Organoid consequences were assessed by viability, size, proliferation markers, differentiation markers, Wnt withdrawal and Wnt restoration. Irradiated colonic organoids were used to examine injury-associated CASP5 isoform induction and downstream Wnt-response genes. Mechanistic experiments measured beta-catenin localization, beta-catenin ubiquitination, AXIN1-associated complexes, APC cleavage, APC site mutants and APC fragments resembling cleavage products.

## Results

Protein-proximity analysis first linked CASP5 to Wnt machinery. BioID2 identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5 in human colonic organoids, and Wnt-related terms appeared in the interactor analysis. CASP5 isoforms co-immunoprecipitated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3, and DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin. The interaction involved the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although direct binding was not fully proven.

Functional assays showed that the Wnt effect was isoform specific. CASP5C increased TOPFlash activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both with and without WNT3A, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 and shifted beta-catenin into cytosolic and nuclear fractions. CARD-less mouse caspase 11 forms did not reproduce this effect, suggesting that CASP5C may reflect a primate CASP5 specialization after duplication, although this evolutionary interpretation remains provisional.

Human intestinal organoids showed the same specificity. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. CASP5C-expressing organoids contained more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger, had more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells, and had fewer CK20-positive enterocytes. CASP5C increased Wnt and proliferation-associated markers, including AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold. Differentiation markers decreased, with ALPI and MUC2 falling by approximately 57% and 53%. These results indicate that CASP5C shifts organoids toward a proliferative, Wnt-responsive state.

CASP5C also affected organoid responses to Wnt limitation and injury. CASP5C-expressing organoids grew longer without Wnt and re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt restoration. After irradiation of human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased 42.4-fold within 24 h, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC then rose faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These findings are consistent with a role in epithelial recovery programs in organoid systems, but they do not prove repair activity in the human body.

The mechanism depended on CASP5C catalytic activity and APC cleavage. CASP5C had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and CASP5C(C173A) lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. Removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased their activity and gave stronger Wnt reporter signalling. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phospho-beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes, and it reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC were concentrated largely in the N-terminal region. CASP5C produced APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro.

APC D556 was a major cleavage site. Mutating D556 removed the major cleavage products, and in APC-knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the destruction complex and increase Wnt reporter activity more effectively than APC(D556A). APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. D556 is therefore a primary site, but the data do not exclude contributions from additional APC cleavage sites.

CASP5 isoforms also opposed one another. CASP5A and CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduce CASP5C-driven Wnt activation. In organoids, knocking down CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5c increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU staining and Ki-67 staining. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. This supports a competition model in which CARD-containing or partially CARD-containing isoforms restrain CASP5C by occupying shared Dishevelled-associated complexes.

Cell-state analyses were consistent with this model. In one single-cell RNA analysis, total CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. Isoform-resolved patterns refined that picture: CASP5c was deeper in crypts and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b were more luminal and associated with differentiated or CK20-positive states. Pseudotime and CIBERSORTx similarly linked CASP5c to early-lineage or transit-amplifying cells and CASP5a/b to mature or differentiating states. During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with MKI67, whereas CASP5a/b rose later with differentiation markers.

Disease datasets provided associations, not causal evidence. CASP5 and CASP4 increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while retaining their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations in colon or rectal cancer were around 3%, similar to AXIN1 in the reported analysis. CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations and be mutually exclusive with APC mutations. In one microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer comparison, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC mutations, about 68% versus 17%. These observations suggest clinical relevance worth testing, but they do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, and they do not establish CASP5C as a validated drug target.

## Discussion

These findings define CASP5C as an epithelial regulator of Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in human intestinal models. The key distinction is isoform specificity: CASP5C amplified Wnt output, whereas CASP5A and CASP5B did not and could restrain CASP5C. This means CASP5 cannot be interpreted only through total gene expression or through analogy to CASP4. Its biological effect depends on which isoform is expressed and in which epithelial state.

The proposed mechanism is that CASP5C associates with Dishevelled-containing complexes and cleaves APC, thereby weakening beta-catenin destruction. The resulting reduction in beta-catenin ubiquitination and destruction-complex association permits greater beta-catenin-dependent transcription. This mechanism explains why CASP5C increases Wnt reporter activity, Wnt target genes and proliferative organoid phenotypes. It also explains why CASP5A and CASP5B can act as restraints: by competing for DVL2, they can reduce access of active CASP5C to the relevant complex.

The organoid data connect this mechanism to epithelial renewal behaviour. CASP5C promoted proliferation-associated markers, reduced differentiation markers, supported growth under Wnt-limited conditions and accelerated Wnt-response gene induction after irradiation. These results fit a repair-associated model, but the evidence remains model-system evidence. Organoids do not reproduce the full immune, microbial and stromal environment of human intestine, and disease-dataset associations remain correlative.

Overall, CASP5C expands the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteases. Rather than acting as the main noncanonical inflammasome caspase in the tested human systems, CASP5C links injury- and cytokine-responsive epithelial biology to a core intestinal renewal pathway. The work highlights APC cleavage and CASP5 isoform balance as important determinants of Wnt signalling in human intestinal epithelial models.
