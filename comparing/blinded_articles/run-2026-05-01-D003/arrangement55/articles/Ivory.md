# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal
epithelial models

## Abstract

Inflammatory caspases are best known as sensors and effectors of innate
immune responses, but the function of human CASP5 has remained unclear.
Here we describe a CASP5 isoform, CASP5C, that promotes Wnt signalling in
human intestinal epithelial models.
CASP5 expression was enriched in intestinal tissues and epithelial cells,
where organoids expressed three isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C.
CASP5C lacked the caspase activation and recruitment domain and was not
explained by proteolytic processing of longer isoforms.
Interaction mapping in human colonic organoids placed CASP5 isoforms near
the Wnt adaptor proteins DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3.
Functional assays showed that CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 or
CASP4, increased beta-catenin-dependent reporter activity.
CASP5C promoted beta-catenin redistribution, increased Wnt target gene
expression and supported proliferation in intestinal organoids.
Mechanistically, CASP5C required catalytic activity and cleaved the Wnt
destruction complex scaffold APC, with D556 acting as a major cleavage site.
CASP5A and CASP5B could oppose CASP5C activity by competing for DVL2.
These findings assign an epithelial Wnt-regulatory function to CASP5C and
suggest that human CASP5 isoforms can have opposing roles in intestinal
homeostasis.

## Introduction

CASP5 belongs to the inflammatory caspase family.
It is related to CASP4 and to mouse caspase 11, proteases that participate
in noncanonical inflammasome signalling.
Despite this relationship, the specific contribution of CASP5 has been less
clear.
In experiments with human intestinal epithelial cells and macrophages,
CASP4 more closely resembled mouse caspase 11 and acted as the main
mediator of responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and bacteria.
CASP5 did not account for this canonical noncanonical inflammasome function
in those settings.

This discrepancy raised the possibility that CASP5 has a distinct role in
the intestine.
CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix, with
expression in epithelial cells and in subsets of monocytes and macrophages.
In colonic organoids, CASP5 expression exceeded that of CASP4, CASP1 and
PYCARD relative to macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed broader expression.
These patterns suggested that epithelial CASP5 warranted analysis outside
the usual inflammasome framework.

The intestinal epithelium depends on Wnt and beta-catenin signalling for
development, repair, stem-cell renewal and differentiation.
DVL proteins connect Wnt receptor activation to downstream regulation of
beta-catenin.
APC is a scaffold and tumour suppressor in the beta-catenin destruction
complex.
We therefore examined whether epithelial CASP5 isoforms influence this
pathway and whether they do so through components of the destruction
complex.

## CASP5 isoforms in intestinal epithelium

Human colon and small-intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms.
CASP5A encoded an approximately 49.7 kDa protein.
CASP5B encoded an approximately 43.2 kDa protein and lacked part of the
CARD region.
CASP5C encoded an approximately 33.3 kDa protein and lacked the CARD.
Small interfering RNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a
cleavage product derived from CASP5A or CASP5B.

CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella exposure and after interferon
beta or interferon gamma treatment in organoids.
Lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelial cells, consistent
with low epithelial TLR4 expression.
These results placed CASP5 in an inducible epithelial context, but did not
support a simple model in which CASP5 copies the CASP4 response to cytosolic
lipopolysaccharide.

Single-cell and differentiation analyses showed that CASP5 isoforms were
not distributed uniformly along the epithelial lineage.
CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one
single-cell analysis, were higher in transit-amplifying cells and were
highest in enterocytes.
However, CASP5c was detected deeper in crypts and overlapped more strongly
with MKI67 and AXIN2 than CASP5a or CASP5b.
CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated
CK20-positive states.
Pseudotime analysis and CIBERSORTx supported an association of CASP5c with
early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and of CASP5a and CASP5b with
mature or differentiating states.
During differentiation after Wnt withdrawal, CASP5c peaked early with
MKI67, whereas CASP5a and CASP5b rose later with differentiation markers.

## CASP5C engages Wnt pathway components

Proximity labelling with BioID2 in human colonic organoids identified DVL1,
DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5.
Interactor analysis also recovered Wnt-related terms.
Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL
proteins.
DVL2 complexes also contained AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near
the beta-catenin regulatory machinery.
Mapping experiments indicated that the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5
catalytic domain contributed to this interaction.
The data are consistent with close association, although they do not by
themselves prove a direct binary interaction.

Functional assays separated CASP5C from the longer isoforms.
In TOPFlash reporter assays, CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity in
HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both with and without WNT3A stimulation.
CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same effect.
CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and promoted beta-catenin
accumulation in cytosolic and nuclear fractions.
By contrast, CASP5A and CASP5B could compete with CASP5C for DVL2 and
reduce the CASP5C-driven increase in Wnt signalling.

The effect was not reproduced by tested CARD-less forms of mouse caspase
11.
This comparison suggests that CASP5C may represent a primate-specialised
adaptation after caspase duplication.
That interpretation remains provisional because the tested systems cannot
define the full evolutionary history of the function.

## CASP5C promotes proliferative organoid states

The isoform-specific effect was also observed in human intestinal organoids.
CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity.
CASP5C-expressing organoids had more viable cells, were about 40% larger,
and contained more Ki-67-positive and EdU-positive cells.
They also contained fewer CK20-positive enterocytes.

Gene expression changes were consistent with a shift towards Wnt-dependent
proliferative states.
CASP5C increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by
1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold.
Markers of differentiation decreased, with ALPI reduced by about 57% and
MUC2 by about 53%.
These data indicate that CASP5C supports proliferative epithelial programmes
in organoids rather than acting as a general property of all CASP5 isoforms.

CASP5C also affected responses to Wnt depletion and restoration.
CASP5C-expressing organoids survived longer after Wnt withdrawal and
re-entered the cell cycle after Wnt was restored.
After irradiation of human colonic organoids, CASP5c transcript increased
42.4-fold within 24 h, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for
CASP5b.
Following irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC increased faster in
CASP5C-expressing organoids.
These findings are compatible with a role in epithelial recovery in the
model system, but they do not establish repair efficacy in the human body.

Loss-of-function experiments supported a competitive relationship among
isoforms.
Knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while preserving CASP5C increased Wnt
reporter activity, organoid size, ATP content, EdU incorporation and
Ki-67 staining.
When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects returned towards baseline.
The simplest interpretation is that CASP5A and CASP5B can restrain CASP5C
by competing for DVL2-containing complexes.

## CASP5C cleaves APC to weaken the destruction complex

The Wnt effect depended on CASP5C catalytic activity.
A catalytic mutant, CASP5C(C173A), lost reporter activation and failed to
produce the beta-catenin effects observed with wild-type CASP5C.
CASP5C had greater enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B.
Deleting the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased their activity and gave
them greater capacity to stimulate the Wnt reporter.
These results suggest that the CARD-containing regions restrain catalytic
or signalling activity in the longer isoforms.

Analysis of destruction complex components showed that CASP5C altered the
composition of AXIN1-containing complexes after Wnt stimulation.
CASP5C reduced the presence of DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated
beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in these complexes.
It also reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination.
Together, these observations indicate that CASP5C weakens the pathway that
normally targets beta-catenin for degradation.

APC emerged as a direct substrate candidate.
Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites were concentrated mainly in the N-terminal
region of APC.
CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and
CASP5C(C173A) did not.
Recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro.
Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556
as a primary cleavage site.
Other APC sites may also contribute, so D556 should not be treated as the
only functional site.

The functional importance of APC cleavage was tested in APC knockout
HEK293T cells.
Reintroduction of wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt the destruction
complex and increase Wnt reporter activity.
The APC(D556A) mutant weakened this response.
APC fragments resembling cleavage products reduced beta-catenin in the
destruction complex and increased Wnt reporter activity.
These findings support a model in which CASP5C cleaves APC, remodels the
destruction complex and thereby amplifies beta-catenin-dependent
transcription.

## Disease associations

CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets, while
retaining their cell-type expression patterns.
This observation indicates association with inflamed tissue states, not
causation.
The available data do not show that CASP5C causes ulcerative colitis or
inflammatory bowel disease.

Cancer datasets also raised associations with Wnt pathway alteration.
CASP5 mutations in colon or rectal cancer occurred in about 3% of cases in
the reported analysis, similar to AXIN1.
CASP5 mutations tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations but were
mutually exclusive with APC mutations in that analysis.
In microsatellite instability-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were
reported more often than APC mutations in one comparison, at about 68% and
17%, respectively.
These findings are hypothesis-generating.
They do not demonstrate that CASP5C drives cancer, nor do they validate
CASP5C as a therapeutic target.

## Discussion

This work identifies an isoform-specific epithelial function for CASP5.
Rather than acting as the human equivalent of mouse caspase 11 in the tested
noncanonical inflammasome assays, CASP5C amplified Wnt and beta-catenin
signalling in intestinal epithelial systems.
The effect required catalytic activity, involved association with DVL
proteins and was linked to cleavage of APC within the destruction complex.
CASP5A and CASP5B did not share this activity under the same conditions and
could restrain CASP5C through competition for DVL2.

The findings refine how inflammatory caspases may contribute to epithelial
biology.
CASP5C connected an inducible caspase programme to Wnt-dependent
proliferation, organoid growth and recovery after injury-like stress in
vitro.
This connection may help explain why CASP5 expression is enriched in
intestinal epithelium despite its limited role in the tested
lipopolysaccharide-driven inflammasome response.
The data also show that isoform structure matters.
Loss of the CARD in CASP5C correlated with higher catalytic activity and
with access to Wnt-regulatory effects that were not observed for CASP5A or
CASP5B.

Several limits remain.
The DVL interaction is supported by proximity and co-immunoprecipitation
data, but direct binding requires further biochemical definition.
D556 is a major APC cleavage site, but other predicted sites may contribute
to the full effect.
The organoid experiments support effects on proliferation and Wnt
responsiveness, but they do not prove tissue repair in vivo.
Finally, the ulcerative colitis and colorectal cancer observations remain
associative.

Overall, CASP5C acts as a Wnt-amplifying CASP5 isoform in human intestinal
epithelial models.
By cleaving APC and weakening beta-catenin destruction, CASP5C promotes a
proliferative epithelial state.
The opposing behaviour of CASP5A and CASP5B provides a mechanism by which
CASP5 isoform balance could tune Wnt pathway output during intestinal
homeostasis and stress responses.
