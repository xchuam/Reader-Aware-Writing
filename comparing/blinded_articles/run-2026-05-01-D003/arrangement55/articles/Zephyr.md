# Caspase 5c amplifies Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models

## Abstract

Inflammatory caspases are best known for innate immune sensing, but the function of human CASP5 has remained less clear than that of CASP4 and mouse caspase 11. Here, we define an isoform-specific activity of CASP5 in intestinal epithelial models. Human intestinal organoids expressed CASP5A, CASP5B and the CARD-less CASP5C. CASP5 expression was enriched in intestinal tissues and epithelial populations, and CASP5C was induced strongly after epithelial injury. Proteomic and biochemical analyses linked CASP5 isoforms to Dishevelled proteins. CASP5C, but not CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 or CASP4, increased beta-catenin-dependent reporter activity, promoted Wnt and proliferation-associated genes, expanded organoid growth, and reduced differentiation markers. CASP5C required its catalytic cysteine and cleaved APC, with D556 contributing to a major cleavage event. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and restrained its signalling effect. These findings support a model in which CASP5C amplifies Wnt signalling in intestinal epithelial systems by modifying the beta-catenin destruction complex.

## Introduction

Human CASP5 belongs to the inflammatory caspase family, which also includes CASP4 and the mouse orthologue-like caspase 11. CASP4 and caspase 11 are established effectors of noncanonical inflammasome responses to cytosolic lipopolysaccharide and intracellular bacteria. CASP5 is related to these proteins, but its biological role has been less settled. In the systems examined here, human CASP4 more closely resembled mouse caspase 11 in sequence and phylogenetic analyses and acted as the principal mediator of tested noncanonical inflammasome responses, whereas CASP5 did not perform that role in human intestinal epithelial cells or macrophages. This separation raised a basic question: if CASP5 is not simply a redundant noncanonical inflammasome caspase, what does it do?

The intestine provided a plausible context for this question because CASP5 expression was concentrated in the intestinal tract and appendix and was detected in epithelial cells as well as some monocyte and macrophage populations. In human colonic organoids, CASP5 was expressed at higher levels than CASP4, CASP1 and PYCARD when compared with macrophages, whereas CASP4 showed a broader distribution. CASP5 expression increased after Salmonella infection and after interferon-beta or interferon-gamma, while lipopolysaccharide alone had little effect in epithelial organoids, consistent with low epithelial TLR4 expression. These observations suggested that intestinal epithelial CASP5 might have a function distinct from canonical inflammatory caspase responses.

Wnt/beta-catenin signalling regulates intestinal development, epithelial repair, stem-cell renewal and differentiation. Dishevelled proteins connect Wnt receptor activation to downstream beta-catenin control, whereas APC acts as a tumour-suppressive scaffold within the destruction complex. Because epithelial homeostasis depends on the balance between Wnt-driven proliferation and differentiation, we asked whether CASP5 isoforms interact with Wnt pathway components and whether any isoform changes beta-catenin signalling in intestinal epithelial models.

## Experimental approach

The study combined human tissue and cell-expression analyses, intestinal organoid models, proteomic interaction mapping, biochemical assays and Wnt reporter systems. Human colon and small-intestinal organoids defined CASP5 isoform expression and epithelial responses. BioID2 proximity labelling identified proteins near CASP5, and co-immunoprecipitation tested association with candidate pathway components. Wnt activity was measured with beta-catenin-dependent reporter assays in HEK293T, HCT116 and organoid systems. Organoid viability, size, proliferation and differentiation were assessed using ATP, Ki-67, EdU and lineage-marker measurements. Mechanistic tests used catalytic mutants, CARD deletions, APC knockout cells, APC cleavage-site mutations, APC fragments and in vitro cleavage of recombinant APC substrates.

## Results

Human intestinal organoids expressed three CASP5 isoforms: CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C, with approximate predicted sizes of 49.7 kDa, 43.2 kDa and 33.3 kDa. CASP5B lacked part of the CARD region, and CASP5C lacked the CARD entirely. Small interfering RNA experiments indicated that CASP5C was not simply a cleavage product of CASP5A or CASP5B, supporting its treatment as a distinct isoform. This isoform structure was important because the absence of the CARD domain correlated with a functional behaviour not shared by the longer isoforms.

Proximity labelling provided the first link between CASP5 and Wnt signalling. BioID2 in human colonic organoids identified DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3 near CASP5, and Wnt-related terms were enriched in the interactor analysis. Co-immunoprecipitation confirmed that CASP5 isoforms associated with DVL1, DVL2 and DVL3. DVL2 complexes also included AXIN1 and beta-catenin, placing CASP5 near the beta-catenin destruction machinery. Domain mapping implicated the DVL DEP domain and the CASP5 catalytic domain, although the evidence does not by itself prove direct binding between purified proteins.

Functional assays showed that Wnt amplification was specific to CASP5C. In TOPFlash reporter assays, CASP5C increased Wnt/beta-catenin reporter activity in HEK293T and HCT116 cells, both with and without WNT3A stimulation. CASP5A, CASP5B, CASP1 and CASP4 did not produce the same response. CASP5C also increased AXIN2 and TCF7 expression and shifted beta-catenin into the nuclear and cytosolic fractions. CARD-less forms of mouse caspase 11 did not reproduce this Wnt effect, suggesting that CASP5C may represent a primate-specialized activity after gene duplication, although that evolutionary interpretation remains bounded by the tested comparisons.

The isoform specificity was also evident in human intestinal organoids. CASP5C, but not CASP5A or CASP5B, increased Wnt reporter activity. Organoids expressing CASP5C contained more viable cells, were approximately 40% larger, and showed increased Ki-67 and EdU positivity. CASP5C also reduced CK20-positive enterocytes. At the transcript level, CASP5C increased AXIN2 by about threefold, TCF7 by 1.4-fold, MYC by 1.3-fold, LGR5 by 1.2-fold and MKI67 by 2.4-fold, whereas ALPI and MUC2 decreased by about 57% and 53%. Together, these results indicate that CASP5C favours a Wnt-high, proliferative organoid state rather than a differentiated epithelial state.

CASP5C also affected organoid behaviour during Wnt withdrawal and restoration. CASP5C-expressing organoids maintained growth for longer without Wnt and re-entered the cell cycle more readily when Wnt was restored. In irradiated human colonic organoids, CASP5c increased 42.4-fold within 24 hours, compared with 5.6-fold for CASP5a and 6.6-fold for CASP5b. After irradiation, LGR5, OLFM4, TCF7 and MYC rose faster in CASP5C-expressing organoids. These data are consistent with a role for CASP5C in epithelial regenerative programs in organoid systems, but they do not establish that CASP5C is required for repair in vivo.

Single-cell and differentiation analyses placed the isoforms along epithelial maturation trajectories. CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one single-cell RNA-sequencing analysis, higher in transit-amplifying cells and highest in enterocytes. Within that pattern, CASP5c was detected deeper in crypt regions and overlapped more with MKI67 and AXIN2 than did CASP5a or CASP5b. CASP5A and CASP5B were more luminal and associated with differentiated or CK20-positive states. Pseudotime, CIBERSORTx and Wnt-withdrawal differentiation analyses similarly linked CASP5c to early-lineage or transit-amplifying states and CASP5a/b to mature or differentiating states.

The longer CASP5 isoforms did not merely lack activity; they could restrain CASP5C. CASP5A and CASP5B competed with CASP5C for DVL2 and reduced CASP5C-dependent beta-catenin signalling. Consistent with this model, knockdown of CASP5a and CASP5b while retaining CASP5C increased Wnt reporter activity, organoid size, ATP levels, EdU incorporation and Ki-67 positivity. When CASP5c was also knocked down, these effects decreased. Thus, isoform balance, rather than total CASP5 expression alone, appears to shape epithelial Wnt output.

Mechanistic experiments connected CASP5C activity to the beta-catenin destruction complex. After Wnt stimulation, CASP5C reduced DVL2, beta-catenin, phosphorylated beta-catenin, APC and beta-TrCP in AXIN1-containing complexes and reduced beta-catenin ubiquitination. A catalytic mutant, CASP5C(C173A), lost reporter activation and beta-catenin effects. CASP5C also had higher enzymatic activity than CASP5A or CASP5B, and removing the CARD from CASP5A or CASP5B increased enzymatic activity and Wnt reporter signalling. These findings indicate that CASP5C requires catalytic activity and that CARD-containing N-terminal regions may limit this activity.

APC emerged as a key substrate linking CASP5C catalysis to Wnt amplification. Predicted CASP5 cleavage sites in APC were concentrated mainly in the N-terminal region. CASP5C generated APC cleavage products, whereas CASP5A, CASP5B and CASP5C(C173A) did not, and recombinant CASP5C directly cleaved truncated APC in vitro. Mutation of APC D556 removed the major cleavage products, identifying D556 as a primary site, although other APC sites may also contribute. In APC-knockout HEK293T cells, wild-type APC allowed CASP5C to disrupt destruction-complex assembly and increase Wnt reporter activity, whereas APC(D556A) weakened these effects. APC fragments resembling cleavage products also reduced beta-catenin in the destruction complex and increased reporter activity. These results support a model in which CASP5C cleaves APC and attenuates destruction-complex control of beta-catenin.

## Discussion

This study identifies an epithelial, isoform-specific function for human CASP5 that is distinct from the tested noncanonical inflammasome role of CASP4. CASP5C amplified Wnt/beta-catenin signalling in human cell and organoid systems, increased proliferative organoid features and reduced differentiation markers. The activity required the catalytic cysteine and was linked to APC cleavage and altered AXIN1-containing destruction-complex assemblies. CASP5A and CASP5B, by contrast, did not amplify Wnt and could restrain CASP5C by competing for DVL2. The central conclusion is therefore not that CASP5 generally promotes Wnt signalling, but that CASP5C can do so in a manner shaped by isoform balance.

The data also clarify why intestinal context matters for CASP5 biology. CASP5 expression was enriched in intestinal epithelial settings, induced by infection-associated and interferon signals, and strongly increased after irradiation in organoids. CASP5c aligned with crypt-like, MKI67-positive and AXIN2-positive compartments more than the longer isoforms, whereas CASP5a/b aligned more with differentiating states. Because total CASP5 transcripts were nearly absent from LGR5-positive stem cells in one analysis, CASP5C may act most prominently in transit-amplifying or early-lineage epithelial states rather than in the most canonical LGR5-positive stem-cell compartment.

The proposed mechanism places CASP5C upstream of beta-catenin stabilization through APC cleavage. APC has a central scaffolding role in the destruction complex, and CASP5C-dependent APC cleavage provides a plausible route by which beta-catenin ubiquitination and destruction-complex association were reduced. The APC D556A experiments identify D556 as important for the major observed cleavage products, but they do not exclude additional APC cleavage sites or parallel contributions from other complex components. The DVL interaction may recruit CASP5 isoforms near Wnt regulatory machinery, although its directness and structural details remain unresolved.

The disease-related observations are best interpreted as hypotheses-generating associations. CASP5 and CASP4 were increased in ulcerative colitis datasets while preserving their cell-type patterns. CASP5 mutations appeared in approximately 3% of colon or rectal cancers, tended to co-occur with Wnt-pathway mutations, and were mutually exclusive with APC mutations in the reported analysis. In MSI-high colorectal cancer, CASP5 mutations were reported more often than APC in one comparison. These findings are consistent with a relationship between CASP5 alterations and Wnt-pathway states, but they do not show that CASP5C causes inflammatory bowel disease or cancer, nor do they establish CASP5C as a validated therapeutic target.

Overall, the findings expand the functional range of inflammatory caspase-family proteins by assigning CASP5C a noncanonical role in epithelial Wnt regulation. In human intestinal organoids and engineered cell systems, CASP5C cleaves APC, weakens destruction-complex control of beta-catenin and promotes a proliferative Wnt-responsive state. The work leaves important next questions about in vivo isoform regulation, epithelial repair and colorectal disease. The strongest supported takeaway is that CASP5C can amplify Wnt signalling through APC cleavage in intestinal epithelial models.
